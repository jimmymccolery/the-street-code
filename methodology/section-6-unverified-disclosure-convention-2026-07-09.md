## Convention body

Every framework-scope document that ships with citations, empirical claims, or literature-grounded architectural claims MUST include a **Section 6 UNVERIFIED disclosure block** enumerating claims investigated but not confirmed to primary-source full-text verification standard. Convention follows Fable 5's exemplary format at `~/Downloads/Next up/story_sifting_dossier.md` §6.

### Required elements per Section 6 UNVERIFIED disclosure

For each flagged claim:
1. **Exact claim statement** (verbatim as attempted)
2. **Attempted verification method** (fetch URL / primary-source paper / secondary source)
3. **Verification outcome** (URL failed / paper paywalled / abstract-only retrieval / text-truncated / not-found)
4. **Downstream constraint** (which framework artifacts must NOT load-bear on this claim; what alternate verification would upgrade it to VERIFIED)

### Framework operational discipline

- Any framework author publishing a document with load-bearing citations must audit at authoring time
- Verified citations → cite normally with URL/DOI
- Unverified citations must NOT be load-bearing in body claims — either flag Section 6 OR remove
- Section 6 disclosure travels with the document; downstream consumers can grep for `## Section 6 UNVERIFIED` to identify caveated claims
- Rule 19 discipline enforcement: substrates auditing framework documents check Section 6 completeness as anti-fabrication mechanism
