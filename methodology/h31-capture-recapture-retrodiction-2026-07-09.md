## 3. Structural obstacle — Chapman estimator applicability at framework-scope

Chapman capture-recapture estimator requires **two independent captures at same population state**. Framework's audit methodology violates this in TWO ways:

**Obstacle 1: Rounds 1-4 have DISJOINT scopes by Rule 11 stratification design.**

| Round | Scope | Overlap with prior rounds |
|---|---|---|
| Round 1 | citation-verification | (baseline) |
| Round 2 | implementation | 0 (different corpus surface) |
| Round 3 | statistical-methodology | 0 (different corpus surface) |
| Round 4 | methodology-self-consistency | 0 (different corpus surface) |

Scope-disjointness ⇒ overlap m=0 across all Round pairs BY CONSTRUCTION not by empirical coverage. Chapman with m=0 gives N̂ = (n₁+1)(n₂+1) - 1 which measures scope-disjoint-population size, NOT defect-population residual within one scope.

**Obstacle 2: Framework REMEDIATES defects between rounds.**

After Round 1's 4 CRITICAL fabrications were folded (citation manifest v1.0 → v2.0), Round 5 Perplexity re-audit found DIFFERENT defects — the population changed. Chapman capture-recapture requires POPULATION STATIONARITY between captures, which framework's remediation-before-next-round discipline explicitly breaks.

**Combined:** Rounds 1-4 → Round 5 retrodiction as Fable's D.5.1 test literally proposes fails Chapman's stationarity assumption on both fronts.
