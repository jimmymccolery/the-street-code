## B.1 (§6.1) Reagan-arc parametric functional form — piecewise-linear vs empirical-mean shapes

**Verdict: PARTIAL — piecewise-linear is adequate for A1's classifier scope and was the right call there; it is NOT what my Stage 7 spec assumes for Family II matchers, and the question as posed is a false dichotomy.**

Grounds. The six shapes are empirically derived SVD modes, not parametric objects — the published paper presents them as mode shapes in its Figure 3 and publishes no parametric or piecewise-linear forms ([Reagan et al. 2016](https://epjdatascience.springeropen.com/articles/10.1140/epjds/s13688-016-0093-1)). So BOTH options in §6.1 (`CRG:375-379`) are approximations of something that has no canonical functional form. The framework's rationale — "DTW time-warping invariance absorbs template shape choice at classification scope" (`CRG:377`) — is correct exactly as scoped: at classifier scope the 66 DTW distances are only feature inputs, and monotone-ish template error washes out in feature selection. It does NOT extend to Stage 7 matcher scope, where DTW distance is compared against a MATCH THRESHOLD per pattern (`S7:65-76` Family II definitions): piecewise-linear corner discontinuities inflate DTW path cost precisely at turning points — the most diagnostic region of man_in_a_hole, icarus, cinderella, and oedipus (the 1- and 2-turning-point shapes, `CRG:377`) — producing systematic false negatives near threshold. Threshold-referenced matching is shape-fidelity-sensitive in a way rank-order feature extraction is not.

**Answer to the question as asked:** my Stage 7 spec assumes the six-shape VOCABULARY (`S7:63`), not a specific functional form; it is silent on parametric form, and that silence is a spec gap this question legitimately exposes.

**Concrete next step.** Keep piecewise-linear at A1 (no rework; H24's result stands as filed). For Stage 7 Family II, specify smooth low-order templates — cosine/Chebyshev basis with 2–4 coefficients per shape, which reproduces the SVD-mode character (Reagan's modes are themselves near-sinusoidal low-order components) without pixel digitization brittleness (`CRG:377`'s legitimate concern). Add one cheap synthetic test to the Stage 7 harness, filable now as an Item 133 amendment: **template-recovery test** — generate trajectories from each of the 6 templates + noise at the H24-observed channel SNR, require the matcher to recover the generating template at ≥95% accuracy and to show ≤5pp accuracy gap between smooth and piecewise-linear variants before either is frozen. If piecewise-linear passes recovery within 5pp of smooth, the framework's simpler choice wins on the evidence and my objection dissolves; if not, the test has paid for itself. Estimated cost: one file, ~150 lines, reuses `dtw.ts`.

## D.5 Residual-defect estimation: make the miss-rate table predictive instead of archival

The 5-round miss-rate table (`CRG:60-66`, ~23–44%) is used rhetorically ("author self-review is insufficient") but never quantitatively. Two upgrades:

1. **Capture-recapture residual estimation.** Audit rounds are independent-ish samplings of the same defect population; overlap statistics estimate what remains. For any artifact audited by ≥2 substrates: defects found by round A, by round B, by both → Chapman estimator `N̂ = ((nA+1)(nB+1)/(m+1)) − 1`, residual ≈ N̂ − found. This technique has decades of software-inspection precedent (UNVERIFIED-at-audit-time for a specific canonical citation — flag per Rule 19; the method's existence in the inspection literature is common knowledge but this audit did not fetch a primary source).
