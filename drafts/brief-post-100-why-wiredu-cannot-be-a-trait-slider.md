## Thesis

Wiredu's sympathetic impartiality (Ch 9 *Person and Community* 1992 p. 199) is a procedural moral test: imaginative role-reversal as a constraint on action. The test asks, "would you accept this outcome if your position were reversed?" HEXACO honesty-humility (Ashton & Lee 2009) is an individual-difference trait dimension measuring exploitation-vs-cooperation tendencies. Mapping a procedural test onto a trait coordinate conflates levels of analysis. A high-H NPC is not "more sympathetically impartial" in Wiredu's sense; it is statistically less exploitative. These are different objects. Post 100 walks through this category error in depth + extends the lesson beyond Wiredu specifically: procedural ethical tests across multiple traditions (Kantian universalizability; Rawlsian original position; Confucian role ethics; Ubuntu communal accountability) are NOT trait coordinates. Treating them as trait coordinates is a category error that the cross-cultural philosophy literature has named (Bilge 2013 ornamental deployment) + a class of mistake game designers should avoid.

## Sections (outline)

1. **Opening humility paragraph (5-element discipline).** Standard humility framing.

2. **Hook:** "I tried to use Wiredu's sympathetic impartiality as the philosophical grounding for a trait dimension. The mapping doesn't work, and the reason is structural, not implementation-detail."

3. **What Wiredu's sympathetic impartiality is.** Primary-text walkthrough of Wiredu Ch 9 p. 199:
   - "Sympathetic impartiality is the first principle of all morals" (per F-17 CORROBORATED via R8 R4 — universalist framing)
   - The principle is a PROCEDURAL TEST: imagine the outcome from each affected party's position; if you would accept the outcome regardless of which position you occupied, the action is ethically permissible
   - Compare to Kantian universalizability ("Act only according to that maxim by which you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law"); structurally similar PROCEDURAL TEST
   - Compare to Rawlsian original position ("veil of ignorance: choose principles as if you don't know your position"); structurally similar
   - Wiredu's contribution: ground the procedural test in Akan philosophical tradition + relational personhood + "Onipa nye abe na ne ho ahyia ne ho" (a human being is not a palm tree)

4. **What HEXACO honesty-humility is.** Brief HEXACO background:
   - HEXACO is a 6-dimensional personality model (Lee & Ashton 2004)
   - H = Honesty-Humility: tendency to be sincere/fair/non-greedy/modest vs deceitful/exploitative/greedy/pretentious
   - INDIVIDUAL-DIFFERENCE TRAIT: measured by self-report or peer-report on 18-60-item inventories
   - Predicts cooperative behavior in economic games (Hilbig & Zettler 2009 *JRP* 43(3):516-519; Thielmann & Hilbig 2015 *PSPB* 41(11):1523-36)

5. **The category error walkthrough.** Why mapping procedural moral test → trait dimension is structurally wrong:
   - **Different objects:** A procedural test is something you DO (a deliberative act). A trait is something you HAVE (a dispositional state). Mapping doing onto having confuses levels.
   - **Different evaluation logic:** A procedural test passes-or-fails per-case (this action satisfies the test or doesn't). A trait dimension is gradient (high or low H). You can't grade a per-case test on a gradient.
   - **Different causal direction:** A trait might predispose someone to apply a procedural test more often, but the trait IS NOT the test. Having high H doesn't mean you sympathetically-impartially evaluated something; it means you tend to be cooperative.
   - **Procedural test's universal claim doesn't transfer:** Wiredu argues sympathetic impartiality is universal (F-17 CORROBORATED) BUT universal at the procedural-test level, not at the trait level. Different cultures + individuals may apply the test differently while the test's structure remains. HEXACO H scores transfer across cultures with empirical caveats; the trait isn't the test.

6. **The ornamental-deployment risk (Bilge 2013).** Why doing this anyway would be worse than not doing it:
   - Bilge 2013 *Du Bois Review* 10(2):405-424 names the risk class: borrowing the prestige of a source tradition while implementing none of its substance
   - A V1 with HEXACO-H generosity mechanics + Wiredu philosophical citations is ornamental: the philosophical citation adds reputation; the mechanic implements no communal accountability; the player experiences no actual Wireduan personhood-as-striving — just statistical generosity tuning
   - "Worse than no mapping" because: the philosophical citation makes a claim the mechanic can't deliver; the gap is the harm

7. **The lesson generalized.** This category error pattern shows up beyond Wiredu:
   - Treating Kantian universalizability as a "deontology trait slider" — same category error
   - Treating Rawlsian original position as a "fairness trait slider" — same
   - Treating Confucian role ethics as a "filial-piety trait dimension" — same
   - Treating Ubuntu communal-accountability as a "communalism trait slider" — same
   - In each case, the PROCEDURAL TEST is a deliberative practice; the TRAIT is a tendency. Confusing them is the structural error.

8. **What does work for cross-cultural philosophical grounding.**
   - Treat the procedural test as INSPIRATION at the framework + research-paper layer
   - Treat the implementation as its own design problem with its own trait sources (HEXACO H for cooperation/exploitation is empirically defensible per its own validation literature; not because Wiredu)
   - Be explicit about the inspiration-vs-implementation distinction (Post 99's 5-element discipline)
   - Cite primary sources directly + carry humility brackets

9. **What this means for Modified-C-bis V1.**
   - Generosity mechanics use HEXACO H + pair-relational-history + sentiment override; grounded on HEXACO + attachment + Gottman empirical literature
   - Wiredu's framework retained as philosophical inspiration in Post 84 + framework paper; NOT operationalized as trait/mechanic
   - This is the DROP verdict from R7 R6 Section 3 option (c) — saves 25-70 hours V1 BUILD code work + avoids ornamental deployment risk

10. **Closing humility paragraph (5-element discipline).** Restate: this post does not claim representation of Akan philosophy; does not implement Wiredu's framework; documents the structural reasoning for why mapping procedural moral tests onto trait dimensions is a category error generalizable across multiple cross-cultural traditions; retraction condition applies if a knowledgeable Akan-philosophy scholar demonstrates the framing materially misrepresents Wiredu (within 14-day response timeline).
