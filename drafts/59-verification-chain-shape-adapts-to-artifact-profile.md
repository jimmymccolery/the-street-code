## The seed: what to write about

I ran four Tier 1 high-stakes re-verification cycles back-to-back on a Sunday morning. Same meta-discipline — Council-grade re-verification of architectural decisions ratified during a week-long AI-knowledge drift window. But the actual verification CHAIN SHAPE was different in each cycle, because the available artifacts were different in each cycle.

- **Phase 7.0 re-verification**: 5 substrate archives + Stage 3 verdict + Stage 4 ratification all archived. Full 2-layer verification chain (CW current-state per-citation alignment + CC substrate-archive re-read via pandoc-extracted docx).
- **UI vision close re-verification**: No archived Stage 3 verdict, no Stage 4 ratification (both inline-chat), 3 substrate archives. 3-layer verification chain (CW current-state + CC commit-body-as-record + CC git-blame on canonical text).
- **PL-10 re-verification**: No archived Stage 3/4, no substrate archives anywhere (Desktop staging folder gone), but architecture IS shipped operational code with 930 tests. Adapted task-divided 2-layer chain (CW covers 2/5 structurally-accessible verifications, CC covers 3/5 CW-inaccessible verifications — shipped code, commit-body, test suite).
- **Q17 re-verification**: 17 archives at _archive/ (richest available-artifact profile of all 4 cycles). Full 2-layer chain sibling to Phase 7.0 (CW current-state + CC substrate-archive cross-check + git-blame textual-immutability verification + composition-layer integration verification).

Same Tier 1 discipline. Four different shapes. The shape adaptation is itself the methodology refinement.

## The arc to narrate

1. **The meta-discipline**: Tier 1 Council-grade re-verification of HIGH-STAKES architectural decisions. Discipline name: `feedback_cw_project_knowledge_refresh_discipline.md`. Came out of an operator-side catch of stale AI project knowledge.
2. **The structural-constraint variable**: What ORIGINAL-SIDE artifacts exist? Did the cycle archive substrate responses? Did it archive Stage 3 verdict? Stage 4 ratification? Were the artifacts saved to _archive/ or only to a Desktop staging folder that got cleaned up later? Is there shipped code that records the decision operationally?
3. **The Phase 7.0 shape (richest archives)**: Full Stage 3 + Stage 4 + 5 substrate archives. CW does per-citation alignment against current state. CC does substrate-archive re-read for convergence-count verification. Two-layer chain. Surfaces 1 ADJUSTS-fold-in + 1 attribution refinement; 7 axes + 4 decisions STAND.
4. **The UI vision close shape (sparser archives)**: 3 substrate archives but inline-chat Stage 3/4. CW does per-citation alignment. CC does commit-body-as-record (the bundled atomic-mutation handoff commit BECOMES the verdict-of-record) + git-blame for added-vs-confirmed disambiguation. Three-layer chain. 5 Q outcomes + methodology observation STAND.
5. **The PL-10 shape (no archives, shipped code)**: Substrate archives don't exist anywhere. CW value structurally minimal (only 2/5 verifications accessible). CC takes the shipped-code + commit-body + test suite verifications. Operator chooses Path B anyway (quality-over-speed preference) — CW surfaces 2 material drift findings + 1 attribution looseness DESPITE structural constraint. Empirical validation that "structurally thin" CW role still produces value at canonical-text alignment scope.
6. **The Q17 shape (richest archives)**: Full 4 substrate responses + multiple orchestrator verdicts + audit prompts + research triangulation responses + CC first-pass synthesis. CW does per-axis canonical-state alignment. CC does substrate-archive cross-check + git-blame textual-immutability (confirms §21.1-§21.8 LITERALLY unchanged since ratification commit) + composition-layer integration verification at §33. Full 2-layer chain.
7. **The pattern**: The verification chain shape adapts upstream per structural-constraint analysis at Stage 0 framing. Tier 1 "5-stage Council-grade" is the META-discipline, not a fixed shape. Shape ADAPTS to available-artifact profile per deployment-class scope.

## Why it's worth writing

This is the methodology refinement that came out of the morning's empirical work. Solo-dev-grade AI orchestration discipline isn't a single fixed procedure — it's a META-procedure that adapts to artifact availability. The discipline is "Council-grade re-verification at HIGH-STAKES re-verification scope." The SHAPE depends on what artifacts exist to verify against. The shape adaptation is operator-side judgment at Stage 0 framing, not AI-side improvisation mid-cycle.

For solo devs running AI orchestration disciplines: don't expect every cycle to look the same. Expect the SHAPE to adapt to your archive hygiene. If you didn't archive substrate responses, your verification chain has one less layer available. If you archived everything, you get the richer chain. The discipline holds across all shapes.
