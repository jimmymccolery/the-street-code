## The lessons worth surfacing

**Cross-substrate triangulation has a 3-layer recovery-mechanism stack.** CC verification-gate-at-prompt-handoff time (pre-deployment) + orchestrator-class meta-audit framing critique (post-substrate-response) + orchestrator-class execution-time canonical-cite-discipline catch via project-knowledge query (during execution). Layers 2 + 3 are orchestrator-class structural advantages substrate-class deployment can't reproduce.

**Substrate-class doesn't catch errors the prompt seeds.** Grok's drafted prompt said "5 soul-axis HUD elements" (off by one — Customer Satisfaction is the OUTPUT not an INPUT axis). Gemini propagated. Perplexity propagated. Gemini Pro Extended propagated. Cross-substrate triangulation gave the illusion of independent verification while all 4 substrates were executing the same seeded error. Only the orchestrator-class CW caught it, by querying project-knowledge directly during execution.

**The most valuable artifact wasn't the research.** It was the orchestrator-built CVD tester HTML — operator-runnable, self-contained, Machado matrices in linearRGB color-interpolation domain, kill-secondary-encoding toggle. Open it in a browser, switch to grayscale, tick the toggle — you see the failure mode in one gesture. That's what a 6-stage research arc compresses into.

**The post-arc honest answer changed the architecture.** When the dev assistant executed the handoff's read-first sweep, it confirmed: zero soul-axis hex values exist anywhere in the source. The orchestrator's placeholder palette IS the V1 ratification candidate. And — separately — the CRT shader at the existing diegetic scoreboard amber-tints every pixel color-only, which means if Phase 7.2 dashboard goes through the CRT pipeline, the CVD-safety math collapses. New architectural question surfaced after the research arc already "completed."

**Effective frame: this is not Council-of-Three. It's the same pattern, scaled up.** Council-of-Three (research-class scope) reproduces at Council-with-N (architectural-deliberation scope) reproduces at sextupartite (solo-dev-stakes scope). Each scope has its own gate criteria, but the structure — multiple substrate-class deployments + at-least-one orchestrator-class catch — keeps showing up because the alternative (single-substrate or even dual-substrate research with no orchestrator overlay) gives the operator a comfortable false consensus.

## Why this matters

Single-substrate research is faster. Dual-substrate cross-triangulation catches some errors. Triple-substrate triangulation catches more. But until you add an orchestrator-class deployment that can (a) verify against project-knowledge, (b) execute code for the math claims, (c) catch the injection-wrapper, and (d) produce the operator-runnable artifact — you don't have the full picture. You have research that converges in interesting ways and you don't know which of the converging things are right.

The 6-stage arc isn't the norm. It's the upper bound of what this discipline can do when stakes warrant it. Most decisions need 2 stages. Some need 3. A few need 5-6. Knowing which one your decision needs is the discipline.
