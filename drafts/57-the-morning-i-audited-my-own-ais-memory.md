## The seed: what to write about

I screenshotted the file inventory of my Pin Paradise Claude Web Project at 6:30am on a Sunday and discovered my orchestrator-class AI had been operating on canonical-state ground truth that was approximately a week stale. The file versions were from around 2026-05-22 → 2026-05-24. Current canonical was from 2026-05-30 closing-arc. 19+ orchestrator-class deployments had been performed against stale project knowledge as ground truth since approximately 2026-05-25 — including high-stakes architectural-lock ratifications, Council deployments, methodology canonization decisions, and a full BUILD that shipped against a §34 lock authored from stale-knowledge-influenced verdict.

The deployments had *looked authoritative*. They cited specific canonical anchors. They produced PROCEED-WITH-CONDITIONS verdicts and per-axis dispositions. They had absorbed my framework's discipline language. None of them had self-detected that the canonical state they were citing as authoritative was a week behind reality.

The recovery mechanism wasn't AI self-catch. The recovery mechanism was an operator-side file inventory audit — me clicking through the project knowledge file list and noticing version numbers I didn't recognize as current.
