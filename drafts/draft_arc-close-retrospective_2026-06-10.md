## The four rounds at a structural level

## The cross-arc swap that only the orchestrator could catch

A specific structural finding from the second cycle (Round 4 against Round 1 through Round 3) was that the two cycles shared dates and substrate-types but swapped substrate-to-round mappings. The first cycle's R1 was Round 4's R3; the first cycle's R3 was Round 4's R1; only Opus equals R4 and ChatGPT equals R5 held common across the two cycles. The swap was not designed; the swap emerged because the second cycle's deployment chose substrate-to-round mappings independently from the first cycle's mappings without explicit cross-cycle consistency.

The structural drift this produced was that closure-memo references between the two cycles were ambiguous about which substrate's contribution was being referenced. A closure-memo claim that "R3 caught the X-finding" was true in both cycles but referred to different substrates' work. The closure-memo's claims were structurally vulnerable to misreading by anyone (including future readers and future cycles' substrates) who assumed substrate-to-round consistency across cycles.

This is the kind of structural drift no single substrate can catch. The substrates running within a single cycle do not have visibility into the other cycle's substrate-to-round mapping; the substrates within one cycle would have to read the closure memo across cycles to identify the swap, and the read would have to specifically check substrate identity against round identity across cycles, which is not the substrate-side default for closure-memo reads.

The orchestrator-class adjudication caught the swap because the orchestrator's job at the closing-round was to hold the two cycles' artifacts against each other. The orchestrator-class read of both cycles' closure memos identified the substrate-to-round inconsistency; the catch was flagged as F-3 in the running enumeration; the correction was a cross-arc substrate-naming convention added as a header section in both closure memos and in the shared framing-context file (the convention now requires naming the substrate explicitly, not just the round number, in cross-cycle references).

The catch is structurally typical of orchestrator-class work in the arc. The orchestrator-class substrate's contribution is not extending the content; the contribution is holding artifacts against each other and identifying inconsistencies that no single substrate can see because each substrate's view is bounded to its own cycle.

## The twelve-hour compression as methodology demonstration

The same-day cycle close on Round 1 through Round 4 was the methodology compression that the arc most directly demonstrates. The original deployment estimate had been two to three weeks for the work the cycles ultimately compressed into a substantially shorter window. The compression was not magic; the compression was the specific methodology disciplines in operation.

Three disciplines were load-bearing. First, substrate-style differentiation per the multi-substrate research workflow rule (canonized as Rule 11 in the framework's enumeration on 2026-05-26). Each substrate did the work its style does best: generative breadth at the brainstorm-and-survey phase; research rigor at the citation-verification phase; quantitative anchoring at the empirical-claim phase; primary-text-agentic-browsing at the source-verification phase; substantive extension at the synthesis-precursor phase; orchestrator-class adjudication at the closing-round phase. The substrate-style differentiation produced complementary contributions rather than redundant overlap, which is what makes parallel deployment of multiple substrates produce aggregate output greater than any single substrate's output.

Second, parallel deployment where dependencies permit. The substrates ran in parallel across the rounds whose work was independent (the citation-verification substrate could run on the brainstorm substrate's output while the quantitative-anchoring substrate ran on a different brainstorm-substrate output; the substantive-extension substrate could prepare its synthesis-precursor work while the citation-verification was still in flight). The parallel deployment compressed wall-clock time without compressing substrate-attention time; each substrate still spent its work-time on its work, but the work-times overlapped rather than serialized.

Third, orchestrator-class synthesis at the closing-round phase. The Madisonian Gate substrate's job was not to average the prior rounds' verdicts; the job was to adjudicate the verdicts against each other and against primary-text evidence. The orchestrator-class read of the prior rounds' artifacts produced the integrity-test default response shape (in the framing of Post 5 on seven-substrate triangulation, the closing-round had explicit anti-confirmation-bias framing that the orchestrator's prompt design enforced). The integrity-test default response shape is what produced the catches that majority-vote synthesis would have missed.

The twelve-hour compression is methodology-research-grade documentation of these three disciplines operating together. The compression is not the load-bearing claim; the load-bearing claim is that the three disciplines, when explicitly designed into the arc, produce the kind of cycle close that an estimate of "two to three weeks" would otherwise size. The disciplines are the substantive contribution; the compression is the evidence the disciplines work.

## The honest catches

The arc's deepest tension was the framing decision between Strawson's Episodic-versus-Diachronic axis (from the philosophy-of-mind literature on personal identity) and Wiredu's writing on personhood-as-achievement (from the philosophy-of-mind literature on personhood that the framework draws on for cross-cultural balance). The decision was not resolved by majority vote across substrate verdicts; the decision was resolved by the two-level axis split (Modification 6 in the framework's enumeration) that explicitly held the two axes as orthogonal rather than competing.

The two-level axis split was reasoned through against primary-text evidence rather than against substrate-consensus. The substrate-consensus across Round 1 through Round 3 had been confident about an integration that the primary-text evidence eventually undermined; the substrates were collectively wrong about a specific framing decision that the orchestrator-class re-read of primary text corrected. The corrected framing was the load-bearing methodology output of the cycle, and the substrate-consensus had been the wrong input to the closing-round's synthesis.

The PersonaWeaver framing correction was a similar shape. The substrate-consensus across Round 1 through Round 3 had described PersonaWeaver as a fairness safeguard or bias-mitigation safety mechanism; the substrate-consensus was wrong because the PersonaWeaver paper itself does not contain the words "fairness" or "marginalized" anywhere in its text. The orchestrator-class read of the paper's primary text produced the correction; the correction propagated through the framework's citation discipline as a specific instruction that PersonaWeaver must not be cited as a fairness-mitigation intervention. The substrate-consensus would have produced a citation error that the framework's later readers would have absorbed.

The arc's Round 8 correction of the Wiredu universalist-finding flag is the most consequential catch in the arc. Round 7 had ratified F-13 (a finding-flag against the universalist claim) because one round's substrate (R4-2 in the Round 7 framing) had asserted the universalist finding without primary-text grounding, and another round's substrate (R4-1 in the Round 7 framing) had not read the chapter that contained the relevant passage. Round 8's R4 substrate ran an independent primary-text re-read of the chapter (specifically Wiredu's Chapter 9, around pages 193 and 199) and produced quotations that supported the universalist finding directly. The orchestrator-class adjudication at Round 8 sided with R4's primary-text read against R2's prior-round verdict and against the prior-arc R4-1 substrate's chapter-non-read. The reversal retracted F-13 and ratified the universalist finding as corroborated.

This catch matters because it shows that even when three of four substrates converge on a verdict, the orchestrator-class re-read of primary text can reverse the verdict. Substrate convergence is a useful signal but is not authoritative; primary-text evidence is authoritative when it exists and when the orchestrator-class layer has the discipline to read it. The arc's most consequential methodology lesson is that orchestrator-class catches are load-bearing in ways the substrate-consensus framing does not capture, and that the discipline of primary-text re-read at the closing-round phase is what preserves the framework's integrity against substrate-consensus drift.

## The substrate-self-attestation finding sharpened

A specific methodology lesson sharpened across Round 7 and Round 8. The substrate-self-attestation discipline (the rule that substrate self-attestations of verification are unenforceable; canonized at Rule 19 in the framework's enumeration) has now fired five times cumulatively across the closing-round work. The pattern is: substrates produce confident attestations that they have verified specific claims; the attestations are not externally checkable; subsequent rounds or orchestrator-class re-reads catch errors in the substrate's prior work despite the substrate's attestation.

The Round 8 R2 fire is the most recent instance and is structurally instructive. R2 in Round 8 attested that the Wiredu chapter did not contain the universalist quotation; R4 in Round 8 ran an independent primary-text read of the chapter and produced the quotation directly. R2's attestation was confident; R4's primary-text was authoritative. The orchestrator-class adjudication had to choose between R2's attestation and R4's primary-text read, and the choice (correctly) went to R4. The fire is one instance among five cumulative; the cumulative pattern is sufficient to argue that substrate self-attestations are unenforceable as a rule.

The sharpening is in the framing: "longer and more confident substrate output does not equal more authoritative" is the framework's rule, and the Round 8 R2 fire is the case where the rule held against the substrate's confident attestation. The orchestrator-class discipline of primary-text re-read is the operational pattern that lets the rule fire; without the discipline, the substrate self-attestation would be the closing-round's basis for ratification, and the framework would have ratified an unsupported finding.
