## Thesis

The 5-element framework (acknowledgment language; primary-text-only citation; no clinical-vocabulary in code identifiers; humility brackets; retraction condition with 14-day response timeline) is the framework's commitment to how it engages with cross-cultural source traditions. ## Sections (outline)

1. **Opening humility paragraph (MANDATORY per 5-element discipline).** Acknowledge: operator is a solo indie developer drawing on structural elements of cross-cultural philosophical and psychological traditions; not representing any tradition; declines paid scholar review + carries interpretive risk solo; invites knowledgeable correction.

2. **Hook:** "I borrow structural elements from cross-cultural traditions for procedural-character design. I don't implement those traditions. The difference matters, and this post is my commitment to maintaining it."

3. **The 5-element disclosure discipline (canonical statement).**

   1. **Acknowledgment language.** Specifically: "I draw on structural elements of [tradition] while declining the use of stage-by-stage application to procedural NPCs / declining to operationalize [tradition's framework] through quantitative crossing of demographic variables." NEVER "I implement [tradition]" or "I use [tradition]."

   2. **What to cite (primary texts mandatory; no substrate paraphrases as substitute).** Specific primary-text anchors for each tradition the framework engages:
      - Wiredu, K., & Gyekye, K. (Eds.). (1992). *Person and Community*. CRVP (Akan philosophy primary text)
      - Cross, W. E., Jr. (1971; 1991). Nigrescence framework primary texts
      - Crenshaw, K. (1989; 1991). Intersectionality framework primary texts
      - Henrich, J., Heine, S. J., & Norenzayan, A. (2010). The WEIRDest people. *BBS* 33(2-3):61-83
      - Operator personally reads these before drafting any post that invokes them

   3. **No cross-cultural vocabulary in code identifiers.** Specifically: no Akan / Yoruba / Zulu / Sanskrit / Mandarin / Spanish / culturally-loaded vocabulary in `case` values, function names, type names, or property names. Neutral identifiers: `pair_memory`, `relationship_edge`, `core_event`, `reflective_summary`, `cooperation_trait`. Cross-cultural philosophy stays at the framework + research-paper layer; code stays neutral.

   4. **Opening + closing humility paragraphs.** Every post that invokes any cross-cultural tradition MUST open + close with humility paragraph (analogous to this post's opening). Specifies what's NOT being claimed; restates the retraction condition.

   5. **Post-publication response discipline + retraction condition.** If a knowledgeable critic (in-tradition scholar; advocate; community member) shows material misrepresentation of any cross-cultural tradition: RETRACT the cross-cultural framing within 14 days of critique receipt; keep technical content if it stands independently; partial retraction acceptable. "Material misrepresentation" judged by primary-text defensibility; substrate-paraphrase defenses do NOT carry weight.

4. **Why this discipline exists.**
   - The operator is a solo indie developer, not a peer-reviewed scholar
   - Cross-cultural traditions deserve respect on their own terms, not as raw material for game design
   - The framework would prefer no cross-cultural framing over careless cross-cultural framing
   - The discipline is the operator's commitment to do better than careless

5. **The traditions Modified-C-bis engages.**
   - **Wiredu's reading of Akan personhood-as-achievement** (Ch 9 *Person and Community* 1992; striving structure at p. 200; universalist framing at p. 193 chapter opening + p. 199 first principle of morals — per F-17 CORROBORATED 2026-06-10).
