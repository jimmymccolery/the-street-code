## Modification 6 — Strawson Episodic axis

### Primary text

- **Strawson, G. (2004).** Against narrativity. ***Ratio*** 17(4):428-452.

### Page citations (FLAG F-9 corrected 2026-06-10)

- **p. 428-429:** psychological-Narrativity (descriptive) + ethical-Narrativity (normative) theses Strawson ARGUES AGAINST. **NOT a license for two-level split.** The prior canonization "p. 429 two-level licensing" is WRONG attribution.
- **p. 430 (CORRECT):** Episodic/Diachronic definitions. Episodic = "one has little or no sense that the self that one is was there in the (further) past and will be there in the future, although one is perfectly well aware that one has long-term continuity considered as a whole human being."
- **Footnote 4 (p. 430-431 boundary):** content is about Episodic-vs-Narrative distinction — NOT a form-finding tendency (R4-2 misattributed).
- **Form-finding discussion approx. p. 441:** form-finding said to "go essentially beyond being Diachronic."
- **p. 447:** harm-of-self-telling — "every studied conscious recall of past events brings an alteration."

### Modification 6 final wording (CW R6 Section 4.4)

> "NPC self-experience is modeled on the Episodic axis: present-state-anchored, first-person, within-era, and non-narrative (Strawson 2004, Episodic definition p. 430). The Episodic axis is history-shaped, not ahistorical: chronologically accumulated experience integrates into a running affective state that biases encoding and behavior but is never exposed to the NPC as a first-person autobiographical narrative in V1. V1 NPCs are specified [-D -F]: they exclude diachronic self-experience and the form-finding tendency, both of which Strawson associates with the revision-prone narrative mode (form-finding discussion approx. p. 441; the harm of studied recall, p. 447). The NPC's *social-status trajectory* across eras is a separate, observer-level construction: it is the player's narrative reading plus the achievement-graded framing borrowed from Wiredu's reading of Akan personhood. It is not Strawson's Diachronic axis and is not sourced to Strawson."
