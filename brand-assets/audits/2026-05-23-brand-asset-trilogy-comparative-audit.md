  🎯 Cross-cutting observations                                
                                                                                                                                                                                              
  Pattern 1 — Subtitle as canonical-self-documentation gate. The Gang + The Archetypes posters BOTH have explicit subtitles and per-element captions; Nine Sigils has neither. This is the
  single biggest accessibility-differential dimension across the trilogy. Recommend Nine Sigils refresh adds subtitle as the highest-ROI single improvement.                                  
                                                               
  Pattern 2 — Diversity correlates inversely with mnemonic-archetype-legibility. The Gang poster's instantly-legible cultural archetypes (jock/nerd/cop/stoner) come bundled with the         
  demographic narrowing concern (young white male defaults). The Archetypes poster trades some mnemonic instant-legibility (6 vs 4 characters; less culturally-stock archetypes) for
  substantially-improved demographic representation. This suggests a mnemonic-archetype-legibility ↔ diversity-representation tradeoff — operator-aware design decision, not a gap.           
                                                               
  Pattern 3 — Canonical fidelity scales with self-documenting visual elements. The Archetypes poster's per-character + per-function + per-status captions create the highest canonical        
  fidelity; Nine Sigils' lack of per-sigil legend creates the lowest. Self-documentation = canonical-fidelity-mechanism, not just accessibility-mechanism.
                                                                                                                                                                                              
  Pattern 4 — Aesthetic register stratification works as designed. Vintage pulp (Nine Sigils, structural-anchor layer) + synthwave youth (Gang, AI-side actor layer) + synthwave professional 
  (Archetypes, human-side actor layer) = three distinct visual registers mapped to three distinct conceptual layers. Brand-asset trilogy coherent without being repetitive.
