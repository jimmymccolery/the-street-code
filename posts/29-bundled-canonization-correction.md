## The bundling event at S009 Step 2 Stage 1

The bundling that Decision 1C corrected happened at S009 Step 2 Stage 1 on 2026-05-08. Per `lessons-learned.md` line 472, "S009 Step 2 Phase B executed Topic 27 promotion (AI-side Beatles model rename + operator-side 'The' prefix archetype canonization + Squad → Gang collective-noun rename + anti-pattern #16 citation correction) across ~17 canonical-scope files via 5 sequential verify-then-deploy stages plus 1 corrective Stage 1.5 rollback." The `operator_role_archetypes.md` v1.0 creation at Stage 1 happened inside the multi-stage rename arc. The file's v1.0 header self-declared "Status: Canonical." Per the artifact's own Provenance section "Bundled-canonization-correction context" subsection (line 142): "The v1.0 file-header self-declaration of 'Status: Canonical' at this artifact's creation 2026-05-08 was bundled with Topic 27 Beatles model rename at S009 Step 2 Stage 1 — a multi-stage rename deployment arc covering AI-side Beatles model rename + operator-side 'The' prefix archetype canonization + Squad → Gang collective-noun rename. The bundling means file-header canonical declaration was a side-effect of the rename arc rather than the product of standalone architecture-revision deliberation on the underlying canonization decision (do these archetypes meet the bar for first-class canonical promotion?)."

The bundling is not an integrity failure at canonical-state level. The substantive canonization had empirical foundation: the six archetypes had fired across the S008 2026-05-07 session arc with per-archetype anchors per `research_queue.md` Topic 14, and the operator-side architecture parallel to the AI-side schema canonized at `role_output_schemas.md` v1.2 was operationally coherent. The deliberation step would have asked the canonization question explicitly rather than letting the file-header declaration ride on the rename arc's broader scope. That step happened retroactively at Decision 1C 2026-05-10.

The hybrid ratification corrected the bundled canonization in two distinct ways. First, the substantive five-archetype canonization received architecture-revision-session-level ratification — the deliberation step that the bundling had skipped now happened explicitly, with Council substrates evaluating each archetype against the multi-fire-within-session bar at 2026-05-07 S008 session arc and against the cross-session validation evidence accumulating since. Second, the per-archetype empirical foundation asymmetry — The Herald's single substantive empirical anchor falling below the cross-session multi-fire bar at Decision 1C apply time — was recognized at element level rather than papered over at file-header level. The Herald was held as research with explicit revisit criteria documented in the same Provenance section.

Per the arc summary observation 3 at `lessons-learned.md` line 1653: "Decision 1C surfaced and corrected a structurally important canonical-record-discipline observation: bundled file-level decisions (e.g., file-header self-declaration during multi-stage rename arc) are not equivalent to standalone architecture-revision-session-level deliberation. Future canonization decisions on first-class canonical artifacts should be standalone architecture-revision deliberations. Decision 1C's hybrid ratification with explicit per-element ratification serves as canonical example of correcting bundled file-level decisions retroactively."

The corrective mechanism is structural rather than corrective at the artifact level. The artifact's body did not change at v1.0 → v1.1. What changed was the canonical-record-discipline status: the file-header self-declaration was replaced with hybrid ratification declaring "Canonical (Hybrid)" per Decision 1C ratification reference + Council outcome + per-archetype status flag pointer; per-archetype status flags were applied beneath each section header; the Provenance section expanded with four subsections including "Bundled-canonization-correction context" documenting the bundling event explicitly.

## The inverse pattern relative to Decision 1B

The bundled-canonization-correction question is structurally distinct from the canonical-state ambiguity Decision 1B addressed. Per `lessons-learned.md` line 1594 Decision 1C structural reasoning: "The bundled-canonization-correction question is structurally distinct from Decision 1B's external-framing-vs-file-header ambiguity (Decision 1B addressed external canonical framing without formal file-header declaration; Decision 1C addresses file-header self-declaration without architecture-revision deliberation — inverse pattern)."

Decision 1B's underlying canonical-state evidence was external framing in canonical-record entries describing `operational_discipline_rules_2026-05-07.md` as a canonical artifact while the file header itself did not formally declare canonical status. The ambiguity was external-framing-without-file-header-declaration. The resolution was first-class canonical promotion via new canonical file at `operational_discipline_rules.canonical.md` v1.0 with formal Status header declaring first-class canonical artifact. [Post 26](26-operational-discipline-rules-canonical-artifact.md) documents the canonical artifact in depth.

Decision 1C's underlying canonical-state evidence was file-header self-declaration ("Status: Canonical" at v1.0 / 2026-05-08) without architecture-revision deliberation. The ambiguity was file-header-declaration-without-standalone-deliberation. The resolution was hybrid ratification per-archetype with the deliberation step happening retroactively. The two decisions form an inverse pair on the canonical-state ambiguity axis: one had external framing claiming canonical status that the file header had not declared; the other had file-header declaration that no standalone deliberation had ratified.

The inverse-pair framing matters for canonical-record-discipline at architecture-revision-session scope. Decision 1B's resolution sets a precedent for promoting artifacts with external canonical framing into formal first-class canonical status. Decision 1C's resolution sets a precedent for retroactively ratifying file-header self-declarations whose underlying canonization decision was bundled. Both resolutions are now canonical-supported corrective mechanisms; future architecture-revision sessions can map ambiguous canonical-state evidence onto whichever pattern applies and apply the corresponding corrective mechanism.

## Per-element ratification as corrective template

Hybrid ratification with explicit per-element ratification is the corrective mechanism of choice when bundled-canonization is discovered post-deployment. Per `research_queue.md` Topic 37 methodological frame at line 1484: "Hybrid ratification template extension: Decision 1C's per-element ratification template (5 canonical + 1 research-held with revisit criteria) generalizes to other potentially-bundled canonizations; develop reusable hybrid ratification template."

The mechanism works because per-element ratification matches the empirical-foundation granularity of canonical artifacts whose elements may have asymmetric empirical support. Operator role archetypes is one such artifact: each archetype has its own empirical foundation citations (NEW TASK spec drafting fires for The Architect; Phase A → B operator confirmations for The Helmsman; substrate-side false-flag fire surfacing for The Sentinel; methodology research observations for The Cartographer; bounded-scope enforcement for The Steward; plain-language SUMMARY.md translation for The Herald). When the empirical foundations vary in cross-session multi-fire bar satisfaction, file-level binary canonical/non-canonical declaration over-claims for elements at the margin and under-recognizes elements that meet the bar substantively.

Per-element ratification also produces explicit revisit criteria for held-as-research elements. The Herald's revisit criteria per `operator_role_archetypes.md` Provenance section (line 150) name three conditions: multi-fire bar (≥3 distinct Herald-function fires across ≥2 distinct sessions post-Cluster-1F); scope stability (Herald scope stable post-Substack-removal); and optional substrate-distance triggering condition (audience-advocate function operating load-bearing in cross-substrate audit work). The revisit criteria do canonical-record-discipline work that file-level non-canonical declaration cannot: they make explicit what evidence would shift the element from research-held to canonical, which keeps the canonization candidacy review process operator-judgment-driven rather than scheduled.

The Sentinel ratification under the hybrid mechanism has structural significance beyond the per-archetype empirical foundation. Per the Decision 1C entry at `lessons-learned.md` line 1594: "The Sentinel ratification preserves Topic 26 (recovery-from-drift threshold) mechanism #4 4-mechanism composition; without ratification, mechanism #4 would reference research-queue-only role architecture, weakening canonical-record-discipline traceability for the framework's recovery-from-drift mechanism." [Post 17](17-recovery-from-drift-as-framework-discipline.md) treats the recovery-from-drift framework where mechanism #4 lives. Per-element ratification let the Sentinel canonization happen specifically and preserve the recovery-from-drift discipline composition without forcing a file-level binary that would either over-canonize The Herald or under-canonize The Sentinel.

## What the precedent doesn't establish

The precedent rests on a single empirical fire (n=1) per `research_queue.md` Topic 37 line 1473: "Cross-fire replication at filing time: zero; this is the only documented bundled-canonization-correction event." Whether the bundled-canonization pattern recurs across the canonical record at other first-class canonical artifacts is unverified at filing time. The audit-target enumeration that would supply the cross-fire evidence has not been executed.

The precedent does not establish that all file-header self-declarations are bundled-canonization candidates. Some file-header canonical declarations may have happened at standalone architecture-revision deliberation; others may have happened in bundled deployment work that nevertheless involved the canonization deliberation explicitly (rather than as a side-effect). The bundling pattern Decision 1C corrected is specifically the side-effect pattern, where the file-header declaration was not the product of the underlying canonization deliberation. The audit would distinguish bundled-with-deliberation from bundled-as-side-effect.

The precedent does not claim hybrid ratification is the only corrective mechanism for bundled-canonization. Per-element ratification fit `operator_role_archetypes.md`'s per-archetype empirical-foundation granularity. Canonical artifacts whose elements share the same empirical foundation may warrant different corrective mechanisms — full ratification, full downgrade, or file-level binary canonical/non-canonical declaration without per-element splits. The corrective template generalizes to canonical artifacts with element-level empirical-foundation asymmetry; it does not generalize unconditionally.

The precedent does not close the canonical-artifact creation protocol question. Whether future canonical artifact creation should explicitly require standalone-deliberation flag at architecture-revision session is open at canonical scope per Topic 37 line 1479. The trigger awaits operational surface — the next canonical artifact creation will be the natural decision point.

Future architecture-revision sessions deliberating canonical-state ambiguity can map evidence onto whichever pattern applies and reach for the corresponding corrective mechanism per the precedent each Decision sets.

The precedent also establishes the audit scope question as canonical-record-discipline observation worth tracking. The question remains open at filing time — the audit-target enumeration is part of Topic 37's outstanding research per line 1473 — but it is canonical-record-supported as a question worth surfacing for operational consideration when the operator decides to initiate the audit.
