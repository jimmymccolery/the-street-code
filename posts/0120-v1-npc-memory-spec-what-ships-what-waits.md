## V1 architecture (locked)

The framework's V1 architecture commits to nine specific architectural elements. Each element is documented in detail in the prior posts of this arc; this post enumerates the commitments at the META-level so that the V1 spec is a single navigable artifact.

The three-layer memory architecture commits to Layer 1 (raw events; bounded recency window per character; the bounded window is approximately the last six to twelve game-months), Layer 2 (encoded-and-reconstructed perspective memory; consolidated entries promoted from Layer 1 during the yearly consolidation pass per the framework's promotion criteria), and Layer 3 (accumulator; running affective state per character; constant per-character storage cost regardless of event count). The architecture is documented in detail in post 0118 in this arc.

The DC1 split for affective state commits to valence, arousal, and stress as three independent scalars rather than a single mood scalar. The split is grounded in the dimensional-circumplex literature (Russell 1980 *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* 39(6):1161-1178 for valence-arousal; Lazarus and Folkman 1984 *Stress, Appraisal, and Coping* for the stress channel) and produces three encoding equations rather than one; the PAD optional-dominance dimension is deferred (not in V1 critical-path).

The trait sources by function commit to: HEXACO honesty-humility for cooperation, exploitation, and generosity behaviors (per Hilbig and Zettler 2009 *Journal of Research in Personality* 43(3):516-519, Thielmann and Hilbig 2015 *Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin* 41(11):1523-1536, and Zettler et al. 2020 *Perspectives on Psychological Science* 15(3):723-760); attachment avoidance and anxiety dimensions for relational encoding (per Fraley, Waller, and Brennan 2000 *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* 78(2):350-365 ECR-R, and Mikulincer and Shaver 2016 *Attachment in Adulthood* second edition); occupation-situated cognition for skill-shaped perception (the broader procedural-cognition literature). The trait sourcing correction (HEXACO H to attachment style for relational encoding) is documented in post 0115 in this arc.

The pair-relational history commits to ordered-pair sentiment scalars in the range from negative one to positive one; sentiment override per Hawkins, Carrère, and Gottman 2002 *Journal of Marriage and Family* 64(1):193-201; an active-sentiments-per-ordered-pair cap of three to five entries with decay-to-summary for older sentiments. The mechanism is documented in detail in post 0116 in this arc.

The consolidation pass commits to a yearly trigger that promotes core events (high-affect; high-pair-history-impact; trait-modulated-significant; first-occurrence) to Layer 2 and decays everyday events via the 0.995-per-game-hour anchor. The architecture and the anchor's design rationale are documented in post 0118 in this arc.

The active inference under Path A commits to sophisticated active inference plus structural-only self-reflection per the framework's Modification 6 Amendment under the closing-round Revision. The boundary discriminator is state-space content (the generative model contains no cross-era self-identity variable); the naive-versus-sophisticated, horizon-depth, and Episodic-versus-Diachronic axes are treated as orthogonal-to-sophistication axes that the framework's commitments do not couple. The audit-first sequencing for the active-inference apparatus deployment is documented in post 0119 in this arc.

The state-space-content audit invariant commits to: no latent state variable indexes character identity across eras. The invariant is the framework's primary audit-layer check; the audit enumerates the generative-model tensor's state variables and verifies the invariant; the invariant's violation flags the architecture as not satisfying position-(a) discipline.

The four-audit mandatory test surface commits to: state-space content audit (primary); amnesia re-initialization test (mandatory); Markov-blanket isolation test; horizon-bound test (τ ≤ 3) as secondary defense-in-depth. The surface's audits are documented in detail across the prior posts in this arc; the audits all run at audit-layer cadence rather than per-frame.

The three retraction-condition triggers commit to: state-space self-variable (Trigger 1; fires if the amnesia re-init test fails); phenomenal-claim leakage (Trigger 2; fires if the framework's marketing copy or player-facing communication leaks phenomenal-claim language about NPC consciousness or self-awareness); bridge over-extension (Trigger 3; fires if the framework's narrative-active-inference bridge from Bouizegarene et al. 2024 *Frontiers in Psychology* 15:1345480 is extended to non-narrative recursive inference in ways the paper does not support). The retraction discipline is operational; the triggers are the framework's public commitments to specific retraction conditions.

## What ships sequentially versus in parallel

The V1 implementation sequences specific elements in a specific order; the sequencing matters because the elements have integration dependencies that the order respects.

First (audit-layer foundation): the state-space content audit, the amnesia re-initialization test, and the Markov-blanket isolation test. The audit-layer apparatus is established before runtime decisions depend on sophisticated inference; the apparatus produces the empirical foundation that subsequent sequencing depends on (the audit-first discipline from post 0119 in this arc applies). The first-sequence implementation cost is in the order of approximately 40 to 60 hours of BUILD-side work; the audit-layer apparatus is V1-shippable independently of the runtime-layer apparatus.

Second (cached policies at runtime): naive active inference scoring at runtime; cached policies for predictable per-frame budget; sophisticated active inference at consolidation boundaries. The cached-policies-with-slow-consolidation pattern is the framework's runtime-layer commitment under audit-first sequencing; the pattern is grounded in commercial precedent (Project Zomboid offscreen-storylet architecture; post 0123 in this arc walks through the pattern) and extended with active-inference machinery at the slow layer. The cached-policies implementation cost is in the order of approximately 60 to 100 hours of BUILD-side work; the implementation depends on the audit-layer foundation being in place.

Third (sophisticated runtime promotion, conditional on empirical evidence): after empirical evidence from the audit layer demonstrates that cached policies are insufficient for V1 game-feel, sophisticated active inference may promote to runtime use. The promotion is V1.x or V1 final-polish phase work, depending on the outcomes of the framework's Round 9 generative-model formalization arc and the V1 player-facing pilot's findings. The promotion is not V1 critical-path; the promotion is a post-V1-evidence decision; the framework's V1 commitment is the audit-first deployment, and the runtime promotion is what the empirical evidence may support.

## The retraction-condition canonical statement

The framework's three retraction-condition triggers are locked at the Round 8 R4 ratification. The triggers' specific framings are:

Trigger 1 fires if the framework's V1 implementation accidentally encodes a state-space self-variable that the amnesia re-initialization test detects. The trigger's response is to identify the offending architectural element, redesign the element to remove the state-space self-variable, and reship the corrected V1 implementation; the trigger does not require retraction of the framework's broader claims if the corrective work resolves the trigger's underlying issue.

Trigger 2 fires if the framework's marketing copy or player-facing communication leaks phenomenal-claim language about NPC consciousness or NPC self-awareness in ways the framework's structural-only commitments do not support. The trigger's response is to revise the offending communication and reissue; the trigger requires the framework to engage with whether the leak reflects a broader claim-drift in the framework's communication discipline.

Trigger 3 fires if the framework's narrative-active-inference bridge from Bouizegarene et al. 2024 is extended to non-narrative recursive inference in ways the paper does not support. The trigger's response is to bound the bridge back to the paper's narrative scope and to revise any framework claims that depended on the over-extension; the trigger's catch is what the audit-layer apparatus checks against periodically.

The triggers' non-firing conditions (the conditions that do not fire the triggers despite resembling them superficially) are also explicit. Sophisticated inference per se is not a Trigger 1 firing condition (sophistication is recursive metacognition, not a state-space self-variable); deep horizon per se is not a Trigger 1 firing condition (horizon depth is not the state-space-content discriminator); player-side diachronic narrative about NPC behavior is not a Trigger 1 firing condition (the diachronic-narrative attribution is the player's mental model, not the NPC's generative model). The discipline is operational; the triggers are bounded to specific failure modes rather than to broad categories of related-but-distinct architectural patterns.
