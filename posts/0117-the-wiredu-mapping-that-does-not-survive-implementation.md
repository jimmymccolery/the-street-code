## The original mapping idea

Wiredu's reading of Akan personhood-as-achievement (Chapter 9 *Person and Community: Ghanaian Philosophical Studies, I*; Wiredu and Gyekye, editors, 1992; Council for Research in Values and Philosophy Series II.1) emphasizes sympathetic impartiality as the first principle of morals. Wiredu Chapter 9 page 199: sympathetic impartiality is "the first principle of all morals" and "the logical basis of the golden rule." The principle is a procedural moral test: the test asks whether an action's outcome would be acceptable to the person if their position were reversed with the position of the action's recipient; if yes under sympathetic imagination, the action is morally permissible; if not, the action is not.

HEXACO honesty-humility (commonly abbreviated as HEXACO H; the trait dimension within the HEXACO six-dimensional personality model that captures honesty-fairness-greed-avoidance-modesty patterns; Ashton and Lee 2009 *Journal of Personality Assessment* 91(4):340-345 is the canonical short-form measurement instrument) measures resistance to exploitation. The trait's validation literature (Hilbig and Zettler 2009 *Journal of Research in Personality* 43(3):516-519; Thielmann and Hilbig 2015 *Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin* 41(11):1523-1536; Zettler et al. 2020 *Perspectives on Psychological Science* 15(3):723-760) is broad enough to support the framework's adoption of HEXACO H as the trait source for cooperation-and-exploitation behaviors in procedural characters.

The surface intuition was that high-H NPCs are more sympathetically impartial; Wiredu's framework would provide philosophical grounding for the trait; the framework's V1 generosity mechanic would route Wiredu-grounded sympathetic impartiality through the HEXACO H trait dimension as the operational implementation. The mapping was defensible at first glance because both Wiredu's principle and HEXACO H operate at the moral-evaluation surface of character behavior; both predict more-cooperative behavior; both ground the prediction in something like fairness-toward-others reasoning. The mapping looked like a clean philosophical-grounding move for the trait.

The mapping does not survive primary-text pressure. The reasons are three; the rest of this post walks through each.

## Why the mapping fails: category error

Wiredu's sympathetic impartiality is a procedural moral test. The test is something the moral agent DOES (imagine the outcome from each affected party's position; evaluate whether the action would be acceptable regardless of which position you occupied; if yes, the action is permissible). The test is a deliberative act performed by the agent on specific candidate actions; the test's output is a per-action permissibility verdict.

HEXACO H is an individual-difference trait dimension. The dimension is something the agent HAS (a dispositional tendency that predicts behavior across multiple situations; the prediction is statistical rather than per-action); the dimension's score is measured by self-report or peer-report on validated psychometric instruments. The dimension is a dispositional state that exists as a trait of the agent; the trait's expression in behavior is statistical and predicts patterns of behavior across populations of situations.

The category error is mapping a doing onto a having. Wiredu's procedural test is not a trait dimension; the test is a deliberative act. Conversely, HEXACO H is not a procedural test; the trait is a dispositional state. A high-H character is not "more sympathetically impartial" in Wiredu's sense; the character is statistically less exploitative across populations of situations. The two predictions overlap (a character who is statistically less exploitative will tend to pass Wiredu's sympathetic-impartiality test on more of the candidate actions the character considers); the overlap is not the same as identity. The mapping conflates the trait's statistical-prediction property with the test's per-action-verdict property; the conflation is the category error.

The category error has been named in adjacent philosophy-of-personality literature; specifically, the error of treating personality-trait dimensions as moral-evaluation mechanisms when the trait dimension is dispositional and the moral evaluation is deliberative. The literature on this distinction is not the framework's primary engagement (the framework is procedural-character design, not philosophy-of-personality scholarship); the category-error pattern is recognizable from the literature, and the framework's catching the error in its own first-pass spec is an instance of the broader pattern. The lesson generalizes beyond the Wiredu case; the lesson is about not mapping deliberative procedural tests onto dispositional trait dimensions when the architecture's commitment is to one or the other; the framework's commitment is to dispositional trait dimensions at the V1 implementation layer; the deliberative procedural tests stay at the philosophical-framing layer where they belong.

## Why the mapping fails: ornamental-deployment risk

Bilge 2013, "Intersectionality undone: Saving intersectionality from feminist intersectionality studies," *Du Bois Review: Social Science Research on Race* 10(2):405-424, names a risk class the framework needs to engage with directly. Bilge's paper is about a different specific case (the academic appropriation of intersectionality from its grounding in Black feminist theory); the structural shape of the risk class is broader than the specific case. The risk class is ornamental deployment: borrowing the prestige of a source tradition while implementing none of the substance.

The ornamental-deployment risk applies to the Wiredu-mapping case directly. If a V1 implementation scored individual utility with no communal-relational accountability mechanic, while the design documents cite Wiredu as the philosophical grounding for the generosity trait, the V1 implementation is ornamental in Bilge's sense. The philosophical citation adds reputation to the framework; the mechanic implements no communal accountability that would deliver on the Wiredu citation's substantive content; the gap between citation and implementation is the harm. The gap matters because the citation's claim is that the framework is engaging with Wiredu's substantive philosophical content; the implementation's substance is that the framework is doing nothing of the kind.

The "worse than no mapping" framing is sharp and worth surfacing directly. A V1 implementation that did not cite Wiredu at all would simply be a V1 implementation with no philosophical-grounding claim; the implementation would be evaluated on its own technical merits, and the cross-cultural-engagement question would not arise. A V1 implementation that cites Wiredu but implements no communal accountability is worse than the no-citation case because the citation makes a claim the implementation cannot deliver. The dishonesty of the citation is the harm; the dishonesty is what Bilge's framework names as ornamental.

The cross-cultural appropriation literature beyond Bilge is broader than what the framework's research arc has engaged in depth; the framework's adoption of Bilge's framing is one engagement with one strand of the broader literature; the broader literature would extend the ornamental-deployment framing into related discussions (e.g., decolonial-theory critiques of academic engagement with non-Western traditions; specific anthropological-research-ethics standards; specific cross-cultural-philosophy methodology guidelines). The framework's engagement is bounded; the bounds are part of the disclosure discipline; the broader literature is what knowledgeable critics would press the framework on if the framework's commitment is examined critically.

## The universalist-framing question and the reversal

Round 4-2 (one of the earlier rounds in the framework's multi-round philosophical research arc) originally claimed that Wiredu frames sympathetic impartiality as a human universal derivable by any culture's rational reflection. Round 6 (a closing-round substrate cycle) flagged the universalist claim as uncorroborated; the framework's canonical record set the flag (the framework calls these flags F-1, F-2, etc.; this flag was F-13) to "uncorroborated" status. Round 8 R2 (the citation-rigor research substrate in a subsequent round) re-read Wiredu Chapter 9 pages 199 through 205 and confirmed the uncorroborated status; the canonical record propagated the F-13 status forward.

Round 8 R4 (the closing-round Madisonian Gate synthesis; the framework's term for the role of orchestrator-class substrate that adjudicates cross-substrate divergences) re-read Wiredu Chapter 9 directly at a different reading depth than R2 had used. R4 noticed something R2 had missed: the chapter OPENS at page 193 with "Morality in the strictest sense is universal to human culture... essential to all human culture." R4 then re-read page 199 with the chapter-opening context: Wiredu states explicitly that sympathetic impartiality "is the first principle of all morals" (page 199) and "the logical basis of the golden rule" (page 199); the framing is universalist by Wiredu's own text. The R4-2 original claim was right; R2 had been wrong; F-13 was retracted in favor of a new flag F-17 (the universalist framing is corroborated; the prior arc's "uncorroborated" status was a primary-text reading error).

The reversal matters operationally for the framework's citation discipline. The framework's prior commitment, under F-13's uncorroborated status, had been to avoid citing Wiredu as a distinctively cross-cultural anchor (the framework's reading at the time was that Wiredu was Akan-specific and could not be cited as a universalist claim). The framework's corrected commitment, under F-17's corroborated status, is to cite Wiredu for the striving-not-introspection structure (Chapter 9 page 200: "every cultivated Akan *Okaniba* sees life as a scenario of continual striving after personhood in ever increasing dimensions"; the striving structure IS in the text) rather than for cross-cultural breadth (since Wiredu himself frames the principle as universalist; citing Wiredu as a "distinctively cross-cultural" anchor would itself be a mild misattribution of the position Wiredu is taking).

The methodology lesson is sharper than the citation correction. Multi-substrate cross-verification (the framework's Rule 11) is necessary but not sufficient; cross-substrate convergence on a wrong reading can propagate through multiple rounds if no substrate operates at the reading depth that would catch the wrong reading. The R2-versus-R4 distinction is the operational example: R2 read pages 199-205 and missed the chapter-opening universalist framing at page 193; R4 read the chapter with the opening context and caught what R2 had missed. The reading-depth difference is what catches the wrong reading; the framework's discipline of routing the orchestrator-class substrate at a different reading depth than the per-question research substrates is what makes the catching possible.

## Why the universalist reversal doesn't save the mapping

Even with universalist framing corroborated, the category error and the ornamental-deployment risk are independent problems. A universalist procedural moral test is still not a trait dimension (the category error stands; the mapping conflates a deliberative act with a dispositional state regardless of whether the deliberative act is culture-specific or universal). A V1 implementation that cites Wiredu but implements no communal accountability is still ornamental (the ornamental-deployment risk stands; the citation's claim is that the framework engages Wiredu's substantive content; the implementation's substance is that the framework does no such engagement; the gap is the harm regardless of whether the cited content is culture-specific or universal).

The mapping does not survive. The framework's Round 7 R6 deliberation cycle adopted Option (c) from a three-option decision tree (Option (a): retain Wiredu as load-bearing AND build a real communal-accountability mechanic; Option (b): decouple HEXACO H from Wiredu philosophical grounding; Option (c): drop the Wiredu mapping as load-bearing V1 mechanics, retain only as philosophical framing in the lead philosophical-inspiration post (post 0112 in this arc), the framework paper, and this self-correction post). The Option (c) ratification stands; the framework's V1 implementation does not include the Wiredu-mapping at the mechanic level; the framework's V1 generosity mechanic uses HEXACO H plus pair-relational history plus sentiment override (grounded on HEXACO plus attachment plus Gottman empirical literature) without the Wiredu philosophical layer.

The Path A operator ratification (the subsequent ratification that promoted sophisticated active inference and structural-only self-reflection to V1 critical-path) does not reverse Option (c). Path A is about active-inference machinery; Option (c) is about the Wiredu-HEXACO-H mapping; the two ratifications are independent decisions on different architectural axes. The framework's Option (c) ratification stands under Path A's broader commitment; the Wiredu mapping remains documentation-only.

## The Friston 2017 Appendix F retraction in parallel

The Wiredu universalist reversal (F-17) is one instance of a meta-pattern that the framework's research arc identified in the same Round 8 closing round. The parallel instance is the Friston 2017 Appendix F status (the canonical-record flag F-8). The two instances share a structural shape worth surfacing because the shape is what the meta-pattern names.

The framework had flagged Friston 2017 Appendix F as "uncorroborated" (specifically: probable conflation with Friston, Da Costa, Hafner, Hesp, and Parr 2021 "Sophisticated Inference"; the framework's research arc had encountered citation patterns where the two papers were being conflated by substrate paraphrases) for four rounds. The flag (F-8) had been carried through the canonical record across multiple rounds without primary-text re-read; the rounds had been reasoning over the F-8 flag rather than re-verifying it against the primary text.

Round 8 R2 (the citation-rigor research substrate) ran a direct primary-text re-read of Friston 2017 Appendix F and found the naive/sophisticated active-inference distinction sitting at pages 42-43 exactly where prior attribution had placed it. The Appendix F content is real; the prior conflation flag was a canonical-record drift, not a real conflation; F-8 was retracted (F-14 is the new flag denoting the retraction). Round 8 R4 (the orchestrator-class substrate) triangulated the R2 finding with an independent primary-text re-read at a different reading depth; the triangulation confirmed the retraction.

The pattern: canonical records carrying stale wrong flags because substrate paraphrase chains converged on a wrong reading. F-13 (Wiredu universalist uncorroborated) and F-8 (Friston Appendix F uncorroborated) share the structural shape. Both flags were set in earlier rounds based on substrate paraphrase chains; both flags were carried through multiple subsequent rounds without primary-text re-read; both flags were retracted in the same closing round when the orchestrator-class substrate's primary-text re-read produced the catch. The pattern is general beyond these two instances; the pattern is what the framework's discipline of periodic stale-flag re-reads is designed to prevent.

## The shared methodology lesson

Multi-substrate verification (the framework's Rule 11) is necessary but not sufficient. Cross-substrate convergence is not proof. Orchestrator-class primary-text re-reads at different reading depths are the discipline that catches what cross-substrate majority-vote misses. The framework's canonical record was wrong in two separate places, in the same week, on the same shape of error.

The discipline I have committed to since the closing round is to re-read flags older than two rounds against the primary text before the flags continue to constrain architectural reasoning. The discipline catches the canonical-record-drift failure mode at the structural level; the discipline produces flag re-evaluations that are grounded in primary-text rather than in substrate-paraphrase. The discipline is operationally cheap (a 30-minute to two-hour primary-text re-read per flag); the discipline catches the kind of error that propagates through multiple rounds and produces wrong architectural commitments downstream.

The discipline is general beyond the framework. Any operator running a multi-substrate research arc with a canonical record that accumulates flags over time faces the same canonical-record-drift failure mode; the discipline of periodic flag re-reads against primary text is what catches the drift; the alternative is the drift propagating through multiple rounds and producing wrong commitments that subsequent work absorbs as background assumptions. The discipline is small; the failure mode it catches is large; the cost-benefit is operationally clear.

## What this changes for procedural-character design more broadly

Cross-cultural philosophical anchors are legitimate philosophical inspiration. They can ground design-theory commitments at the framework-paper layer. They should NOT be operationalized as trait-coordinates without doing the operationalization work properly. The operationalization work properly would require: a real communal-accountability mechanic that delivers on the philosophical content's substantive claims (Option (a) cost: 33-88 hours; banked Steam V1.0 candidate; a subsequent post in this arc walks through what the Option (a) mechanic would actually require in code); primary-text-grounded citation discipline that cites the source tradition specifically rather than categorically; cross-cultural disclosure discipline that bounds the framework's engagement to structural-borrowing-only.

The five-element disclosure discipline I committed to in the lead philosophical-inspiration post (post 0112) is the framework's commitment for this kind of engagement. The discipline applies to any cross-cultural philosophical anchor the framework adopts; the discipline is the floor for the framework's engagement; the floor is what makes the framework's cross-cultural claims accountable to specific public standards.
