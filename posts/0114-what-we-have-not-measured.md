The hardest part of running a procedural-character design framework as an indie developer is being honest about the empirical gap. The framework I am developing (a working name for the framework is Modified-C-bis, an internal architecture specification for a multi-decade tycoon sim's procedural-character architecture) is a design theory. It is informed by literature; it is ratified through multi-substrate adjudication; it is grounded in primary-source reads; it has not been validated by an empirical pilot in a player-facing field setting. The distinction between informed and validated is what this post is about. The post documents what HAS NOT been measured; what the pre-registered pilot is designed to measure; what claims the framework explicitly does NOT make beyond the pilot's scope.

Pre-registration is the discipline that converts "we think this works" into "here's the test we will run and the conditions under which we will say it doesn't." The discipline is not specific to the framework; the broader open-science movement has developed the discipline as a response to the replication crisis in psychology and adjacent empirical fields. The framework adopts the discipline because the framework's claims are empirical-class claims (claims about player experience; claims about NPC believability; claims about the architecture's design predictions) and the alternative to pre-registration is publication-bias-without-discipline that the literature has documented in detail as a failure mode.

The post is structurally honest. The architecture documents committed to a particular shape of empirical anchor in earlier framework arcs; the architecture documents now reflect a modification (the framework calls it Modification 4) that demotes the empirical anchor to "design theory plus pre-registered pilot plan" rather than "validated framework." The demotion is what this post documents at the public-discourse level so that readers engaging with the framework's claims engage with them under the empirical status the framework actually has, not under the status the framework's earlier documents had implied.

## The framework's empirical status

The framework's substantive content includes specific claims about procedural-character architecture: a three-layer memory model with separate raw, encoded, and accumulator layers; a DC1 split that separates valence, arousal, and stress as independent affective scalars; trait-source pathways that route HEXACO honesty-humility to cooperation-and-exploitation behaviors and attachment style to relational-event encoding; pair-relational sentiment dynamics grounded in Hawkins, Carrère, and Gottman 2002 ("Marital quality: Modeling the dynamics of the relationship over the lifespan," *Journal of Marriage and Family* 64(1):193-201) sentiment-override; consolidation-pass mechanics that bound multi-decade memory at game-year cadence; sophisticated active inference at consolidation boundaries under an operator ratification the framework calls Path A; a four-audit mandatory test surface with state-space content audit as the primary check.

These claims are architectural commitments. They are grounded in the cited literature; they are internally coherent against the framework's other commitments; they are operationally implementable. They are not empirically validated at the player-facing level. The architecture's design predictions (e.g., that the pair-relational mechanic produces the kind of perceived character continuity that a multi-decade tycoon sim's design surface requires; that the audit-layer test surface catches state-space content violations reliably; that the cached-policies-plus-sophisticated-consolidation pattern delivers per-frame performance within iOS V1 budgets) are predictions; the predictions are what an empirical pilot would test, and the pilot has not run.

The Modification 4 demotion is the framework's response to the empirical status. The framework's earlier documents had cited specific empirical anchors as load-bearing for the architecture's claims; the demotion re-classifies those anchors as design-theory inspiration plus pre-registered-pilot-plan validation pathway. The re-classification does not change the architecture's substantive content; the re-classification changes what the framework is committing to about the substantive content's empirical status. The architecture commits to the substantive content as the framework's working design; the empirical-status claim is bounded to design theory until the pilot runs and produces evidence.

## What we have NOT measured

The list below enumerates the framework's empirical gap honestly. Each item is a specific claim that the framework's architecture commits to and that the framework has not measured at the player-facing level.

The cryptographic-puzzle intra-agent self-discovery effect on player Self-Concept Clarity has not been measured. The framework's architecture commits to a specific puzzle structure (described conceptually in a previous post in this arc as a second-order Bayesian inversion puzzle); the architecture commits to the puzzle producing a specific player-side effect (movement on Self-Concept Clarity, a construct from the social-psychology literature on self-knowledge; Campbell et al. 1996 *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* 70(1):141-156 is the canonical SCC measure); the architecture has not run the empirical measurement that would test whether the puzzle structure produces the predicted effect. The pre-registered pilot is the apparatus that would test the prediction; the apparatus has not run.

The framework's Marcus Reed Protocol's outcome-distribution improvement effect on actual NPC populations has not been independently replicated beyond a small internal sample (the framework's research arc documents an internal replication at N=11 substrate runs; independent replication at production scale has not happened). The protocol's audit methodology is documented internally; the methodology's effect on the NPC population the framework's character-generation pipeline produces is the empirical claim; the claim is supported at the internal-replication level and is not validated at the independent-replication or production-scale level.

The era-progressive identity-expression mechanic has no empirical anchor at all. The framework's architecture commits to a specific pattern of identity-expression evolution across game-decades (NPCs' self-presentation and behavior change across the multi-decade scope in ways the framework's design predictions specify); the architecture does not have an empirical anchor for the pattern. The mechanic is entirely a design proposal at the present moment; the cross-cultural literature the framework engages with (Cross 1971/1991 Nigrescence framework; Crenshaw 1989/1991 intersectionality) provides structural inspiration for the mechanic's shape, but the literature is human-scoped and the framework's adoption requires a substrate-neutrality bridge that the empirical validation has not run.

The two-level axis split (the framework's Modification 6, which separates Episodic-Diachronic axes from naive-sophisticated axes from horizon-depth axes as three orthogonal axes) has not been validated empirically. The framework's commitment to the orthogonality is theoretically grounded (the axes track different properties in the philosophy-of-mind and active-inference literatures) and operationally consequential (the orthogonality lets the framework be precise about which axes the V1 spec commits to without overcommitting on adjacent axes); the empirical claim that NPCs designed with the orthogonal-axis discipline produce the design-predicted behavior on each axis independently has not been measured. The claim is at the design-theory level; the empirical validation is the pilot's later scope.

The list is not exhaustive. The framework has other empirical-class claims (e.g., specific predictions about per-frame performance under cached-policy invalidation; specific predictions about consolidation-pass cost at multi-decade scale; specific predictions about cross-cultural identity-tag schema-placeholder effects on cross-cultural-audience reception) that the framework has not measured. The four claims above are load-bearing enough to enumerate explicitly; the broader gap is real and the framework's empirical-status claim is bounded by the broader gap.

## The pilot plan

The pre-registered pilot the framework has planned is an X-Phi arm only (X-Phi is short for experimental philosophy; the methodological tradition uses vignette-based survey methods to elicit player or participant intuitions about specific philosophical or design questions). The pilot is planned for July through August 2026, after a specific operator-side prerequisite closure that the framework's research arc requires before the pilot can deploy responsibly. The pilot has an OSF (Open Science Framework) preregistration the operator will file at the relevant OSF registry before the pilot deploys; the preregistration documents the hypotheses, the measurement instruments, the analysis plan, and the conditions under which the pilot would falsify the framework's design predictions.

The pilot uses cozy-game audience pre-screen recruitment to assemble a participant pool whose game-genre preferences align with the framework's design surface; the pre-screen reduces noise from participants whose evaluation criteria would not be aligned with the design surface the framework is designed for. The pilot uses an independent ethics protocol the operator's research practice has established (the protocol is documented internally; the framework's broader scope discipline keeps protocol-specifics out of public posts). The pilot uses abstract Character A and Character B vignettes throughout; the vignettes do not name specific NPC roster members from the framework's commercial product, and the abstraction is the discipline that lets the pilot's findings be publishable without leaking commercial-scope NPC-specific details into the public record.

The pilot's narrow claim is one specific test. The test measures how participants attribute personhood properties to a procedurally-described Character A versus a procedurally-described Character B when the descriptive structure varies along specific axes the framework's architecture commits to. The narrow scope is deliberate; the pilot does not test the framework as a whole; the pilot tests one specific design prediction that the framework can falsify or support depending on the data. A positive pilot result supports the specific prediction; a negative pilot result requires the framework to engage with the negative result honestly (the design prediction was wrong; the framework's commitment requires a specific response; the response is part of the pre-registration's pre-specified analysis plan).

The pilot is not the framework's empirical validation. The pilot is one empirical test of one specific design prediction; the framework's broader empirical-status claim requires multiple pilots across multiple specific design predictions, and the broader validation is a multi-year research-program scope rather than a single pilot's scope. The framework's commitment is to run the first pilot honestly, publish the pilot's findings whether they support the framework or not, and use the pilot's findings to scope the next pilot's design prediction selection. The discipline is iterative; the framework's empirical status updates with each pilot's findings; the framework's design-theory status is the baseline and the empirical status is what the pilots can move.

## Why pre-registration matters

Pre-registration is the discipline that converts framework-confidence into framework-testability. Without pre-registration, a positive empirical result confirms whatever the framework happened to claim (and the post-hoc claim can be tuned to match the result); without pre-registration, a negative empirical result is dismissed as a measurement issue (because the framework's claim was not specified precisely enough to rule out measurement-issue interpretations). With pre-registration, both directions of result inform the framework honestly; the positive result confirms the pre-specified prediction; the negative result requires the pre-specified response.

The discipline matters specifically for solo indie operators where the alternative to pre-registration is post-hoc framing that the operator's own optimism would absorb. The operator's optimism is real and operationally useful (the operator's commitment to the framework is what produces the design work that the framework runs on); the optimism is also a structural failure mode for empirical claims because the optimism biases post-hoc framing in the direction of the framework's commitments. Pre-registration is the discipline that prevents the optimism from biasing the empirical findings; the discipline is operational because the pre-registered hypotheses and analysis plans constrain what the post-hoc framing can claim.

The discipline matters specifically for procedural-character frameworks because the framework's design surface is multi-decade and the empirical validation timeline is necessarily slow. A framework whose validation is multi-year cannot rely on rapid iteration to catch claims that the empirical evidence does not support; the slow validation timeline means that claims propagate through years of derivative work before the empirical evidence catches them. Pre-registration provides the discipline that bounds claim-propagation; the framework's claims are pre-registered with the empirical anchor pathway; downstream framework work can reason over the pre-registered claims with the awareness that the anchor pathway is in motion rather than that the claims are already validated.

## The abstract Character A / Character B framing

The pilot's vignettes use abstract Character A and Character B framing per a specific operator-side scope discipline I am calling Item 3 closure. The framework's commercial product has a specific NPC roster that lives in the framework's operator-side canonical record; the roster includes characters with names, biographical profiles, demographic specifics, and narrative arcs that are part of the commercial product's substantive content. The roster is protected scope; the framework's soft-disclosure discipline keeps the roster-specific details out of public posts, public pilots, and any other public-facing surface.

The Character A and Character B framing satisfies the disclosure discipline at the public-pilot scope. The vignettes describe characters structurally (the structural properties the framework's architecture commits to; the procedural-descriptive shape that the framework's design predictions reference); the vignettes do not name specific commercial-product NPCs; the abstraction is at the descriptive-structure level rather than at the character-identity level. The pilot's findings are publishable because the vignettes are structural rather than identity-specific; the commercial product's NPC roster remains protected because the public pilot does not reference it.

The discipline is also a methodology demonstration. The framework's other research-output (architecture documents; framework-paper drafts; research blog posts) operates under the same disclosure discipline; the abstraction discipline at the pilot level shows that the framework's substantive content is structurally publishable without leaking commercial scope. The discipline matters for any framework that has both research-output and commercial-product output; the discipline's value is that it lets the research-output ship publicly while the commercial-product output stays scoped to commercial channels.

## What this changed in my workflow

Three specific changes. First, when the framework adopts a new architectural commitment, I now explicitly document what would constitute empirical evidence for or against the commitment before the framework's research arc closes the commitment as ratified. The discipline produces a pre-registration-ready specification at the time of commitment; the alternative is a commitment whose empirical-evidence pathway has to be reconstructed post-hoc, and the post-hoc reconstruction is the failure mode that pre-registration is designed to prevent. The discipline costs a few minutes per commitment; the benefit is that the commitment carries its own empirical pathway forward.

Second, when the framework's documentation makes an empirical-class claim, I now check whether the claim has a pre-registered empirical pathway or whether the claim is at design-theory status. The check produces explicit labeling in the documentation; claims with a pre-registered pathway are labeled with the pathway; claims at design-theory status are labeled as such. The labeling discipline catches drift between design-theory claims and empirical claims; the drift is a specific failure mode in iterative framework work where design-theory commitments accumulate empirical-claim framing as the framework's research arc continues, and the labeling catches the drift before it propagates downstream.

Third, when external commentary engages with the framework's claims, I now reframe the engagement to match the framework's actual empirical status. The discipline catches misframings that import empirical-validation claims the framework has not made; the reframing is operational discipline for the framework's accountability. The discipline is small (typically a paragraph of clarification per misframing); the discipline matters because misframings propagate in ways that the framework's later work has to engage with, and catching the misframing at the earliest stage reduces the propagation cost.

## What I am not claiming

I am not claiming the framework has been validated empirically. The framework is at design-theory status plus pre-registered pilot plan; the validated status would require the pilot to run, the pilot's findings to be published, and a subsequent multi-pilot research program to extend the validation across the framework's broader claims. The framework's empirical status is what the post documents honestly; the validated status is what the pilot pathway is moving toward but has not reached.

I am not claiming pre-registration eliminates empirical-claim failure modes. Pre-registration bounds the post-hoc-framing failure mode; the discipline does not bound the measurement-instrument failure mode (a poorly-designed measurement instrument produces noise regardless of pre-registration); the discipline does not bound the participant-recruitment failure mode (a participant pool that does not match the framework's target audience produces findings that do not generalize regardless of pre-registration); the discipline does not bound the analysis-plan failure mode (a pre-specified analysis plan that does not match the underlying statistical structure of the data produces wrong conclusions regardless of pre-registration). The framework's commitment to pre-registration is one component of empirical discipline, not a substitute for the broader empirical-research discipline that the open-science literature documents.

I am not claiming the Character A / Character B abstract framing is the framework's permanent commercial-disclosure scope. The framework's soft-disclosure rule has a revisit date (2026-11-04); the framework's commitment is to revisit the disclosure decision at the revisit date and to consider whether the framework's commercial profile has changed in ways that would change the disclosure decision. The current abstraction is operational discipline for the current scope; the abstraction is not a permanent commitment to keep NPC-roster specifics private.

I am not claiming the framework's design predictions are uniformly testable at near-term pilot scope. The pilot the post discusses tests one specific design prediction; the framework's broader design predictions (some of which are multi-decade scope; some of which involve cross-cultural reception dynamics; some of which involve production-scale performance) are not testable at near-term pilot scope and require multi-year multi-pilot research programs that the framework's commitment to validation extends over. The pilot is the first instance of the framework's empirical pathway; the broader pathway is what the framework is committing to over time, not what the pilot's near-term findings would close.

I am not claiming the framework's design-theory status is a weakness. Design theory is a recognized category of theoretical contribution in design-research literature (Cross 2007 *Designerly Ways of Knowing* is one canonical source); design-theory contributions can be substantive and valuable without being empirically validated in the experimental-research sense. The framework's design-theory status is honest scope; the empirical-validation pathway is the framework's commitment to moving beyond design-theory scope where the substantive claims warrant the empirical anchor. The two are complementary, not in tension; the framework's commitment is to both.

## What I am claiming

The framework is at design-theory status plus pre-registered pilot plan. The framework's claims are bounded by the empirical status; readers engaging with the framework's claims engage with them under the empirical status the framework actually has, not under the validated status the framework's earlier documents had implied. The pre-registration discipline is the bridge between framework-confidence and framework-testability; the discipline lets the framework's claims be tested honestly under conditions the framework has pre-specified, and the discipline lets both positive and negative results inform the framework's subsequent work.

If you are developing design-theory frameworks with empirical-class claims and you do not have a pre-registered empirical pathway for the claims, the absence is operationally consequential and the literature on the replication crisis has documented the consequences in detail. The discipline is not exotic; OSF templates are available; the discipline costs a few minutes per claim and produces explicit labeling in the framework's documentation that catches design-theory-versus-empirical drift before it propagates downstream. The discipline is what makes the framework accountable to the empirical evidence the framework's claims would require, and the accountability is what distinguishes design-theory contribution from design-theory speculation.

The first pilot is the first instance of the framework's empirical pathway; the pathway is what the framework is committing to over time, and the commitment is what this post puts on the table publicly.
