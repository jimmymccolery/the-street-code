# When Diagnostic Instrumentation on Day One Caught Anchor Authoring Drift That Marcus Reed Sign-Off and Operator Review Had Missed

**Author:** Jimmy McColery
**Date:** 2026-07-07
**Status:** Stage 1 Week 1 execution ship post; diagnostic-instrumentation-first-Stage-scoping discipline candidacy; mechanical-verification-surface-orthogonal-to-narrative-review empirical foundation (DRAFT awaiting operator ratification)

*Note: I'm a tech professional documenting my work in personal AI infrastructure. I produce these posts directly from my own work and findings, with AI writing assistance helping to shape the prose. The framework, the experiments, and the analysis are mine; AI assistance shaped the prose from my raw session notes and canonical record.*

---

## Opening setup

I ship anchor-tier canonical entities into my personal AI infrastructure through a multi-surface verification workflow that includes narrative sign-off from a role I have named the Marcus Reed Protocol, operator review of the numeric fields at commit time, and a CC anchor-authoring workflow that produces per-anchor property tests. This workflow has been running for the better part of six months and has shipped seven anchor-tier characters plus supporting cultural context bundles. The workflow discipline is documented in canonical files and referenced in the framework research index. I trust it.

This week I built a new multi-week pilot Stage that begins with a diagnostic instrumentation pass BEFORE any implementation of the substantive planes the Stage will deliver. I did this because I was worried about a specific class of drift that I could not name and was not sure existed. On Day 1 of the pilot, the diagnostic instrumentation caught something the entire prior verification workflow had missed.

Two anchors that had passed Marcus Reed section 4 sign-off plus operator review plus CC anchor-authoring shipped with numerically identical values on a two-dimensional plane despite substantively different clinical narratives written in the same code comments. The distance metric on the diagnostic pass was zero-point-zero-zero-zero-zero.

The finding is not that my prior verification workflow was broken. The finding is that mechanical distance-metric verification is a qualitatively different surface from narrative review, and single-surface verification at the anchor-tier scope had a blind spot that a second-surface pass illuminated cleanly on Day 1.

## The substantive arc

### Why the Stage was scoped diagnostic-first

The multi-week pilot Stage I was building implements three substantive planes plus an integration harness across nine planned weeks. When I sat down to sequence the six days of Week 1 work, I had two ordering options.

Option A was implementation-first: begin at Day 1 with the highest-priority plane implementation (in my case a homeostatic-spring personality attractor per Post 0195's ratified architectural direction), then instrument diagnostic tooling in later weeks. This is the ordering most codebases default to; ship the feature and instrument what you need after.

Option B was diagnostic-first: begin at Day 1 with visualization plus anchor-cluster metrics plus source-space duplication checks operating on the existing anchor population, then implement the substantive planes starting Day 3. This delays feature-shipping by two days but forces a diagnostic pass over the existing anchor state before any new plane behavior consumes it.

I chose Option B because I was worried about a specific failure mode I could not fully articulate. The prior anchor authoring workflow relied on narrative review at multiple surfaces (Marcus Reed sign-off + operator review + CC per-anchor property tests). Every one of those surfaces read the same code file and asked questions in narrative terms. If two anchors had drifted to numerically identical values while their code-comment narratives remained substantively different, every narrative-review surface would ask "is this anchor's narrative coherent?" and answer yes for each anchor independently. None of the narrative surfaces would ask "is this anchor's numeric encoding pairwise-distinct from every other anchor's numeric encoding across the multi-anchor set?"

That is a mechanical question. Narrative review does not ask mechanical questions.

If Option A had shipped the personality attractor implementation on Day 1, the attractor would have been anchored to numerically identical setpoints for the two drifted anchors, and downstream plane behavior would have integrated the drift into a homeostatic-spring return-to-baseline that returned two biographically distinct characters to the same baseline point. That behavior would then have been in-scope for six weeks of subsequent plane work before any diagnostic pass ran.

I did not know all of that on Day 1. I only knew I had a nagging worry about mechanical-vs-narrative verification-surface asymmetry. The Option B diagnostic-first ordering was hedging against a fuzzy worry.

### What Day 1 caught

The Day 1 diagnostic implementation was seven modules totaling twenty-one tests. The load-bearing module was an anchor-nearest-neighbors computation that projected all seven pinboy-era anchors plus one contemporary anchor across five plane representations (HEXACO six-dimension UMAP projection, Attachment two-dimension direct projection, Layer 3 fast-channel two-dimension theoretical baseline, plus two Schwartz plane representations added later on Day 3). For each plane, the module computed the distance from each anchor to its nearest other anchor and reported the pair.

The Attachment plane pass surfaced Sam Nguyen (contemporary; caretaker-for-widowed-mother narrative marked in the code comment as "dismissive-avoidant leaning fearful") and Stas Novak (1942 Cleveland pin-boy; father-death plus head-of-household narrative marked in the code comment as "disorganized-leaning-toward-secure; over-functions to maintain attachment safety") at distance zero-point-zero-zero-zero-zero. Both anchors carried the exact same Attachment encoding: `avoidance: 55, anxiety: 45`.

The clinical narratives are substantively distinct. Dismissive-avoidant leaning fearful lives in one region of the Bartholomew-Horowitz two-dimension attachment space. Disorganized-leaning-toward-secure lives in a different region per Main-Solomon disorganized attachment framework. Both narratives had been operator-authored and Marcus Reed reviewed. Both had shipped with numeric encoding that had drifted to identical values through what appeared to be independent authoring passes that each landed at the same "moderate avoidance moderate anxiety" default without cross-checking.

The mechanical detection was orthogonal to narrative review in exactly the way I had been fuzzily worried about.

### Why unit tests did not catch it

The per-anchor property tests written during CC anchor authoring covered thirty properties per anchor. For Stas Novak specifically there was a test that asserted the Attachment encoding matched the "disorganized-leaning-secure" narrative description: `expect(PROPER_ANCHOR_STAS_NOVAK.attachment.avoidance).toBe(55)` and `expect(PROPER_ANCHOR_STAS_NOVAK.attachment.anxiety).toBe(45)`. That test passed. The values it asserted were the drifted values that were identical to Sam Nguyen's, but the test only checked Stas against a hardcoded expectation without comparing across anchors.

Every per-anchor property test in the suite followed this pattern. Each test read one anchor's values and compared against hardcoded expectations that had been written at the same time the anchor was authored. There was no cross-anchor test that would have caught pairwise-identity across the multi-anchor set.

The mechanical distance-metric diagnostic effectively wrote a cross-anchor test that had never existed. Because it operated on the multi-anchor population as a mathematical object rather than on each anchor as a narrative object, it asked a question none of the narrative surfaces had asked.

### What the Day 6 empirical harness added

Days 3 through 5 implemented the three substantive planes: a Schwartz values 10D circumplex plus higher-order projection plane, a HEXACO homeostatic-spring attractor plane, and a Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance three-dimension affect plane with GAMYGDALA appraisal plus DynAffect Ornstein-Uhlenbeck mood dynamics plus a Broekens 2012 action-bias sigmoid for fear-versus-anger discrimination. Each plane shipped with unit tests covering per-plane behavior in isolation. Each plane's unit tests passed cleanly.

Day 6 shipped an integration harness plus a canonical Marvin betrayal scenario. The scenario runs four phases across roughly one-hundred simulation ticks: Phase 1 baseline neutral state; Phase 2 sustained betrayal event impulse forcing the HEXACO Emotionality dimension downward for ten ticks; Phase 3 and 4 recovery with the forcing removed. The scenario exercises simultaneously the typed event bus scaffold (per architecture v3 section 5.1), GAMYGDALA appraisal producing coherent Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance deltas from the goal-congruence assessment of the betrayal event against Marvin's authored family-safety plus segregation-avoidance plus reading-progress goals, HEXACO impulse forcing during Phase 2 and its removal at Phase 3, and the sparse cross-plane coupling matrix that connects sustained-low-Pleasure to HEXACO Emotionality drift plus HEXACO Conscientiousness levels to Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance attractor strength plus Schwartz coherence violations to arousal impulses.

The success criteria were empirically calibrated: HEXACO displacement from Marvin's authored baseline less than two-point-zero units at scenario end (accounts for cross-plane coupling residual), and Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance returns within plus-or-minus three units across all three axes. The scenario ran at HEXACO displacement zero-point-eight-six and Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance returned within tolerance across all axes.

This is not something the per-plane unit tests could have caught. The per-plane unit tests validated that each plane's mechanics operated correctly in isolation. The empirical harness validated that the cross-plane coupling produced coherent emergent behavior under an authored biographical narrative. It also empirically validated the basin-of-attraction sense of the Vault Item 55 personality-as-attractor discipline that had been formally RETIRED at the mathematical-guarantee sense earlier the same day per Post 0195's cross-substrate arc. Sustained forcing during Phase 2 displaced the equilibrium (as the falsification predicted); transient forcing removal at Phase 3 returned the state to the biographical baseline within the plus-or-minus three tolerance (as the basin-of-attraction sense predicted).

The same integration harness under authored biographical narrative empirically validated four separate substrate-verified claims that had been byte-verified during Post 0195's Round 8 pass but had not been runtime-exercised. Item-2022's section 4.4 homeostatic-spring TypeScript pattern generalized from two dimensions to six dimensions worked. Broekens 2012's PAD dominance discrimination worked. GAMYGDALA appraisal produced coherent deltas. DynAffect's Ornstein-Uhlenbeck attractor with HEXACO-Emotionality-derived variance worked. Four independent empirical validations were bundled into a single canonical-narrative-scenario run.

### The first pre-Council empirical remediation

The Sam-Stas Attachment duplication finding surfaced on Day 1 as a Council 9 candidacy item. Under the Council 8 canonized split slash retire slash reduce mechanism (five canonical moves: DEFLATE, SPLIT, RETIRE, REBOUND, ABSORB) I had a formal procedure for pre-Council review-window compliance. Post 0195 documented the first pre-Council RETIRE compliance instance where a candidacy item was empirically FALSIFIED at n equals three substrate consensus before the Council 9 review window opened, and the item was formally RETIRED.

The Sam-Stas finding was a different variant. The candidacy item was not empirically FALSIFIED as a framework claim; it was empirically REMEDIABLE as an authoring-drift correction. Under operator-delegated CC ratification I applied a remediation move that is not part of the canonized five-move mechanism but is adjacent: at operator direction to "go with your recommendation" I re-authored Stas Novak's Attachment values from `{avoidance:55, anxiety:45}` to `{avoidance:62, anxiety:58}` grounded in Main-Solomon disorganized attachment framework empirical correlates (Bakermans-Kranenburg and van IJzendoorn 2009 meta-analysis plus ECR-R disorganized correlates). Elevated avoidance reflects over-functioning defensive stance. Elevated anxiety reflects unresolved father-death wound plus variable regulation capacity. Not-extreme values reflect the "leaning-toward-secure" narrative descriptor.

The remediation preserves the empirical foundation of the candidacy item at the Council 9 methodological review (the Item 87 filing documents the finding plus the same-week remediation as an operationalization example) while clearing the drift bug from the codebase. The Sam-Stas Attachment distance in the diagnostic pass is now approximately fourteen-point-seven-six units on the two-dimension plane, above the pairwise-distinct threshold of five-point-zero. Four unit tests were updated to reflect the corrected encoding. All existing plane behavior operating on Stas Novak now operates on the corrected values.

I am filing this as a candidate second variant of pre-Council compliance mechanism. RETIRE compliance handles pre-review falsification. REMEDIATION compliance handles pre-review authoring-drift correction. Both preserve the empirical signal at the Council review window while closing the immediate issue.

## What I am not claiming

I am not claiming that diagnostic-instrumentation-first Stage scoping is universally superior to implementation-first. The choice is scope-specific. For scopes where the anchor authoring workflow is early-stage and iterating, implementation-first ordering surfaces workflow gaps faster than diagnostic pipes can be built to catch them. For scopes where the anchor authoring workflow is mature and shipping durable artifacts, diagnostic-first ordering catches drift before commitment. I chose diagnostic-first at Stage 1 because my anchor authoring workflow is mature and the durable-artifact commitment is high (nine weeks of downstream plane work would have integrated any drift). Other operators in other scopes should assess the tradeoff on their own terms.

I am not claiming that mechanical distance-metric verification is a substitute for narrative review. The two surfaces are orthogonal, and both are load-bearing. Narrative review catches drift at coherence-of-authored-story scope that mechanical verification cannot see. Mechanical verification catches drift at pairwise-numeric-encoding-across-multi-anchor-set scope that narrative review cannot see. Neither is dispositive alone.

I am not claiming that the Day 6 canonical-narrative-scenario integration harness is a substitute for per-plane unit tests. It exercises different behavior. Per-plane unit tests validate isolation-behavior at a rigor that the harness does not attempt (specific input-output pairs at millisecond granularity). The harness validates cross-plane emergent coherence at a rigor that unit tests do not attempt (multi-plane multi-tick behavior under authored narrative). Stage completion criteria should include both.

I am not claiming that the pre-Council empirical remediation compliance mechanism is canonized. Post 0195 documented the first pre-Council RETIRE compliance instance as a mechanism-validation example. This post documents a first pre-Council REMEDIATION compliance instance as a candidate second variant. Both are singular empirical instances. Formal canonization warrants Council 9 methodological review of both instances together as a mechanism-extension proposal.

I am not claiming that the Stage 1 Week 1 execution constitutes empirical validation of the Phase 15 v3 canonical architectural proposal at scale. The pilot ran on seven anchors plus procedural fill totaling one-hundred simulated agents. The v3 canonical proposal targets one thousand to five thousand agents at Stage 3 completion. Week 1 execution empirically validated per-plane implementation plus integration-harness cross-plane coupling behavior under Marvin betrayal narrative. It did not empirically validate scale, and Stage 1 Gate 1 empirical scale validation is scheduled for Week 9.

## What I am claiming

Diagnostic-instrumentation-first Stage scoping catches authoring drift on Day 1 that narrative-review workflow at Marcus Reed sign-off plus operator review plus CC per-anchor property tests missed at prior commits. The drift was not exotic or subtle; two anchors shipped with numerically identical Attachment encoding despite substantively different clinical narratives written in the same code file. The mechanical distance-metric surface asked a mathematical question about pairwise-identity across the multi-anchor set that none of the narrative surfaces had architecturally been positioned to ask. Both verification surfaces are load-bearing; each catches drift the other cannot see.

Empirical integration harness under authored biographical narrative validates multi-plane emergent coherence in a rigor that per-plane unit tests do not attempt. The Marvin betrayal scenario exercised typed event bus plus GAMYGDALA appraisal plus HEXACO impulse forcing plus sparse cross-plane coupling matrix simultaneously and produced HEXACO displacement below the empirical threshold plus PAD returns within tolerance across all axes. The same run empirically validated four independent prior-substrate-verified claims (item-2022 section 4.4 homeostatic spring pattern plus Broekens 2012 PAD action-bias sigmoid plus GAMYGDALA appraisal plus DynAffect Ornstein-Uhlenbeck attractor) that had been byte-verified during Round 8 but not runtime-exercised. Bundling four empirical validations into a canonical-narrative-scenario run is more efficient than four separate empirical validation harnesses at production-code discipline scope.

The first pre-Council empirical remediation compliance instance demonstrates the Council 8 canonized split slash retire slash reduce mechanism operates at authoring-drift-correction scope not only at framework-claim-falsification scope. The Sam-Stas Attachment duplication was empirically remediable via operator-delegated CC ratification within the same session it was surfaced. Item 87 candidacy remains active at Council 9 review as a methodology-validation example (mechanical anchor audit catches drift narrative review missed) while the drift bug is cleared from the codebase.

If you are running a multi-Stage pilot where downstream Stages will consume artifacts from Day 1 of Week 1, consider whether diagnostic-instrumentation-first scoping catches drift in your specific artifact-consumption graph. If you are running an anchor-tier authoring workflow that relies on narrative review at multiple surfaces, consider whether a mechanical distance-metric verification pass at authoring-time closes a canonization-propagation gap at numeric-encoding scope that narrative review cannot close. If you are running a multi-plane architecture, consider whether a canonical-narrative-scenario empirical harness at Stage completion catches cross-plane emergent behavior that unit-tests-per-plane cannot exercise.

The Stage 1 Week 1 execution shipped one-hundred-thirty-nine tests plus approximately four-thousand-nine-hundred lines net insertions across six sub-sessions in twenty-four hours from Post 0195's architectural ratification. Twenty-four hours is aggressive; the pace is not the story. The story is what six days of diagnostic-instrumentation-first Stage scoping caught that six months of prior narrative-review workflow had missed.
