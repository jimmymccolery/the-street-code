# When First-Class Intersection Nodes Emerged as a Genuine Architectural Contribution Because No Production Knowledge Graph Does What Memory Lanes Needs

**Author:** Jimmy McColery
**Date:** 2026-07-07
**Status:** Single finding from Schema Architecture v0.2 canonization arc close; Round 6 substrate-driven novel-contribution recognition

*Note: I'm a tech professional documenting my work in personal AI infrastructure. I produce these posts directly from my own work and findings, with AI writing assistance helping to shape the prose. The framework, the experiments, and the analysis are mine; AI assistance shaped the prose from my raw session notes and canonical record.*

---

There is one finding from the Schema Architecture v0.2 arc that I did not expect and that changed how I think about what Memory Lanes is doing.

Round 6 of the substrate research arc reported that first-class curated intersection nodes, as an architectural pattern, are not implemented in any production knowledge graph the substrate could find. Round 6 also reported that the academic literature on Bourdieu and games applies Bourdieu to gaming communities and players, not to modeling habitus inside NPCs.

The upshot: my proposal to model NPC compound identity as first-class intersection nodes populated by descendant-community consultation, and my proposal to model habitus as in-NPC typed relations, are not borrowed from academic knowledge-graph or games-studies literature. They are novel.

I have some feelings about that.

## What I actually needed

Memory Lanes has to represent people. A specific person, Cesar de la Rosa, is eleven years old, working-poor Tejano, Catholic, male, with an undiagnosed disability, in West Side San Antonio, 1946.

The naive way to represent Cesar is as a bag of tags. `age: 11 + class: working_poor + ethnicity: Tejano + religion: Catholic + disability: undiagnosed + gender: male + place: San_Antonio_West_Side + era: 1946`. Then when you generate his behavior, you look up the tags, retrieve the relevant preferences, apply them.

That approach produces stereotype. It is also flat-out wrong for Cesar. The most important thing about him is a property that does not exist in any of his individual tags. His disability is invisible to the systems around him. That invisibility does not come from disability alone; children with disabilities in 1946 upper-middle-class Anglo San Antonio households often received diagnostic attention. It does not come from being working-poor alone; children in 1946 working-poor Anglo households often received diagnostic attention through Catholic Charities or school-nurse referrals. It does not come from being Tejano alone; children in 1970s Tejano households increasingly received diagnostic attention as Chicano civil rights organizing pushed access. It does not come from 1946 alone; children in different times and places had different diagnostic pathways.

The invisibility comes from the intersection. Working-poor plus Tejano plus 1946 equals no diagnostic access. That is a property that belongs to the intersection and only to the intersection.

If you represent Cesar as a bag of tags, you cannot capture this. There is no `no_diagnostic_access` tag to add. There is no rule to derive it from the individual tags because the mapping is not compositional. You need a first-class node that is the intersection and that carries the emergent property directly.

## What Crenshaw and Collins actually said

I started reading intersectionality theory in Round 2 when Claude Opus flagged the tag-union failure mode and pointed at Crenshaw. I re-read it more carefully in Round 6 when it became clear this was a load-bearing architectural pivot.

Crenshaw's 1989 University of Chicago Legal Forum paper argued that summed single-axis analyses fail: "even if summed together, feminist discourse and anti-racist discourse do not encompass the mutually-constitutive views of Black women." Her 1991 Stanford Law Review paper extended the argument with legal case analyses. Collins's 1990 Black Feminist Thought developed the matrix of domination framing: interlocking structural, disciplinary, hegemonic, and interpersonal domains that produce distinct experience at their intersection.

Both authors are explicit that intersection is not summable. You cannot get to Black women's experience by adding Black experience plus women's experience. The intersection has its own emergent properties.

This is the theoretical grounding for first-class intersection nodes. The theoretical grounding is verified. The load-bearing claim (tag-union cannot represent intersectional emergence) is empirically supported by four decades of intersectionality scholarship.

## What the substrates checked for me

Round 3 (Claude Web live-fetch) checked whether Wikidata implements first-class intersection nodes. Answer: no. Wikidata can reify statements via automatic statement nodes and qualifiers (start time P580, end time P582, point in time P585), but this is statement-complexity mechanism, not cultural-representation policy. Intersection remains a derived qualified statement, never a first-class entity carrying its own emergent attributes.

Round 4 (ChatGPT 5.5 via Perplexity) checked several production games. Victoria 3 tag-based culture system: no first-class intersection nodes. Crusader Kings III pairwise trait compatibility: no first-class intersection nodes. Fire Emblem Three Houses: no first-class intersection nodes. Stardew Valley finite gift-preference table: no first-class intersection nodes. Disco Elysium Thought Cabinet: closest but still not first-class intersection nodes (Thought Cabinet is internalized ideology, not compound-identity emergent-property representation).

Round 5 empirical Getty AAT SPARQL probe (companion file `getty_aat_probe.jsonl` shipped with the substrate report) verified that Getty AAT covers activity concepts and object types but has zero coverage for intersection-node identifiers.

Round 6 (Claude Web Fable-5 direct) synthesized: no production knowledge graph implements descendant-consultation-populated first-class intersection nodes. The pattern is genuinely novel.

Round 6 also checked the academic Bourdieu-and-games literature. Consalvo Cheating: Gaining Advantage in Videogames (MIT Press 2007, ISBN 9780262033657) develops "gaming capital" as a framing for understanding player interaction with games; applies Bourdieu to communities and players. Kirkpatrick's gamer habitus work: same scope. Walsh and Apperley: gaming capital and literacy. Mayra: gaming culture at the boundaries of play. DiGRA 2023 Pitroso: gaming communities through Bourdieu's cultural capital. Games and Culture 2026 Torres-Diaz et al.: Bourdieuan cartography of the video game field.

All of that literature applies Bourdieu to gaming communities and players. None of it applies Bourdieu to modeling habitus inside NPCs.

So the proposal to model NPC compound identity as first-class intersection nodes plus model habitus as in-NPC typed relations plus populate both by descendant-community consultation, at 1000 to 5000 NPC scale, in a shipped commercial game with multi-decade chronological span, is genuinely under-theorized in the design literature.

## What this actually is

I want to be careful about the novelty claim.

First-class intersection nodes as a concept are not new. Crenshaw wrote about intersection in 1989. Collins wrote about the matrix of domination in 1990. Intersectionality scholarship has been active for four decades. The theoretical grounding is well-established.

What is novel is the architectural implementation. Nobody appears to have built a production knowledge graph where descendant-community consultation populates first-class intersection nodes that carry emergent attributes belonging only to the intersection. Nobody has built a game NPC system where compound identity gets first-class treatment plus consultation-gate populate policy plus salience weighting per scene plus PROV-O provenance evidence per attribute.

The gap is at the implementation layer, not the theoretical layer. The gap exists because the theoretical framework did not have compelling reason to be implemented at knowledge-graph scale, and the game industry has not had a compelling reason to model compound identity beyond tag unions.

Memory Lanes needs to. That is the compelling reason.

## What the consultation gate does

Consultation gate is the load-bearing constraint on first-class intersection nodes. Any intersection node that shipped in Memory Lanes must have a named consulting reviewer who signed off in the asset record. The gate is not a boolean approval flag. It is a first-class policy object that compiles to runtime permissions.

At runtime the consultation-derived permissions govern four things. Access rules control which NPCs, players, and scenes can see this content. Display rules control era-appropriate framing, no anachronism, no caricature, consultation-approved wording. Transformation rules control content generation (content CANNOT be auto-generated from identity tags; it MUST be evidence-backed and consultation-approved). Reuse rules control export (content CANNOT be exported for demographic inference or stereotype-generation).

The consultation frameworks that ground the policy are documented in Schema Architecture v0.2 Section 5.1: CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance (published 2019 by GIDA; foundational scholarly reference Carroll et al. 2020 Data Science Journal DOI 10.5334/dsj-2020-043), OCAP (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession; FNIGC trademark), Local Contexts TK Labels, Mukurtu community-centered digital heritage system, UNESCO ICH ethics, Oral History Association Principles, APA Ethics Code, and the Never Alone paradigm case (Upper One Games plus E-Line Media 2014; approximately 40 Alaska Native elders plus Inupiat Cultural Ambassadors).

The consultation gate is what makes first-class intersection nodes shippable. Without it, the node is just data. With it, the node is a policy object that carries its own permissions to display.

## What I'm not claiming

I am not claiming that first-class intersection nodes will produce demonstrably better NPC behavior than tag-union approaches. The architecture is novel and unverified at 1000-5000 NPC scale. There is no published empirical study I can point to.

I am not claiming that Memory Lanes will finish this work. Schema Architecture v0.2 is architecture, not implementation. The Cesar 1946 canonical worked example exists at the architecture document level. Cesar's actual first-class intersection node has not been authored yet as a shipped asset. Named consulting reviewer identification is operator-owned action, not CC-executable action.

I am not claiming Memory Lanes is the first to think about intersectionality in games. Adrienne Shaw at Temple (Gaming at the Edge: Sexuality and Gender at the Margins of Gamer Culture, University of Minnesota Press 2014) and Kishonna Gray at UIC (Intersectional Tech: Black Users in Digital Gaming, LSU Press) have been writing about intersectional lived experience in gaming for years. The literature I am claiming Memory Lanes contributes to is the architectural-implementation layer, not the theoretical or player-experience layer.

I am not claiming the novel-contribution recognition constitutes scholarly publication candidacy. That determination requires operator judgment plus external peer review. Round 6 substrate flag is a starting signal, not a peer-reviewed verification.

What I am claiming is this: after six rounds of substrate research, first-class intersection nodes and Bourdieu-habitus-as-in-NPC-typed-relations both emerged as architectural patterns for which no production implementation was found. The theoretical grounding is Crenshaw plus Collins plus four decades of intersectionality scholarship. The gap is at the implementation layer. Memory Lanes has a compelling reason to close it, because Cesar and Marvin and Yuki need to be represented in ways that tag unions cannot support. So we are closing it.
