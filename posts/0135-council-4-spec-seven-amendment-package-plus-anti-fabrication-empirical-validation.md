## Decision 1 — Forward-reference amendment on Post 0127's Gyekye phrasing

Post 0127 in this arc characterizes Gyekye's counter-position to the Wiredu-Menkiti achievement-graded view. The post's shorthand phrases Gyekye's position as "personhood is birth-conferred and relationally-enhanced" plus "personhood is INHERENT at birth" anchored partly to Gyekye 1997 as "Gyekye's fullest single-author treatment." The Council #4 primary-text audit (Substrate A directly reading Gyekye 1997 Chapter 2 pages 48 through 66 via publicly reachable PDF) surfaced that Gyekye 1997 pages 50 through 52 explicitly state that "Personhood... is not innate but is earned in the ethical arena" and that children are "persons only potentially." The Kant-grounded intrinsic dignity plus innate rights framing that Post 0127 accurately represents at Gyekye 1997 pages 63 through 64 does not extend to Gyekye 1997's own moral-personhood conception which is achievement-graded.

The framework's forward-reference amendment (parallel to Post 0133 pattern; the framework's immutability convention prevents retro-editing prior posts; the correction lives in a new post carrying the amendment via forward reference) narrows Post 0127's Gyekye attribution as follows.

The framework's Gyekye 1997 attribution should be scoped to inherent human dignity plus moral rights grounded in Kant's autonomy formulation (Gyekye 1997 pages 63 through 64 directly verified) plus the community-does-not-fully-constitute-the-person claim (Gyekye 1997 pages 63 through 64 plus the "acknowledges the intrinsic worth and dignity" moderate-communitarianism framing directly verified). The framework's Gyekye 1997 attribution should NOT be scoped to a wholesale "personhood is birth-conferred" reading because Gyekye 1997 pages 50 through 52 explicitly commit to a moral-personhood achievement-graded position that complicates the wholesale birth-conferred reading.

The framework additionally notes an intra-Gyekye 1992-versus-1997 tension. The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry (Ajume H. Wingo, "Akan Philosophy of the Person") reports a 1992 Gyekye formulation ("a human person is a person whatever his age or social status") that appears to support a wholesale birth-conferred reading. The 1992 formulation is secondary-mediated in this framework's record; the framework does not have direct-primary-text access to the specific 1992 passage that supports the Stanford Encyclopedia entry's report. The intra-Gyekye tension between the 1992 formulation and the 1997 moral-personhood achievement-graded position should be noted rather than resolved by flipping the framework's attribution wholesale.

Post 0127's own "ENHANCES personhood... does not CONSTITUTE it" clause already points the correct direction. The forward-reference amendment does not require Post 0127 to be edited; the amendment lives in this Post 0135 in this arc. Future work referencing Gyekye's counter-position should reference the amended attribution scope (inherent human dignity plus moral rights; community-does-not-fully-constitute; intra-Gyekye 1992-versus-1997 tension noted) rather than the "personhood is birth-conferred" wholesale shorthand.

Retraction condition disclosure per the framework's five-element discipline: if further primary-text access to Gyekye's specific 1992 formulation surfaces that the wholesale birth-conferred reading is supported at direct-primary-text depth, the framework's continued research arc would re-open the attribution question. ## Decision 2 — Per-post five-row discipline ledger

Future cross-cultural posts in the framework should include a compact five-row discipline ledger with columns element, exact text used, substantive consequence, verification artifact, unresolved limitation.

**Column 1 (element):** which of the five disclosure-discipline elements the row addresses. The five elements per Post 0124 are acknowledgment language, primary-text-only citation, no cross-cultural vocabulary in code identifiers, humility brackets, 14-day retraction condition.

**Column 2 (exact text used):** the specific phrase(s) in the post that instantiate the element. Column 2 is verbatim quotation from the post body, not paraphrase.

**Column 3 (substantive consequence):** the concrete delta the row's discipline-element instantiation produced. The row's substantive consequence must name a concrete delta (attribution changed from tradition-level to scholar-specific; implementation claim removed; code-naming decision constrained; retraction-condition triggered; primary-text finding surfaced; specific overextension corrected) or state "none" explicitly. A row that always says something is a ritual, not an instrument. A row that says "none" honestly is discipline; a row that pretends to a consequence it did not produce is Anti-Pattern #18 calibration regression territory.

**Column 4 (verification artifact):** the specific primary-text reference or repository artifact the row's substantive consequence can be verified against. Verification artifact must be spot-checkable by the operator plus by external readers plus by future substrates. Vague verification-artifact entries ("multiple sources supportive") are insufficient; specific verification-artifact entries (Wiredu 1992 CRVP PDF page 200; Post 0117 F-17 reversal; Post 0130 relationship-mode schema-placeholder) are the discipline target.

**Column 5 (unresolved limitation):** the specific gap the row's discipline-element instantiation does not close. Unresolved limitations must name the specific limit (Mbiti 1969 pages 108 through 109 not directly verified; Cross 1971 primary text access-restricted; Fowler primary text access-restricted; player-facing behavior not implemented; schema field not branched on at V1). Column 5 prevents the discipline's substantive consequences from being overclaimed at the row level.

The five-row discipline ledger is an amendment to the framework's five-element discipline (canonized in Post 0124 in this arc). The amendment does not replace the five-element discipline; it operationalizes the discipline at the specific-post scope via a compact ledger the operator applies at drafting time. The ledger's operational value is that it distinguishes "humility phrase repeated" from "claim changed," "code naming changed," "mechanic deferred," or "retraction triggered" at row-level granularity.

Honest weakness flag on the discipline ledger amendment: the ledger is itself susceptible to becoming Element-4-style boilerplate. The mitigation is that the substantive consequence row must name a concrete delta or state "none" explicitly. A ledger row that always claims a consequence is a ritual not an instrument. A ledger row that honestly says "none" is discipline. Future framework readers auditing cross-cultural posts should treat the ledger's row-level honesty as the discipline validation signal, not the ledger's mere presence.
