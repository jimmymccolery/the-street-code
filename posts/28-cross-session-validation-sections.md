**Status:** Self-experimentation by a non-credentialed practitioner. n=1 operator deploying a three-substrate Council architecture for a single 2026-05-10 architecture-revision session arc. The section convention described below is empirically established at n=2 deployments within this single arc; cross-arc replication is unverified at filing time. ## Opening

The 2026-05-10 Master Setup between-sessions cleanup arc architecture-revision session deployed three canonization decisions via the Council of Three substrate-class-distance pattern. Decision 1A canonized drafting-drift as a separate anti-pattern row. Decision 1B canonized operational discipline rules as a first-class canonical artifact via a new file. The recommendation came as halt-and-flag rather than as content the deployment package asked the substrates to produce. The convergence was emergent. Both canonical artifacts (`operational_discipline_rules.canonical.md` and `operator_role_archetypes.md`) gained Cross-Session Validation sections as part of their ratification acts; both sections share structural shape; both are open-ended (future sessions append) per `master-setup/research_queue.md` Topic 36 (line 1419) canonization framing.

This post is the second post in Bundle F on the 2026-05-10 architecture-revision session arc methodology research, following [Post 27](27-council-of-three-outcome-patterns.md) on the three Council outcome patterns from the same arc. ## What the section convention doesn't establish

The convention is empirically established at n=2 deployments within a single architecture-revision session arc. Cross-arc replication is unverified at filing time. Future architecture-revision sessions producing canonical artifact promotions may continue the convention, may diverge from it, or may produce hybrid forms that extend the section structure. Per `research_queue.md` Topic 36 Trigger conditions at line 1451: "Third Cross-Session Validation section deployment at future architecture-revision session (n=3 cross-arc evidence substantively meets pattern-recurrence threshold beyond single-arc observation)."

The convention does not claim that Cross-Session Validation sections are the only way to address cross-session validation gaps in canonical artifacts. It claims that this is the way the substrates and the operator addressed the gap at Decision 1B and Decision 1C deployments. Other canonical artifact promotion decisions at other arcs may address the same gap differently, or may produce canonical artifacts whose empirical foundations don't have the same gap to address in the first place.

The convention does not establish that the substrate halt-and-flag → section addition pattern is substrate-class-independent in the Topic 22 sense. The pattern's signal is at structural scope rather than per-element scope; the per-element convergence pattern that Decision 1C exhibited (and that [Post 27](27-council-of-three-outcome-patterns.md) treats) is a stronger signal for Topic 22 purposes. Topic 36's connection to Topic 22 supplies adjacent empirical evidence stream, not falsification evidence.

The convention does not close the convention extension question. The question is open at canonical scope per `research_queue.md` Topic 36 line 1434; operational trigger awaits.

## What the section convention does establish

What the convention does establish is canonical record for a Council-of-Three deployment innovation that emerged from substrate halt-and-flag at two consecutive canonical artifact promotion decisions within a single architecture-revision session arc. The convention is named, dated, and canonized at `research_queue.md` Topic 36 (line 1419) per Type 2 reflection Observation 2 promotion to Topic-level handling at architecture-revision session arc closure.

The convention also establishes structural scaffolding at canonical artifact scope. Both `operational_discipline_rules.canonical.md` and `operator_role_archetypes.md` now carry sections that accumulate multi-session evidence as the framework operates over time. Each future architecture-revision session that exercises one of these rules or archetypes can add an entry documenting the empirical evidence; the cumulative evidence stream becomes available for cross-section synthesis at framework operational maturity. The canonical-record-discipline applied to historical narratives at anti-pattern #10 governs entry preservation: entries are not retroactively edited.

The convention also establishes a deployment-overhead reduction path for future Council deployments at canonical artifact promotion scope. The first deployment that surfaced the cross-session validation gap required substrate-side methodology research observation at halt-and-flag scope; the second deployment at the same architecture-revision session could draw on the precedent. Future deployments can recommend section addition as standard per established convention rather than re-surfacing the gap. The convention establishes the reduced-deliberation-overhead path that pattern recognition at Council outcome scope is supposed to enable per [Post 27](27-council-of-three-outcome-patterns.md)'s operator-judgment scaffolding framing.

The Council of Three architecture commitment at `master-setup/council_of_three_architecture_decision_2026-05-07.md` rests on substrate-class-distance as the criterion for verification independence. The Cross-Session Validation section convention is one of the empirical artifacts of that architecture operating across the 2026-05-10 architecture-revision session arc. The convention predates this post — it emerged from the substrates' halt-and-flag recommendations at Decision 1B and Decision 1C deployment, was deployed in two canonical artifacts at arc apply work, and was canonized at Topic 36 at arc closure. What this post does is position the convention relative to the broader canonical record so that future architecture-revision sessions can extend it, refine it, or apply it as standard convention rather than reinventing it.
