I deployed five substrates against one research question and the verdicts did not agree. The disagreement was the finding.

The research question was a specific philosophical-architectural question about how to structure simulated character cognition in a procedural-generation context. The question's content is not the point of this post; the point is that the question was sufficiently complex that no single substrate's processing produced a complete or correct answer. Each substrate's strengths produced specific contributions; each substrate's blind spots produced specific omissions or errors; the cross-substrate cumulative output was richer than any single substrate's output; the orchestrator-class synthesis that adjudicated the cumulative output was where the load-bearing verdict actually got made.

This post is the methodology meta. The five substrates and the sixth synthesizer are documented at the role level (each role does what its style does best); the specific substrate-routing decisions are soft-disclosed per the framework's disclosure rule (the directional principles are publishable; the implementation specifics stay out of public posts). The methodology lessons that sharpened across the arc's closing rounds are the substantive contribution of the post.

## The five substrates and their roles

The substrate-role pattern in the arc was: a research-rigor-with-citations substrate for academic-citation rigor; a citation-verification substrate for catching citation hallucinations; a generative novel-surfacing substrate for surfacing content the citation-rigor substrates would not surface; a primary-text-agentic-browsing substrate for source-verification work that the in-training substrates cannot perform reliably; and an operationalization-depth substrate for converting research findings into implementation-ready architectural content. The five roles are documented in the framework's Rule 11 multi-substrate research workflow; the specific substrate-to-role assignments are soft-disclosed per Approach 2 of the disclosure rule.

The roles are not interchangeable. The research-rigor substrate produces citation candidates from its training-data distribution; the citation-verification substrate hits the same citations against web search and primary sources; the convergence-or-divergence between the two substrates is the signal that drives the citation-rigor verdict. The generative novel-surfacing substrate produces angles the citation-rigor substrates would not have surfaced because the novel substrate is not anchored to peer-reviewed-citation defaults; the angles surface candidate research surfaces that the citation-rigor substrates can then verify or reject. The primary-text-agentic-browsing substrate runs against actual source documents and produces verbatim-quote-grounded findings that the in-training substrates cannot match because the in-training substrates do not have current source-document access. The operationalization-depth substrate converts the upstream substrates' research output into implementation-ready content that the operator can integrate into the architectural design document.

Each role's output is partial. The research-rigor substrate produces a citation set with high-quality anchors but limited novel-surfacing; the novel-surfacing substrate produces interesting candidate angles with limited citation rigor; the primary-text substrate produces verbatim-grounded findings with limited integration into the broader architectural context; the operationalization substrate produces architectural-ready content with limited independent verification work. The substrate set's aggregate output covers the surface in ways no single substrate covers.

## Where the substrates agreed and where they disagreed

Across the arc, the substrates agreed on some specific findings and disagreed on others. The agreements were useful signals; the disagreements were the load-bearing data.

The agreements covered cases where the citation chain was robust enough that any capable substrate could surface it from its training distribution. A specific cross-substrate agreement in the arc was a publication-status confirmation on a load-bearing citation: the primary-text-agentic-browsing substrate verified the publication status against the journal's current website; the operationalization-depth substrate cited the same publication status from its training distribution; the cross-substrate convergence was independent verification across two distinct access patterns. The convergence-as-signal pattern is documented in earlier posts as a useful weak signal; the pattern was useful in this arc for closing publication-status questions where multiple substrates could verify against different sources.

The disagreements covered cases where the substrates had different primary-text access, different training-distribution defaults, or different reasoning patterns. A specific disagreement in the arc was on a load-bearing identifier mismatch: the generative novel-surfacing substrate had cited a specific paper identifier from its training distribution; the primary-text-agentic-browsing substrate's read of the actual paper produced a different identifier; the two identifiers were similar enough that the surface-level read did not catch the mismatch but were different enough that the paper they referenced was not the same paper. The disagreement was the signal that triggered the operator-side and orchestrator-side primary-text verification that resolved the mismatch and produced the canonical identifier the framework adopted.

The disagreement on the identifier mismatch was structurally informative beyond the specific identifier. The pattern (substrate citing from training distribution; primary-text substrate citing from current source; disagreement triggering verification) became a discipline for handling load-bearing identifiers throughout the arc. The discipline is now part of the framework's citation methodology: any load-bearing identifier must be primary-text-verified before the framework adopts it, and substrate-only citation is acceptable only for non-load-bearing references where the identifier's correctness does not affect downstream architectural commitments.

## The orchestrator's role in adjudicating the disagreements

The sixth substrate in the arc was an orchestrator-class Madisonian Gate substrate (Claude Web with project-knowledge access to the framework's canonical state). The orchestrator's role was not to extend the substrate analyses or to add new content; the role was to adjudicate the prior substrates' verdicts against each other and against canonical primary-text evidence.

The orchestrator's adjudication produced two specific orchestrator-class catches in the closing rounds of the arc that majority-vote synthesis across the prior substrates would have missed. The first catch was a disambiguation of three orthogonal axes that the substrates had conflated to varying degrees: an inference-scheme axis, a temporal-horizon axis, and a self-experience-continuity axis. Each substrate's analysis had treated one or two of these as identifiable; the orchestrator's read of the framework's canonical citations identified that the three axes are orthogonal in the primary source and that the conflation was producing architectural confusion in the framework's specification. The catch was the framework's most consequential structural disambiguation in the closing rounds.

The second catch was a retraction of a prior-round flag. Round 7 had ratified a flag against a load-bearing cross-cultural claim because one substrate had asserted the claim without primary-text grounding and another substrate had not read the relevant chapter. Round 8's primary-text-agentic-browsing substrate ran an independent re-read of the chapter and produced quotations that supported the original claim directly; the orchestrator's adjudication at Round 8 sided with the primary-text-substrate's verdict against the prior-round substrate's verdict, and the flag was retracted. The retraction reversed the framework's stance on the cross-cultural claim and required a correction-footnote cascade through the affected documents.

Both catches share a structural shape: the orchestrator's read of primary-text evidence against substrate verdicts produced verdict reversals that the substrate-consensus framing would not have produced. Substrate convergence is not authoritative; primary-text evidence is authoritative when it exists and when the orchestrator-class layer has the discipline to read it. The orchestrator's adjudication discipline is the operational pattern that lets the framework be precise about which substrate verdicts hold and which substrate verdicts get reversed by primary-text re-read.

## The structural lessons that sharpened across the closing rounds

Two methodology lessons sharpened across the arc's closing rounds. The first is Rule 19 (substrate self-attestation unenforceable). The rule was canonized earlier in the framework's history; the rule has now fired at n=5 cumulative across Rounds 7 and 8. The fires include substrate self-attestations under explicit attestation-prompt framing that subsequent rounds reversed by independent primary-text verification.

A specific fire in Round 8 illustrates the rule. One substrate attested explicitly that it had verified a specific claim against primary text; the attestation was confident; the substrate's response framing was that the verification had been performed. A subsequent substrate ran an independent primary-text read of the same source and produced a different verdict. The orchestrator's adjudication had to choose between the two substrates' verdicts; the choice (correctly) went to the substrate whose primary-text read could be checked against the source rather than to the substrate whose attestation was unenforceable. The fire is one instance among five cumulative; the cumulative pattern is sufficient to argue that substrate self-attestations are unenforceable as a rule and that the orchestrator-class re-read discipline is the operational pattern that handles the unenforceability.

The second lesson sharpened from "longer plus more confident substrate output is not more authoritative" to a more specific framing: even when three of four substrates converge on a verdict, the orchestrator-class re-read of primary text can reverse the verdict. The arc's Round 8 cross-cultural-claim reversal is the working example. Three of the prior-round substrates had converged on the flag-the-claim verdict; one substrate had produced a chapter-non-read; the orchestrator's primary-text re-read at Round 8 reversed all of these and ratified the original claim. The reversal required the orchestrator to weight primary-text evidence above three-of-four substrate convergence; the weighting is the methodology commitment that the framework's verdict-discipline produces.

The two lessons together produce the framework's epistemic structure at the closing-round layer. Substrate convergence is suggestive; substrate attestation is unenforceable; primary-text evidence is authoritative when it exists. The orchestrator-class adjudication is the layer where these three signals get weighted into the closing-round verdict. The weighting is not a fixed formula; the weighting is the operator's and orchestrator's discipline at the synthesis layer, and the discipline is what determines whether the verdict matches the architectural reality or matches the substrate-consensus default.

## What this changed in my workflow

Three specific changes since the arc closed. First, when I deploy a multi-substrate research arc, I explicitly name the substrate-role assignments in the prompt structure rather than leaving them implicit. The assignments (research rigor; citation verification; novel surfacing; primary-text browsing; operationalization depth) get explicit prompts that frame each substrate's contribution within the arc; the orchestrator gets explicit instruction to weight the substrate contributions against the substrate-role assignments and against primary-text evidence at adjudication time.

Second, when I encounter a substrate self-attestation in a Council deployment, I treat the attestation as one signal among several rather than as load-bearing evidence. The discipline is to identify whether the attestation can be checked independently (against primary text; against another substrate's independent verification; against operator-side review) and to weight the attestation's contribution to the closing-round verdict by the check's outcome. The n=5 cumulative fires across Rounds 7 and 8 are sufficient to argue that attestations under unenforceable framing should not carry the load of closing-round verdicts.

Third, when I run a closing-round adjudication, I require explicit primary-text re-read of any load-bearing claim where the substrate-consensus is the input. The re-read is the operator's or orchestrator's discipline; the discipline is what produces verdict reversals when the primary-text evidence diverges from substrate consensus. Without the re-read, the closing-round verdict absorbs substrate consensus as ground truth; with the re-read, the verdict matches the primary-text evidence even when the substrate consensus would have produced a different verdict.

## What I am not claiming

I am not claiming the five-substrate role assignment is the canonical methodology for all multi-substrate research arcs. The role assignment was shaped by the specific research surface and the substrate landscape available to me at deployment time. Workflows with different research surfaces or different substrate landscapes would shape their role assignments differently. The methodology candidacy is for the substrate-style differentiation discipline (each substrate does what its style does best) and for the orchestrator-class adjudication discipline (the closing-round weights primary-text above substrate consensus); the specific five-substrate role assignment is a working operational shape, not a template.

I am not claiming the orchestrator-class catches in this arc are reproducible at any other research surface. The catches are n=2 from a single arc on a single research surface; the catches are consistent with the framework's earlier observations on orchestrator-class adjudication, but the reproductions across other arcs are open work. The methodology candidacy is the discipline; the catches are the evidence the discipline works on this surface.

I am not claiming substrate convergence is a useless signal. The convergence is useful for cases where multiple substrates have done independent verification work and reached the same conclusion through access patterns that do not share blind spots. The convergence is not authoritative for cases where the substrates are converging on a training-distribution default that primary-text evidence does not support. The discipline is to use convergence as a working signal at low-stakes references and to backstop convergence with primary-text re-read at load-bearing references.

I am not claiming the arc's five substrates exhaust the substrate landscape. The substrate landscape includes substrates I did not deploy in this arc (other LLM lineages; agentic browsing alternatives; specialized verification substrates); the arc's five substrates were chosen for fit-to-role at the surface and within the operator-attention budget the arc supported. Other arcs at different surfaces or with different budgets would deploy different substrate sets; the role discipline transfers across substrate sets, while the specific substrate selection does not.

I am not claiming the orchestrator-class layer is the only verification layer in the workflow. The verify-against-canonical-files discipline (Rule 14) covers the operator-side canonical-source verification at load-bearing references; the substrate-self-attestation discipline (Rule 19) covers the substrate-side failure mode; the orchestrator-class adjudication discipline is the synthesis-layer verification. The three disciplines together produce a verification stack where each layer has its own discipline and the layers are not interchangeable; the orchestrator-class layer is one of three load-bearing layers, not the only one.

I am claiming that a multi-substrate research arc deploying five substrates plus an orchestrator-class synthesizer produced cross-substrate verdicts that no single substrate's processing produced, and that the disagreements between substrates were the load-bearing methodology data of the arc.
