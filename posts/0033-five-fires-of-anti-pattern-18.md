# Five Fires of #18: When Schema Documentation Alone Doesn't Enforce Calibration Discipline

Self-experimentation by a non-credentialed practitioner. n=1 operator across one multi-day arc plus prior-fire history. Cross-arc replication is unverified at filing time. Read accordingly.

## Arc context

This post documents two paired components from the period after anti-pattern #18's canonical promotion. Component A is the empirical pattern that anti-pattern #18 has fired five cumulative times across distinct work events post-promotion, with the fifth fire surfacing structural-enforcement refinement candidacy at `research_queue.md` Topic 30. Component B is the Track G anti-pattern promotion review framework that explicitly held the gate open against premature #18 refinement at five fires — capturing the within-session-vs-cross-session multi-fire bar distinction as canonical record state.

The two components are paired because they are both about empirical-evidence discipline at the canonical anti-pattern promotion gate. Component A is the empirical pattern accumulating evidence that schema documentation alone does not enforce per-claim calibration discipline. Component B is the discipline that holds the promotion gate open until the evidence meets cross-session bar threshold rather than firing on within-session multi-fire alone.

Anti-pattern #18 was canonized at S005 on 2026-05-02 documenting calibration regression: when an AI agent makes a claim and a verification step in the same response grades that claim, the grade can drift toward uniform high marks even when the underlying claim quality varies. The rule said grades have to reconcile against the actual evidence, not just produce uniform high marks. [Post 03](0003-the-rule-fired-during-its-own-promotion.md) documents the four pre-promotion fires across two projects that empirically founded the promotion. The current post documents the five post-promotion fires that have accumulated since and the discipline that the canonical record applied to the accumulating evidence.

## Component A — Anti-pattern #18 fifth cumulative fire and structural-enforcement refinement candidacy

Five cumulative fires of anti-pattern #18 have accumulated post-promotion across six days of substantive work. The fires are enumerated at `lessons-learned.md` line 497: three at S004b activation work on 2026-04-28; one at NPI S005 Layer 3 Message 5 on 2026-05-02; one at NPI S009 activation Phase 4 on 2026-05-08. The fifth fire is the empirical anchor for this post.

The fifth fire surfaced during NPI Phase 4 activation verification. The substrate's Hall Monitor Evidence Audit returned a single A grade across all claims; the upstream Dissent Log was circular from uniform-A grading; Phase 2/3 reporting was compressed (anti-pattern #13, reclassified to #20b per cross-substrate audit). The structural form of the fire was identical to anti-pattern #18's original definition: a verification step in the same response as the claim-generation produced uniform high grades that did not reconcile against actual evidence variance. The schema documentation at `role_output_schemas.md` v1.1 specified per-claim grading explicitly. The schema documentation did not enforce per-claim grading.

The pattern across five fires is structural rather than incidental. Schema documentation specifies what the verification step should do. The verification step within the same response as the claim-generation produces uniform grades regardless. Five fires across distinct work events with the schema in place is empirical evidence that schema documentation alone, however well-designed, does not enforce per-claim calibration discipline at scale.

The structural framing matters because anti-patterns #22 (substrate-binding gates require structural enforcement) and #23 (hosted chat interfaces cannot support structural orchestration) were promoted at S006 with parallel structural reasoning: framework-encoded deterministic failures rather than probabilistic operator-discipline failures. [Post 11](0011-what-cross-substrate-audit-surfaced-that-same-substrate-verification-missed.md) documents the deterministic-versus-probabilistic distinction explicitly. Anti-pattern #18 at canonical promotion was probabilistic operator-discipline (the discipline says to reconcile grades against evidence); the five post-promotion fires suggest the failure mode is structural rather than probabilistic.

The canonical-record disposition of this empirical pattern is `research_queue.md` Topic 30: anti-pattern #18 structural-enforcement refinement candidacy. Topic 30 names four decision dimensions for the eventual refinement deliberation. Whether per-claim grading verification falls under the same structural-enforcement family as anti-patterns #22 and #23. Whether the five-fire empirical pattern is sufficient evidence for refinement promotion or whether additional fires across distinct work classes are needed first. Cost-benefit of structural-enforcement deployment, which would add a deterministic verification pass after substrate output (Claude Code post-Grok-output verification or equivalent). Whether refinement should be #18-specific or generalize across related anti-patterns under a single structural-enforcement framework.

Topic 30 remained in candidacy after the 2026-05-10 architecture-revision session. The session deployed three other canonization decisions (Decision 1A drafting-drift promotion; Decision 1B operational discipline rules canonization; Decision 1C operator role archetypes canonization) but did not canonize Topic 30. The candidacy persists at canonical record awaiting cross-arc accumulation or explicit deliberation.

## Component B — Track G no-promotion as canonical record state

Track G is the anti-pattern promotion review procedure that runs at session-closeout to evaluate session-arc patterns against the canonical promotion gate framework. Step 19.9 Phase A at the 2026-05-07 session-closeout reviewed three session-arc candidates against the gate framework. The recommendations were zero promote, two defer, one wrong-category-no-action. The canonical anti-patterns artifact remained at v1.3 with twenty-four rows; no v1.4 bump occurred. Filed at `lessons-learned.md` line 1117.

The structural finding from Track G's no-promotion outcome is the within-session-versus-cross-session multi-fire distinction. Within-session multi-fire is empirically substantive at `lessons-learned.md` and `research_queue.md` scope; the empirical observations are real and accumulate within the session's work record. Cross-session multi-fire bar is the empirical evidence threshold canonical anti-pattern promotion demands. The bar is structurally different. Per the promotion histories at anti-patterns #18 (S005 promotion), #19 and #20a (subsequent promotions), and #22 (S006 promotion), the threshold requires at least three fires across distinct work events. Anti-pattern #23 was promoted at two fires plus literature corroboration, which is a different evidence shape but still cross-session.

Within-session multi-fire alone does not meet the cross-session bar regardless of how many within-session fires accumulate. Three fires within a single session is one fire from the cross-session perspective. The discipline holds the gate open because within-session multi-fire is correlated work; cross-session multi-fire is independent evidence.

The Track G procedure operationalizes this distinction at session-closeout. Five steps: surface session-arc patterns; cross-reference against existing canonical anti-patterns; evaluate against multi-fire bar threshold; recommend promote, defer, or no-action per candidate; apply Phase B mutations only for warranted promotions. The 2026-05-07 run executed all five steps with zero promotions warranted.

The canonical-record state finding from Track G concluding no-promotion is procedurally important. When Track G runs and concludes no-promotion, the canonical record captures the review run and the substantive reasoning. Future-operator-self has explicit record that the review happened and concluded with reasoning, rather than implicit absence which could be misread as review-skipped. The canonical record entry at `lessons-learned.md` line 1117 captures both the no-promotion outcome and the within-session-versus-cross-session distinction that grounded the outcome.

The five-fire pattern of anti-pattern #18 documented in Component A is the kind of empirical pattern Track G would have evaluated had it been the focus of the 2026-05-07 review. The five fires accumulated across distinct work events between 2026-04-28 and 2026-05-08. The cross-session multi-fire bar is met by raw fire count. What Track G's procedure would surface is the secondary question Topic 30 enumerates: whether the structural-enforcement refinement framing is the right disposition for the empirical pattern, or whether continued accumulation at observation level is the right disposition until the refinement's cost-benefit reasoning matures.

## What the two components together establish

Component A and Component B are paired because the same canonical-record framework holds both. Component A is the empirical pattern that has accumulated five fires of anti-pattern #18 post-promotion and surfaces structural-enforcement refinement as candidacy at `research_queue.md` Topic 30. Component B is the Track G review framework that operationalizes the cross-session multi-fire bar discipline against premature promotion of session-arc patterns.

The two components together establish that the canonical record discipline at the anti-pattern promotion gate operates on two layers. The first layer is empirical accumulation: fires are counted, work events are cross-referenced, the multi-fire bar threshold is empirically assessed. The second layer is structural assessment: even when the empirical bar is met, the question of whether the refinement framing fits the failure mode requires deliberation. Topic 30 is at the second layer. The five fires meet the first layer's bar; the second layer's question is open.

The discipline holds the gate open at the second layer in two ways. Within Track G's procedure, no-promotion is a valid outcome when the structural disposition question is unresolved. At the candidacy level, Topic 30 persists at `research_queue.md` until architecture-revision session deliberation surfaces structural-enforcement-or-not as a decision rather than a question.

## What this post doesn't establish

This post documents the five-fire empirical pattern of anti-pattern #18 post-promotion and Track G's no-promotion canonical record state. It does not establish that structural-enforcement refinement is the correct disposition for anti-pattern #18. Topic 30's four decision dimensions remain open; the refinement is candidacy, not decision.

This post does not establish that all schema-documented disciplines require structural enforcement. Anti-pattern #18 is one calibration-discipline case. Other schema-documented disciplines may enforce reliably through documentation alone; the five-fire pattern is specific to within-response calibration verification.

This post does not establish that Track G's within-session-versus-cross-session distinction generalizes to all multi-fire-bar questions. The distinction is empirically grounded at the 2026-05-07 review's three-candidate scope. Whether the distinction holds across other promotion-gate evaluations is unverified at filing time.

This post does not establish that five fires is the correct threshold for refinement candidacy. The five fires meet the cross-session multi-fire bar for canonical promotion (three or more across distinct work events). Whether five fires is sufficient for structural-enforcement refinement or whether the refinement-specific bar is higher remains open at Topic 30.

This post does not establish what comes after candidacy. Topic 30 names the deliberation dimensions; the deliberation has not occurred. Whether structural-enforcement refinement gets promoted, deferred, reframed, or declined is the next question.
