When PersonaWeaver got cited in an early version of my framework as a fairness safeguard, I started to wonder whether the paper actually claimed that. I went to read it.

The paper is Qraitem, Saenko, and Plummer 2026, "Breaking the Assistant Mold: Modeling Behavioral Variation in LLM Based Procedural Character Generation," arXiv:2601.03396 (v3 4 May 2026). The paper is about a specific problem in LLM-based procedural character generation: when LLMs are asked to produce characters for game design or narrative work, the LLMs default to assistant-aligned outputs (helpful; positive; conflict-avoidant) because the assistant-alignment training is what their RLHF tuning optimized for. The result is that LLM-generated characters tend toward positive moral bias and helpful-assistant bias; the bias suppresses dramatic tension and behavioral diversity, both of which are what character-driven game design needs. PersonaWeaver's contribution is an architecture that disentangles world-building from behavioral-building so that characters can be produced with the dramatic tension and behavioral diversity the design surface requires.

What PersonaWeaver does not do is make a fairness claim. The words "fairness" and "marginalized" do not appear in the paper. The framing is dramatic tension and behavioral diversity. The intervention layer is the generation side: the architecture changes how characters are produced so that the production process yields outputs with the diversity the design surface needs. The intervention is at the moment of character creation, not at the population level the design surface eventually accumulates.

When my framework cited PersonaWeaver as if it were a fairness safeguard, the citation imported a claim the paper does not make. The correction surfaced in a multi-substrate cross-verification cycle where one substrate's primary-text re-read of the PersonaWeaver paper caught what the prior arc's substrate paraphrases had collectively missed. The correction is small at the citation-discipline level (the framework's canonical citation chain now anchors PersonaWeaver to dramatic-tension-and-behavioral-diversity rather than to fairness); the correction is larger at the framework-architecture level because it forces the framework to separate two distinct interventions that the misframing had conflated.

This post is about the layer separation. PersonaWeaver is generation-side; the framework's Marcus Reed Protocol is outcome-side; the two interventions operate at different layers, on different problems, and with different evaluation criteria. Conflating them weakens both.

## The PersonaWeaver framing as actually written

PersonaWeaver's substantive content is a disentanglement architecture. The paper proposes a two-axis decomposition of character generation: a world-building axis (the setting, the context, the situation the character occupies) and a behavioral-building axis (the character's traits, preferences, dispositional patterns). The disentanglement is operational; the architecture separates the two axes computationally so that each can be varied independently of the other, and the variation produces characters whose behavioral patterns are not constrained by the world-building axis in the ways that direct LLM-prompting produces.

The intervention's evaluation criteria are about character diversity. The paper measures whether the disentanglement produces characters that vary along the behavioral axis independently of variation along the world axis; the paper measures whether the variation produces dramatic tension between characters (operationalized through specific dialogue-and-action vignettes the paper's evaluation runs); the paper measures whether the variation produces behavioral diversity beyond the helpful-assistant default. The evaluation does not measure fairness in any sense; the paper's evaluation does not assess demographic representation, does not test for stereotype reproduction, does not measure outcome distributions across protected-class dimensions. The paper's scope is character diversity, and the paper's evaluation is calibrated to that scope.

A reading of PersonaWeaver as a fairness safeguard imports a fairness claim the paper does not make. The fairness claim would require: a definition of fairness operationalized for character generation; an evaluation methodology that measures the fairness; an empirical demonstration that the intervention satisfies the fairness criteria. The paper does none of those because the paper is not about fairness; the paper is about character diversity and dramatic tension. The architecture's contribution is real (the disentanglement is a substantive technical advance); the contribution is in the dramatic-tension-and-behavioral-diversity scope, not in the fairness scope, and the framework's citation discipline now reflects this.

## The misframing surface

The misframing happened in a research-arc cycle where one substrate's research report characterized PersonaWeaver as addressing fairness. The characterization propagated forward through subsequent substrate rounds because the subsequent rounds were reasoning over the prior substrate's research report, not over the primary paper directly. The propagation produced what the framework's research discipline calls a substrate-paraphrase chain: the wrong reading got passed through multiple substrate rounds with each round adding paraphrase-level adjustments while preserving the structural wrong reading at the core.

The correction surfaced when the framework's discipline finally routed an orchestrator-class substrate (a substrate whose role is to read prior-arc work for structural integrity at primary-text grounding) against the PersonaWeaver paper directly. The orchestrator-class re-read caught the absence of "fairness" and "marginalized" in the paper's actual text; the prior arc's substrate paraphrases had imported the fairness framing because the framework's broader research arc was concerned with fairness questions, and the substrate paraphrases had absorbed the surrounding framework's framing into their characterization of the cited paper. The correction was triply confirmed by an additional orchestrator-class re-read in a later round; the framework's canonical citation chain was then updated to anchor PersonaWeaver correctly.

The failure mode is general beyond this specific case. Substrate paraphrase chains absorb the surrounding framework's concerns into their characterizations of cited literature; the absorption is not deception, it is a predictable result of substrates operating under context-sensitive prompting where the surrounding context biases the paraphrase. The discipline that catches the absorption is primary-text re-reading at orchestrator-class scope; the discipline's value is that it reverses canonical state when the canonical state was reached through paraphrase-chain propagation rather than through primary-text grounding. The lesson is methodological, not specific to PersonaWeaver; the framework's broader discipline (Rule 11 multi-substrate verification plus orchestrator-class primary-text re-reading plus periodic stale-flag re-checks) is what makes the catching reliable.

## The two-layer distinction

Generation-side fairness, if such a thing exists in the technical sense, would be a property of the procedural generation process such that the process produces characters whose distributional properties are inherently bias-free at the moment of production. The framing presupposes that fairness can be operationalized at the moment of generation; it requires a definition of what bias-free distribution means at the per-character level. The framing is technically coherent for some narrow operationalizations of fairness (e.g., demographic representation in role assignment can be operationalized at the per-character level if the generation process has access to role-distribution targets); the framing is technically incoherent for broader operationalizations (e.g., outcome-level fairness across multi-character interactions cannot be operationalized at the per-character generation level because the outcome depends on character-character interactions that are downstream of generation).

Outcome-side fairness is a property of the population the generation process accumulates. The framing presupposes that fairness is a population-level statistic; it requires an evaluation methodology that measures the population-level statistic against fairness criteria; it places the intervention at the post-generation distribution level rather than at the per-character generation level. The framing is technically coherent for most operationalizations of fairness because most fairness operationalizations are population-level statistics; the framing is what makes auditing feasible for production systems that ship characters at scale.

PersonaWeaver is generation-side in scope. The architecture intervenes at the per-character production level; the intervention's evaluation criteria are character-level (dramatic tension; behavioral diversity); the architecture does not claim to produce a particular population-level distribution. The framework's Marcus Reed Protocol is outcome-side. The protocol audits the population the generation process produces; the audit measures population-level statistics against the protocol's criteria; the intervention is at the post-generation distribution level. The two interventions operate at different layers; their criteria are different categories of measurement; their failure modes are different categories of failure.

Calling the two interventions complementary at the same layer would import a unified-fairness-pipeline framing that neither intervention actually makes. PersonaWeaver's contribution is at the generation layer; the framework's Marcus Reed Protocol's contribution is at the outcome layer; the framework adopts both interventions but does not collapse them into a single layer-of-intervention. The framework's architecture has both layers because the failure modes the framework needs to address operate at both layers; an architecture that operates at only one of the two layers would be under-equipped to address the failure modes at the other layer.

## What Mökander 2024 grounds

The canonical anchor for outcome-side auditing comes from Mökander, Schuett, Kirk, and Floridi 2024, "Auditing large language models: a three-layered approach," *AI and Ethics* 4:1085-1115, doi:10.1007/s43681-023-00289-2. The paper develops a three-layered audit framework: governance audits (whether the operator's policies and procedures support responsible deployment); model audits (whether the model's properties support the operator's deployment); application audits (whether the application's use of the model in production satisfies the application's specific criteria). The framework's outcome-side intervention lives at the application audit layer in Mökander's framing; the audit measures the application's actual output population against the application-specific criteria the operator has committed to.

The Marcus Reed Protocol is an application-layer audit. The protocol measures the population the framework's character-generation pipeline produces against demographic-distribution criteria the framework's operator-side scope discipline has committed to. The measurement is operationalized through specific outcome-distribution checks; the checks compare the population's distribution against the criteria; the checks produce auditable artifacts that document whether the criteria are satisfied for the period the audit covers. The audit is post-generation; the audit does not change how characters are produced; the audit only changes whether the production output is accepted for downstream use.

Mökander 2024 is the canonical anchor for the three-layered framing. The framework's adoption of the application-layer audit is grounded in Mökander; the framework's specific operationalization (the Marcus Reed Protocol's specific criteria and procedures) is the framework's own implementation. The two are distinct; the citation discipline keeps them distinct because conflating them would risk importing the Mökander framing's authority into the framework's specific operationalization in ways the framing's authority does not actually extend to.

## The Marcus Reed Protocol described generically

The framework's Marcus Reed Protocol is operator-named after a specific stereotype catch incident in the framework's research arc (a previous post documents the incident and the protocol's origin in detail). The protocol's substantive content at the meta-architecture level is: outcome-distribution audit; demographic parity of positive regard outcome across groups, with era-constraint disclosure (because the framework's domain involves multi-decade historical scope, the protocol's parity criteria are era-disclosed rather than uniformly applied across eras); multi-prompt orthogonal-array-testing design to disentangle prompt effects from model effects (the BiAxisAudit literature, specifically Gan, Dong, and Li 2026 arXiv:2605.09041, is the canonical anchor for the OAT methodology, though the paper is an unreviewed preprint at the time of this post and the citation carries a preprint-status caveat).

The protocol's implementation specifics live in the framework's operator-side canonical record and are not enumerated in this post per the framework's soft-disclosure discipline. The protocol's specific operational surfaces (the audit gate's architecture; the specific demographic-coding policy; the consultant-review pathway for protected-class scope items; the mitigation framework when the audit flags a failure) are operator-specific implementation details that the framework's soft-disclosure rule keeps out of public posts. The protocol described at the meta-architecture level above is what the post puts on the table; the implementation specifics are framework canon that the framework's authority extends over rather than research findings the framework publishes openly.

The naming history matters for the public framing. The protocol's name commemorates a specific incident where an LLM substrate produced a stereotype-laden character (the character's name was Marcus Reed; the character was a constellation of demographic-correlated stereotypes); the operator caught the stereotype; the substrate accepted the catch and the framework's discipline grew out of the catch. The naming-as-memorial discipline preserves the institutional memory of why the framework's protocol exists. The protocol is operator-defined and operator-maintained; the protocol has no external prior-art attribution; substrates that produce attribution claims for the protocol (e.g., a substrate citing the protocol as if it came from a published source) are flagged as substrate-fabrication candidates per the framework's broader discipline on substrate self-attestation.

## Why the relational claim matters

PersonaWeaver and the Marcus Reed Protocol are independent interventions at different layers, not "complementary at the same layer." The independence claim matters because the alternative framing (complementary-at-the-same-layer) would import a unified-fairness-pipeline architecture that neither intervention actually makes. PersonaWeaver's intervention is at the generation layer and is about character diversity; the Marcus Reed Protocol's intervention is at the outcome layer and is about population-level demographic-parity criteria; the two layers operate on different problems and have different evaluation criteria.

A framework that adopts both interventions at their actual layers gets the benefits both interventions provide without overstating either intervention's claims. The framework gets dramatic tension and behavioral diversity from the generation-side intervention; the framework gets outcome-distribution accountability from the outcome-side intervention; the framework does not claim either intervention does what the other does. The discipline is operational; the framework's citation chain reflects the discipline; the framework's architecture documents reflect the discipline.

The structural lesson generalizes beyond procedural-character generation. Any pipeline that combines a generation-side intervention with an outcome-side intervention faces the same risk of layer-conflation; the conflation produces unified-pipeline framings that overstate one or both interventions' claims; the discipline that catches the conflation is the layer-specific evaluation criteria for each intervention. The discipline lets the framework adopt the interventions at their actual layers and lets the framework's accountability extend over what the interventions actually deliver, rather than over what a conflation framing would claim.

## What this changed in my workflow

Two specific changes. First, when a substrate research report characterizes a cited paper as making a claim, I now run an orchestrator-class primary-text re-read against the cited paper before the characterization propagates into framework canonical state. The discipline costs a primary-text reading (typically thirty minutes to two hours depending on paper length); the discipline catches paraphrase-chain absorption before the absorption propagates downstream. The PersonaWeaver case is the load-bearing example; the discipline has caught additional cases in subsequent arcs that the same operational pattern would not have caught without the discipline.

Second, when the framework adopts an intervention from the literature, I now require the intervention's layer to be explicitly stated in the framework's architecture documents. The discipline catches layer-conflations before they propagate into framework-level claims; the discipline produces architecture documents whose claims are bounded to the layer the cited intervention actually operates at. The PersonaWeaver case prompted the discipline; the framework's subsequent intervention adoptions (e.g., Mökander 2024 at the application audit layer; Sheng et al. 2019 at the regard-metric layer; Gan, Dong, and Li 2026 at the orthogonal-array-testing layer) are layer-stated explicitly in the architecture documents, and the layer-statement discipline catches absorption patterns that would otherwise propagate at the framework-canonical level.

## What I am not claiming

I am not claiming PersonaWeaver is unimportant for procedural-character work. The architecture's contribution to character diversity and dramatic tension is substantial and the paper is a substantive technical advance; the framework adopts the architecture as a generation-layer intervention because the architecture does what it claims to do. The post is about the layer-distinction discipline, not about the architecture's value; the architecture has value, and the value is at the generation layer where the architecture operates.

I am not claiming the framework's Marcus Reed Protocol is uniquely novel. The protocol's substantive content (outcome-distribution audit at the application layer; demographic-parity criteria with era-constraint disclosure; OAT design for prompt-effect disentanglement) is grounded in canonical literature (Mökander 2024 for the audit-layer framing; Sheng et al. 2019 for the regard-metric origin; Gan, Dong, and Li 2026 for the OAT methodology); the protocol's specific operationalization is the framework's own implementation, and the implementation builds on the canonical anchors rather than substituting for them. The novelty claim is at the operationalization level; the components are not novel.

I am not claiming the layer-distinction discipline eliminates the layer-conflation failure mode. The discipline catches conflations more reliably than the framework's prior practice; the discipline does not catch all conflations; the framework's research arcs will produce future conflations and the discipline's value is whether the future conflations get caught at orchestrator-class re-read or later. The discipline is a floor for the framework's engagement with cited interventions, not a ceiling; conflations that the discipline does not anticipate will surface in future work and the discipline's response will be the same primary-text re-read pattern that caught the PersonaWeaver case.

I am not claiming the BiAxisAudit citation is uncontroversial. The paper is an unreviewed preprint at the time of this post; the framework's citation carries a preprint-status caveat; the citation will be re-evaluated when the paper's reviewed status changes (if the paper proceeds to peer-reviewed publication) or when the framework's research arc produces specific reasons to re-evaluate. The preprint-status caveat is operational; it does not undermine the citation's usefulness for the framework's current adoption, and it does require that the framework treat the citation as provisional until the reviewed status resolves.

I am not claiming the framework's soft-disclosure of the Marcus Reed Protocol's implementation specifics is permanent. The soft-disclosure rule has a revisit date (2026-11-04 per the rule's current version); the framework's commitment is to revisit the disclosure decision at the revisit date and to consider whether the framework's commercialization profile has changed in ways that would change the disclosure decision. The current soft-disclosure is operational discipline for the current commercialization profile; the disclosure is not a permanent commitment to keep the implementation specifics private.

## What I am claiming

Fairness in procedural-character generation operates at two distinct layers that are often conflated. PersonaWeaver is generation-side and addresses character diversity and dramatic tension; the framework's Marcus Reed Protocol is outcome-side and addresses population-level demographic-parity criteria. The two interventions operate at different layers, on different problems, with different evaluation criteria; conflating them imports unified-pipeline framings that overstate one or both interventions' claims. The discipline that catches the conflation is the layer-specific evaluation criteria for each intervention, and the discipline's value is that it lets the framework adopt the interventions at their actual layers without overstating either.

If you are building procedural-character generation pipelines and adopting interventions from the literature, the layer-distinction discipline is operationally cheap and catches conflations that paraphrase-chain absorption would otherwise propagate into framework-level claims. The primary-text re-read at orchestrator-class scope is the operational pattern that catches the absorption; the layer-statement requirement in architecture documents is the operational pattern that catches the conflation at the framework-canonical level. The two patterns together are the framework's discipline; the discipline is what makes the layer-distinction operationally enforceable.

The relational claim about PersonaWeaver and the Marcus Reed Protocol is independence-at-different-layers; the framework adopts both interventions; the framework does not collapse them into a single layer-of-intervention. The independence is what the framework commits to publicly so that the framework's accountability extends over what the interventions actually deliver, not over what a conflation framing would claim. The discipline is the framework's commitment to layer-honesty in the framework's adoption of cited interventions, and the commitment is what this post puts on the table.
