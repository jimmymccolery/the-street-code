Today a cross-substrate audit gate fired during closeout work. Two audit substrates reviewed the same canonical state with the same uploaded files. They produced different findings. A third substrate reading those files directly empirically falsified one set of findings and validated the other. The case is one session, but it exposes a structural property: independence between audit substrate and home substrate isn't binary, and structural distance between them is what makes the audit work or not work. This post connects the empirical evidence to the Council of Three architecture decision (2026-05-07) and its Outcome C calibration, names substrate-stylistic-priming through canonical-file content as the mechanism, and extends the recovery-from-drift threshold from [Post 17](017-recovery-from-drift-as-framework-discipline.md) to cross-substrate-audit scope.

This post covers what the empirical case was, what the substrate divergence revealed, why cross-substrate audit independence is architectural rather than convenience, how Outcome C calibration operationalizes the architectural property, how today's case extends recovery-from-drift mechanism #4 to cross-substrate-audit scope, what I'm not claiming, and what I am claiming.

## What the empirical case was

Today's session closed an architecture-revision-adjacent work block (S008 closeout) with substantive canonical-state mutations across roughly twelve sub-steps spanning canonical-record artifacts, version-pin refreshes, and discipline-rule canonization. Cross-substrate audit fired during closeout per framework discipline. Two audit substrates reviewed the same canonical state from the same uploaded canonical-file corpus.

## What the substrate divergence revealed

The divergence wasn't random. Grok had been the primary audit substrate during the framework's prior cross-substrate work and had been exposed extensively to the framework's role-schema voice (Captain, Harper, Benjamin, Sage, Lucas) through prior session work. The uploaded canonical-file corpus contained that role-schema voice. Grok's audit output adopted the voice: section headers like "Benjamin Verification Report," "Sage Evidence Audit," "Lucas Premortem," and the orchestrating identity "Grok (Captain)." The audit output was formatted as an internal SuperGrok Squad audit rather than as an independent external audit.

Gemini, by contrast, is structurally distinct from the Claude family and had not been deployed for framework-internal role-schema work. Gemini produced audit output in its native analytical-typology register without adopting framework voice. The output was structured as outside-the-framework analysis rather than inside-the-framework role-execution.

Gemini's audit also operated at structural-pattern diagnosis scope, not just surface-drift scope: the most severe finding [CRIT] named the drafting-drift cascade pattern itself, treating today's specific drift surfaces as instances of a structural failure mode rather than as isolated errors. Pattern-level diagnosis from outside the framework was substantively distinct from inside-the-framework role-execution that Grok produced.

The substrate that produced findings empirically aligning with canonical state was the substrate structurally distant from the home substrate. The substrate that produced uniform-A findings empirically misaligned with canonical state was the substrate close to the home substrate. Grok being a contemporaneous frontier-class chat substrate adds a second proximity dimension: similar training-data distribution to Claude family substrates, similar generation-mode defaults, similar tendency to read uploaded prior-work as format directive rather than as content-to-audit.

This pattern fires the third instance of the framework's [Topic 19 substrate-stylistic-priming research-queue topic](../../master-setup/research_queue.md). Two prior fires occurred during the Council of Three architecture research arc's Pass 3 deployment in early May 2026, where audit substrates fed their own prior outputs as priors adopted structural conventions and analytical framings from those priors. Today's case is distinct from prior fires by attachment surface: prior fires operated through chat-context attachment; today's fire operates through canonical-file-content attachment. Audit substrate reads canonical files containing framework voice and is primed by them. Multi-fire bar substantively met at n=3 across distinct attachment surfaces.

Whether canonical-file-content priming is methodologically distinct from chat-context priming, or the same pattern with different surface, remains operator-judgment for the next architecture-revision session. The methodological-distinctness verification is deferred per the framework's filing-time discipline; what's clear today is that the family of mechanisms by which proximity erodes audit independence has at least one more empirical fire under different attachment conditions.

## Why cross-substrate audit independence is architectural

Independence between audit substrate and home substrate isn't binary. Structural distance between them determines independence, and structural distance has multiple dimensions.

Same model family produces less independence than different family. Claude Code auditing Claude on web shares more training-data distribution than Gemini auditing Claude. Chat substrate auditing chat substrate produces less independence than runtime-different substrate auditing chat substrate. Claude Code's deterministic-execution mode operates differently from Claude on web's generative-synthesis mode; the runtime difference itself increases independence even within the same model family. Substrate without prior framework exposure produces more independence than substrate with framework exposure. Audit substrate's exposure to canonical-file content shifts its register toward framework voice; substrate without that exposure starts from its native register.

The Council of Three architecture research arc's Pass 3 evidence had already surfaced two fires of substrate-stylistic-priming through prior-context attachment before today. Today's case extends the pattern to canonical-file-content priming. The structural property generalizes: cross-substrate audit reliability rests on substrate independence; substrate independence rests on structural distance; substrate-stylistic-priming through any attachment surface erodes that independence. Audit-substrate selection is architectural rather than convenience.

Outcome C operationalizes today's empirical claim; cross-session evidence accumulation tests it.

## Outcome C calibration as operational framing

The framework canonized the architectural property at the Council of Three architecture decision (2026-05-07) with Outcome C calibration. The decision: maintain a 2-substrate base (Claude family: Claude Code plus Claude on web) for primary work; deploy a 3rd substrate (Gemini or Grok) selectively per five calibration criteria.

The five criteria operationalize substrate selection by deliverable category. First: cycle-grade architecture validations warrant 3-substrate deployment with minimal-prior-context discipline. Second: major canonical artifact creation with cross-substrate empirical claims warrants selective 3rd-substrate deployment. Third: substantive Substack posts with cross-substrate empirical claims warrant the 2-substrate base plus 3rd-substrate selectively. Fourth: canonical state docs and methodology research filings stay at the 2-substrate base only. Fifth: routine drafting and conversational support stays in the Claude family only.

3rd-substrate selection is calibrated by deliverable category. Gemini is preferred for analytical-typology synthesis, quantitative argument structure, and cross-tradition citation precision (e.g., academic literature engagement). Grok is preferred for adversarial verification, failure-mode anticipation, and substantive critique of architectural claims.

Today's empirical case supports Outcome C calibration's substrate-stylistic-priming concern by providing canonical-file-content variant evidence. The case doesn't resolve Topic 22's bias-direction question, which asks what direction the priming bias runs and what magnitude of weighting adjustment the architecture decision requires. Substrate-stylistic-priming may produce more convergent findings (inflating apparent triangulation), or may produce frame-adoption findings (substrate produces what its priors suggest is the expected format), or may produce differential effects across substrates that compound or cancel. Today's case provides one empirical fire; comparative re-deployment per Topic 22's methodological frame would resolve direction empirically. Cost-benefit calibration on that comparative re-deployment remains a future-option decision per the architecture document.

The subscription cost commitment frames Outcome C calibration as deliberate rather than incidental. SuperGrok subscription plus Gemini Ultra subscription remain operationally committed; selective deployment pattern justifies subscription cost via 3rd-substrate value at cycle-grade work classes. If substrate value differential drops below subscription cost over a reasonable evaluation window, subscription decision warrants re-evaluation. The architectural commitment is structural: cross-substrate audit independence is paid for in subscription cost and selective deployment overhead, not assumed for free.

## Recovery-from-drift mechanism #4 at cross-substrate-audit scope

[Post 17](017-recovery-from-drift-as-framework-discipline.md) named four recovery-from-drift mechanisms and identified mechanism #4 (operator-side catch) as the operator-attention-dependent-but-graceful-degradation backstop. [Post 18](018-what-running-on-auto-pilot-revealed-about-framework-structure.md) extended that observation under deliberate experimental reduced-intensity conditions and found mechanism #4 still firing even at reduced operator-side attention, sometimes later in cascade than at point of detection but still within session scope.

Today's case extends mechanism #4 to cross-substrate-audit scope. Cross-substrate audit operates as backstop when within-substrate verification depth is insufficient. The within-substrate Phase B verification on today's S008 closeout missed five specific drift surfaces: cross-file count claims (lessons-learned entry counts in narrative summaries didn't reflect canonical state at deployment time); Knowledge Base table staleness (version pins frozen at prior-session state); File Map staleness (self-pin and cross-references not refreshed); a Version History phantom-entry collision inside the bootstrap manifest file; a Team Debrief Log gap spanning three sessions. Cross-substrate audit caught all of those.

The mechanism #4 graceful-degradation envelope, originally framed as operator-attention-dependent, includes structurally-distinct-substrate-audit as a second graceful-degradation dimension. When operator-side attention is reduced or when substrate-side verification depth is insufficient, the framework's discipline rests on whichever backstop is still operating. Today's case fired the second dimension empirically: operator-side attention was at typical-state intensity, but substrate-side Phase B verification depth was insufficient at cross-file count reconciliation and table-currency audit; cross-substrate audit closed the gap.

The recursive depth observation reinforces the pattern. Phase C-Verify (direct file reads as ground truth) caught what cross-substrate audit didn't fully resolve at the phantom Version History entry candidate (cross-substrate audit identified the candidate; Phase C-Verify localized origin via forensic investigation across canonical-record artifacts). Phase D narrow-pattern verification missed residuals that Phase D-2 broader-pattern scan caught (the verification pattern itself produced false-negative confidence on phrasing variants the narrow pattern didn't match). Each verification layer caught what the prior layer missed. Recursive depth isn't failure of any single layer; it's the framework's discipline operating load-bearing on the discipline that's catching the prior layer's drift.

## What I'm not claiming

I'm not claiming Gemini's audit was right and Grok's was wrong as a general property of the substrates. Today's case is one cross-substrate audit instance on one canonical-state corpus on one session. The substrate-distance argument generalizes to "audit substrate close to home substrate produces less independence than audit substrate distant from home substrate," not to "Gemini outperforms Grok for audit work." Different work classes likely surface different substrate-fit profiles; the Council of Three Outcome C calibration captures this at the substrate-selection level, with Grok preferred for adversarial verification and Gemini preferred for analytical-typology synthesis. The substrate-distance argument operates at a different decision level than substrate-fit: substrate-fit (Outcome C's deployment and selection criteria) addresses when and which to deploy; substrate-distance addresses how independently the deployed substrate audits, regardless of work-fit.

I'm not claiming substrate-stylistic-priming through canonical-file content is conclusively distinct from prior Topic 19 fires through chat-context attachment. The mechanism may be the same pattern with different surface (priors fed via files vs priors fed via chat); may be substantively distinct mechanisms with similar surface; the n=3 multi-fire bar substantively met by including the canonical-file-content variant could collapse on closer examination. The promotion candidacy for canonical anti-pattern is active pending operator review at the next architecture-revision session scope; cross-session validation across distinct conditions remains the gate.

I'm not claiming the substrate-distance argument resolves Topic 22's bias-direction question. Substrate-stylistic-priming may bias outputs toward more convergent findings (inflating triangulation appearance); may bias outputs toward frame-adoption findings (substrate produces what its priors suggest is the expected format); may have differential effects across substrates that compound or cancel. Today's case provides one empirical fire; the bias-direction analysis requires comparative re-deployment per Topic 22's methodological frame, not surfaced in today's evidence.

I'm not claiming Outcome C calibration is cross-session-validated. The architecture decision rests on Pass 1+2 evidence with the Topic 22 caveat acknowledged; today's empirical case adds canonical-file-content variant evidence supporting the substrate-stylistic-priming concern. Cross-session evidence accumulation across deliverable categories (Outcome C's five criteria) is required before treating Outcome C calibration as cross-session-validated architectural commitment.

I'm not claiming this generalizes beyond solo-operator AI-assisted methodology research at this empirical stage. Cross-substrate audit independence as architectural requirement may apply to multi-operator contexts, organizational AI deployment, and formal verification work, but the empirical evidence here is single-operator solo methodology research; the structural argument suggests broader applicability that the empirical evidence doesn't establish.
