## What Park 2023 contributes

Park 2023's three-process decomposition is the paper's most directly-borrowed contribution to the framework. The decomposition has three processes:

Observe. The agent records relevant environmental events. The observation process operates as a perception layer that filters the agent's environment for events the agent's architecture considers relevant; the filtered observations enter the agent's memory stream as raw observations. The observation process is not LLM-heavy; the process is closer to event-recording than to event-interpretation; the LLM-heavier processes operate on the recorded observations downstream.

Reflect. The agent periodically synthesizes observations into higher-level inferences. The reflection process operates on accumulated observations from the memory stream; the process produces higher-level inferences that the agent's subsequent reasoning depends on (the example from the Park 2023 paper: an agent observes the player bowling on Lane 5 every Saturday for two months; the reflection process synthesizes the observations into the higher-level inference "the player has a Saturday-bowling pattern on Lane 5"; the inference becomes part of the agent's memory stream alongside the original observations). The reflection process is LLM-heavy; the process runs on the LLM to produce the higher-level inferences from the accumulated observation context.

Plan. The agent uses reflections to score candidate actions. The planning process operates on the agent's current state plus the higher-level inferences from the reflection process; the process scores candidate actions against the agent's goals and selects actions to pursue. The planning process is LLM-heavy when the candidate-action evaluation requires LLM-level reasoning; the process can be simpler when the action evaluation can be cached or pre-computed.

The memory stream is the paper's persistent-state architecture. The stream accumulates observations, reflections, and plans across the agent's lifetime; the stream is the persistent context that subsequent agent reasoning depends on; the stream's growth is bounded by the paper's specific implementation choices (memory-importance scoring; memory retrieval at the relevant context window per agent action; periodic summarization to prevent context-window overflow).

The paper's published evaluation ran 25 agents in a confined sandbox scenario (a small town-simulation environment; multiple-day scenario duration; LLM-driven agent reasoning at all three processes; user-study evaluation of agent-behavior believability). The evaluation supported believability claims at the small-population small-scope scale; the evaluation did not extend to consumer-game scale or to multi-decade scope or to per-frame-budget-constrained shipping platforms.

## Why this maps cleanly to the framework

The three-process decomposition aligns with the framework's three-layer memory architecture. The Park 2023 observation process aligns with the framework's Layer 1 raw events (both layers receive filtered environmental observations as raw inputs; both layers' contents enter subsequent processing downstream). The Park 2023 reflection process aligns with the framework's Layer 2 encoded-and-reconstructed perspective memory plus the framework's consolidation pass (both layers operate on accumulated observations to produce higher-level structured representations; both layers' outputs are accessible to subsequent reasoning). The Park 2023 planning process aligns with the framework's active-inference decision-scoring layer plus the substrate-neutrality bridge from Bouizegarene et al. 2024 *Frontiers in Psychology* 15:1345480 (both layers score candidate actions against the agent's goals and state; both layers run on inference machinery that the framework's broader commitments specify).

The alignment is structural, not implementational. Park 2023 runs the three processes on LLM at all layers; the framework runs the three processes on a mix of LLM (at the slow consolidation-pass cadence), cached policies (at the fast per-frame runtime cadence), and active-inference apparatus (at the audit-layer cadence; at the consolidation-pass cadence for cached-policy regeneration). The structural alignment is the framework's borrowing from Park 2023; the implementational divergence is the framework's adaptation to the production-deployment constraints Park 2023 did not address.

The structural alignment matters for the framework's positioning. Park 2023 is the canonical recent precedent for the kind of architecture the framework adopts; citing Park 2023 as the conceptual backbone is honest about the framework's lineage; declining Park 2023's direct-LLM-scaling deployment is honest about the framework's adaptation to constraints Park 2023 did not address. ## The scaling-constraint that forces the hybrid path

The arithmetic at the framework's target scale is sharp and worth walking through in detail. Park 2023's published evaluation: 25 agents; one LLM call per observation, per reflection, per plan; paper-evaluation duration limited to days of scenario simulation. The framework's V1 target: tens of NPCs (approximately 80 to 100 characters per the V1 spec); each NPC makes roughly ten decisions per game-minute at the framework's interaction density; the per-frame budget at iOS V1 target framerate cannot tolerate per-frame LLM calls.

The per-decision cost arithmetic at current LLM API pricing produces specific numbers worth surfacing. Per-decision LLM compute at approximately $0.001 to $0.01 per inference (the cost range varies by model and by API pricing tier; the range is conservative for current OpenAI and Anthropic pricing as of mid-2026); the framework's per-game-hour decision count at the V1 scale is in the order of tens of thousands; the per-game-hour LLM cost would be in the order of tens of dollars per player per hour at the conservative end of the cost range; the per-player-session cost across multi-hour sessions would be hundreds of dollars per player; the framework's indie-budget commercial product cannot absorb the cost structure.

The per-frame-latency arithmetic compounds the cost problem. Even ignoring the LLM API cost question, the per-frame latency of LLM API calls is in the order of hundreds of milliseconds to seconds per call depending on the model and the API provider; the per-frame budget at iOS V1 target framerate is approximately 33 milliseconds; the latency mismatch makes per-frame LLM calls operationally infeasible regardless of cost. The framework's NPCs cannot make per-frame LLM calls at the target framerate; the architecture has to do something else for the per-frame decision pipeline.

The direct-scaling math does not close for indie-budget production deployment. The constraint is operationally consequential; the constraint is what forces the hybrid path the framework adopts; the constraint is not a soft preference, the constraint is a hard binding on what the V1 deployment can do at the production layer.

## The architectural surgery the framework adopts

The framework's adaptation keeps Park 2023's backbone and substitutes the implementational layers. Specifically:

The observation layer (Park 2023 process 1) becomes cached and sampled events plus Markov-blanket isolation. The framework's Layer 1 raw events store the observations; the observation cadence is per-event rather than per-frame; the Markov-blanket isolation discipline ensures the NPC's perception reads only the NPC's own perceptual window rather than reading player-side narrative state. The observation layer's compute cost is bounded by the event-occurrence rate (which is much lower than per-frame); the layer's cost is operationally tractable at the framework's target scale.

The reflection layer (Park 2023 process 2) becomes the framework's yearly consolidation pass. The consolidation pass (documented in post 0118 in this arc) runs at game-year cadence; the pass evaluates Layer 1 events for promotion to Layer 2; the pass produces consolidated Layer 2 entries that subsequent reasoning depends on. The LLM apparatus runs at the consolidation pass to drive the consolidation summaries IF computationally affordable at the yearly cadence (LLM API costs at yearly cadence are much more manageable than at per-frame cadence; the per-game-year LLM call count is in the order of tens to hundreds rather than tens of thousands; the cost structure becomes tractable at the cadence shift); the consolidation pass's LLM usage is the framework's primary LLM-deployment surface in V1.

The planning layer (Park 2023 process 3) becomes cached active-inference policies at runtime plus sophisticated-inference EFE evaluation at consolidation boundaries (audit layer; post 0119 in this arc). The cached policies drive per-frame visible behavior cheaply; the sophisticated-inference evaluation runs at consolidation-pass cadence to evaluate whether the cached-policy decisions align with the framework's discipline and to regenerate cached policies for the upcoming game-year. The planning layer's compute cost is bounded by the cached-policy lookup at runtime (per-frame budget compatible) and by the consolidation-pass evaluation at yearly cadence (operationally tractable budget).

The substitutions preserve Park 2023's structural backbone while substituting the implementational layers with the framework's hybrid-path commitments. The framework's NPCs run the observe-reflect-plan cycle at the structural level; the implementation cycles between cached and LLM-and-active-inference layers at different cadences; the cycling is what makes the architecture fit within the V1 budget while preserving the structural commitments Park 2023's backbone supports.
