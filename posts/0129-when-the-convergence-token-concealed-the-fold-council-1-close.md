I am a solo indie developer documenting the methodology of a procedural-character architecture for a multi-decade bowling-tycoon simulation. The Council methodology is the framework's operational discipline for architectural decisions with non-trivial downside if wrong. Council #1 was the framework's first deployment of the methodology against a specific decision: whether to add a relationship-mode variable (Fiske Relational Models Theory primitives) to the pair-relational architecture, or whether the existing decomposition (HEXACO honesty-humility trait plus attachment style plus Hawkins-Carrere-Gottman sentiment override) is operationally sufficient for the V1 design surface.

This post documents how Council #1 closed. Three substrates converged on the same verdict token. The token was misleading. The framework's orchestrator layer contested the pattern classification I had assigned to the convergence, correctly, and surfaced two synthesis contributions the substrates missed. This post documents the close. Post 0130 in this arc specs the actual architectural output.

## The Council configuration

The Council configuration reserved distinct roles for three substrates so their contributions would not collapse into overlapping evidence. Substrate A ran on Perplexity Deep Research and covered the primary-source depth question: is the Fiske Relational Models Theory construct actually reducible to what the framework's existing decomposition already carries? Substrate B ran on Claude Web Opus extended-thinking and covered the implementation-schema minimization question: what is the minimum honest addition that delivers the primitive, where does it live in the framework's three-layer architecture, and what does it cost against the Path A budget envelope? Substrate C ran on Grok DeepSearch and covered the commercial-precedent breadth question: how have shipped commercial procedural-character architectures represented relationship type versus relationship affect, and what player-perceptual failure modes has the absence of type representation produced in long-running simulations?

The role separation was not a preference; it was structural. Substrate A was instructed not to survey commercial precedent (Substrate C's assigned role). Substrate B was instructed not to duplicate Substrate A's primary-source mapping (Substrate B did not read the Fiske primary text, and disclosed this cleanly at its verification trail). Substrate C was instructed not to adjudicate whether Fiske Relational Models Theory is the right anthropology (Substrate A's assigned role). The output of this structure is that when the substrates converge on a verdict, they converge via non-overlapping evidence bases, and the convergence is a corroboration signal rather than an amplification of a shared prior.

## The convergence and the reading I got wrong

All three substrates returned the same verdict token: ADD-AT-V1.x. Add the primitive, but not at iOS V1 proper; the addition belongs at the V1.x point release rather than at the fixed V1 ship date. No substrate returned DO-NOT-ADD as its primary verdict; none returned ADD-AT-V1. Convergence at the verdict token was unambiguous.

The Council methodology's Rule 14 (the Madisonian Gate Verdict Classification Discipline) requires the orchestrator to classify the convergence pattern into one of six enumerated verdict patterns rather than treating verdict-token convergence as sufficient. My initial pattern classification was Pattern A (the Convergent-Axis-Level pattern with fold-set integration into a full-default ratification). The reasoning I gave: three non-overlapping reasoning chains landing on the same verdict is the literal Pattern A signature, and the Pattern A signature was present. The reasoning was defensible; the classification was wrong.

The framework's orchestrator layer (the Claude Web ORCHESTRATOR-mode chat operating against the refreshed Master Setup project knowledge) contested Pattern A and argued Pattern B (the ADOPT-FOLD pattern with conservative-bias default lean per Rule 10). The contest was correct and I conceded on reflection.

The specific discriminator: my Pattern A read collapsed at token-level convergence. I stopped at "three substrates said the same verdict via three different reasoning chains" and treated the modal-status underneath the verdict token as uniform. The modal-status underneath the verdict token was not uniform. Substrate A treated the addition as warranted-and-timing-open (the addition is earned on theory-and-measurement grounds; the only live question is when). Substrates B and C explicitly preserved live DO-NOT-ADD reversal branches: B's branch is gated on three specific ablations (mode-blind ablation; mode-permutation ablation; sentiment-collinearity ablation) plus a WEIRD-default disclosure check; C's branch is gated on empirical contact with tycoon-specific NPC populations revealing whether the scalar regime alone maintains sufficient differentiation.

Two of three substrates explicitly preserved a DO-NOT-ADD reversal branch conditioned on ablation. That is not a same-direction refinement you fold into ratification; that is a fold that conditions the verdict and carries non-trivial reversal probability. Per Rule 10's Conservative-Bias default (lean B ADOPT-FOLD over C RATIFY-BASE under any doubt), the correct classification is Pattern B, and forcing Pattern A here would flatten the conditional structure the substrates deliberately built. Flattening the conditional structure into a full-default ratification is the exact "absorb divergence into consensus" move the Madisonian Gate exists to prevent (it is the A-side sibling of the RATIFY-BASE-when-ADOPT-FOLD-applies failure mode that the framework's Anti-Pattern #29 catches on the C-side).

The concession matters as documented empirical foundation for the framework's Rule 14 discipline. The classification error I made is exactly the failure mode Rule 14 exists to catch: verdict-token convergence obscuring modal-status divergence. The framework's discipline caught the error via the orchestrator layer's structural verification requirement rather than via my own second-look. ## The construct-distinctness versus implementation-orthogonality reframe

The orchestrator layer surfaced a load-bearing distinction the substrates conflated. The distinction resolves the apparent conflict between Substrate A's warranted-and-timing-open modal status and Substrates B-and-C's candidate-pending-evidence modal status.

Substrate A discharged construct-redundancy: is the Fiske Relational Models Theory construct reducible to what the framework's existing decomposition already carries? Substrate A's answer, backed by Zakharin and Bates 2023 (independently verified; PLOS ONE 18(6):e0287391; primary-source confirmed at the article page for every study-structure detail the substrate cited), is no. The paper's Studies 2 and 3 explicitly test the two-factor reduction against the four-factor Fiske model and reject the reduction; combining Communal Sharing and Equality Matching worsens fit; simpler equality-inequality or close-distant models do not fit. The Fiske Relational Models Theory construct survives as a four-factor construct with sample-size validation at Study 2 N=617 and Study 3 N=615 across British samples at the University of Edinburgh.

Substrate A's answer does not discharge implementation-collinearity: is this build's `relationship_mode` enum redundant with this build's sentiment scalar, given the framework's mode-assignment heuristics and the framework's specific tycoon-domain NPC population? That is a different question. Construct-level distinctness in the general population is not the same object as implementation-level orthogonality in the framework's population. Substrates B and C are asking the implementation-collinearity question. Substrate A answered the construct-redundancy question.

Because the framework's V1 commitment is an implementation, not a psychology-debate adjudication, the correct modal status for the operator's decision is the conditional framing that B and C preserved. Substrate A is right that the Fiske Relational Models Theory construct is not reducible as a theory; Substrates B and C are right that whether this build's enum earns its variance is currently unproven; the two are not in conflict once the questions are separated.

## The general-factor foreshadowing of collinearity

The orchestrator layer surfaced a second contribution the substrates missed. The contribution comes from reading Substrate A's linchpin citation more carefully than any of the three substrates read it.

Zakharin and Bates 2023 finds that the four-factor Fiske Relational Models Theory model requires a general factor in Studies 2 and 3 to fit well. Study 4 characterizes the general factor's substantive content: the general factor is associated with relationship closeness (higher scores on the general factor typically occur for closer relationships). Closeness is the construct-neighbor of the pair-relational sentiment scalar the framework already implements (the ordered-pair sentiment scalar in the range from negative one to positive one, documented in post 0116 in this arc, which loads the pair's affective-history-modulated interpretation of subsequent events).

The primary source Substrate A cited as proof of Fiske Relational Models Theory's non-redundancy contains the empirical seed of the collinearity worry that Substrates B and C flagged only as untested precaution. Substrate A cited the general factor as a nuance without linking it to Substrate B's collinearity ablation. Substrate B flagged the collinearity worry without grounding it (Substrate B did not read the Zakharin-and-Bates paper directly; Substrate B verified the arXiv IDs the operator's canonical citations file lists, cross-checked one unrelated citation as a sanity control, and left the specific Zakharin-and-Bates paper for Substrate A's role). Substrate C flagged convergence-risk by commercial analogy, not by psychometric evidence.

The synthesis contribution is: Substrate B's ablation number three (the sentiment-collinearity ablation) is not merely precautionary. It is evidence-motivated by the primary source Substrate A cited. The ablation must not merely check whether the `relationship_mode` enum is distinguishable from the sentiment scalar; the ablation must quantify how much of the mode's variance survives after partialling out the closeness-linked general factor that Zakharin and Bates found is required for the four-factor structure to fit. That residual is the actual justification surface for the enum. None of the three substrates connected these primary-text findings to the specific implementation-collinearity ablation Substrate B specified; the orchestrator-layer contribution is that connection.

## The composed gate

The orchestrator layer surfaced a third contribution the substrates missed: composing Substrate B's ablation suite with Substrate C's tycoon-population setting produces a gate that neither substrate specified.

Substrate B specified an ablation mechanism at the general architecture level (mode-blind ablation, mode-permutation ablation, sentiment-collinearity ablation, WEIRD-default disclosure check). Substrate B did not specify the setting in which the ablation would run. Substrate C specified the setting where the primitive earns its distinction (empirical contact with tycoon-specific NPC populations: staff-loyalty relationships, customer-patronage relationships, league-and-competitor-dynamics relationships). Substrate C did not specify the mechanism.

The composed gate reads Substrate B's ablation suite as the mechanism and Substrate C's tycoon-population scenarios as the setting. Substrate B supplies the how; Substrate C supplies the where; neither substrate stated the composition. The composition is the concrete V1.x readiness test: run the sharpened sentiment-collinearity ablation against the tycoon-specific relational scenarios and measure the mode-variance residual. If the residual is negligible, the verdict collapses to DO-NOT-ADD and the reserved schema slot is retired at near-zero sunk cost. If the residual survives, operationalize the mode-gate and the cached-policy integration.

The second part is BANK: gate the V1.x operationalization (the mode-gate on decision-scoring plus the cached-policy integration) behind the sharpened sentiment-collinearity ablation.
