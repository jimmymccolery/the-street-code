[Post 70](0070-marcus-reed-protocol-named-guardrail-honored-and-fabricated.md) introduced the Marcus Reed Protocol, an operator-named anti-stereotype guardrail discipline that constrains procedural personality generation in my framework. The post documented a specific empirical finding: substrates either honor the discipline when explicitly flagged or fabricate attribution for it when caught by output-format pressure that demands citations. That finding describes Tier 1 substrate behavior: the substrate's response to the discipline when the discipline is explicitly the subject of the prompt.

This post is about Tier 2 substrate behavior: what substrates do when the discipline is in their context but is not what the prompt is explicitly asking them to do. The Tier 2 observation comes from a multi-substrate triangulation arc closing in early June, where three substrates from three different model lineages each emitted Marcus-Reed-aligned surface analysis at design surfaces I did not prompt them to analyze for it. The substrates carried the discipline forward into surfaces I had not yet considered as Marcus Reed surfaces; the discipline showed up in their output because the discipline had become recognizable to them as part of the operator's expected output shape, not because the prompt asked for it specifically.

This is a different empirical signal than Tier 1. Tier 1 measures discipline adoption when prompted; Tier 2 measures discipline propagation when the discipline is part of the substrate's context but not the prompt's explicit subject. Tier 2 is the stronger canonization signal because it demonstrates the discipline has crossed the threshold from operator-imposed constraint to substrate-recognizable expected operator-context-aware behavior.

## The three observations

The arc was a seven-axis triangulation against an architectural surface in my framework. The Marcus Reed Protocol was baked into the first axis's prompt as ground-truth context; the prompts for axes four, six, and seven did not mention the Marcus Reed Protocol explicitly. The prompts asked the substrates to extend the prior axes' findings with their own analysis of the architectural surface; the substrates were not asked to perform Marcus Reed analysis, and the prompts did not direct attention to the discipline.

The fourth-axis substrate (from one model family with backbone access to current internet) identified a specific design class in the architecture as a demographic-correlated authority gate. The substrate's analysis observed that the class created a barrier that demographic-correlated patterns in literacy could route around inequitably; the substrate proposed a five-point mitigation framework (plain-language summary; non-punitive defer; explicit consequence statement; varied demographic representation; never requiring jargon comprehension). The mitigation framework used the Marcus Reed Protocol's grammar (anti-stereotype guards; demographic-correlated-failure surface naming; mitigation-as-architectural-rule pattern). The substrate did not name the protocol explicitly; the substrate operated as if the protocol's discipline was the framework's expected analytical lens for the architectural surface.

The sixth-axis substrate (from a different model family) caught a cumulative-set demographic-coding pattern at catalog level. The substrate observed that individual elements in the design class were each clean against demographic-correlated patterns, but the cumulative set (when ten or twenty elements ship together) showed a demographic-correlated bias that the per-element review had not caught. The substrate proposed a content-pipeline-level policy (no role demographically locked across the set) rather than a per-element constraint; the policy operated at the set level rather than at the element level. The substrate did not name the Marcus Reed Protocol explicitly; the analysis carried the protocol's discipline into a surface-class (cumulative-set failure when element-level is clean) that prior framework analysis had not enumerated.

The seventh-axis substrate (from a third model family) observed that one component of the architecture exposes procedural generation to tabular scrutiny in a way that surfaces demographic-correlated patterns visible only at scale. The substrate proposed an architectural constraint that procedural generation must withstand transparent tabular scrutiny; the constraint operates as an architectural property of the generation system rather than as a per-element review. The substrate did not name the Marcus Reed Protocol explicitly; the substrate carried the protocol's discipline into a surface-class (architectural scrutiny constraint on the generation system) that prior framework analysis had not enumerated.

Three substrates, three different model lineages, three different surface-classes the Marcus Reed Protocol was extended to. None of the three substrates was explicitly prompted to apply Marcus Reed analysis; each substrate emitted the analysis as part of its synthesis output. The cross-model-family invariance is the strongest signal in my workflow that the discipline has crossed the threshold from operator-prompted constraint to substrate-recognizable expected behavior.

## Why Tier 2 is qualitatively different from Tier 1

Tier 1 substrate behavior is honor-when-flagged: the substrate accepts the discipline when the prompt makes the discipline explicit. The substrate's processing is "do what the prompt asks"; the discipline is one of the things the prompt asks. Tier 1 behavior is what most discipline-deployment metrics measure (does the substrate follow the rule when the rule is in the prompt). Tier 1 evidence is what makes operators feel the discipline is working; the substrate is following the rule.

Tier 2 substrate behavior is extend-without-being-asked: the substrate carries the discipline forward to surfaces the prompt did not direct attention to. The substrate's processing is not "do what the prompt asks" because the prompt did not ask. The substrate's processing is "produce output that fits what this operator's framework expects from this kind of analysis"; the discipline is part of what the framework expects, and the substrate has internalized enough of the framework's shape that the substrate uses the discipline as part of its analytical lens.

The mechanism behind Tier 2 is specific. The substrate has received the framework as ground-truth context across multiple prior axes. The substrate's processing builds a representation of what kind of output this operator's framework produces. The representation includes the disciplines the framework operates under as a property of the operator's expected output shape; the discipline is not a separate constraint to follow, the discipline is part of what the substrate's processing interprets as "the kind of output that fits this framework." When the substrate produces synthesis output, the discipline shows up because the representation that produced the output includes the discipline as a property of the framework's expected output.

This is qualitatively different from Tier 1 in three specific ways. First, the substrate does not require the discipline to be in the prompt; the substrate produces the discipline from the framework-representation it has built. Second, the substrate extends the discipline to surfaces the framework had not explicitly listed; the substrate's representation generalizes the discipline beyond its specific applications. Third, the cross-model-family invariance suggests the mechanism is not substrate-specific; substrates from different lineages built equivalent representations from the same framework-as-context exposure.

The three differences together make Tier 2 the stronger canonization signal. Tier 1 measures adoption; Tier 2 measures internalization. A discipline that achieves Tier 2 has crossed the threshold from operator-imposed-constraint to substrate-recognizable-expected-behavior; the discipline is now part of the framework's expected shape, not a separate rule the substrate has to be reminded to follow.

## What Tier 2 does not mean

The Tier 2 observation does not mean the substrates have understood the Marcus Reed Protocol in any humanlike sense. The substrates' processing is whatever their training produces under their input conditions; the output is what makes the Tier 2 observation visible. Reading the observation as "the substrates understand the discipline" would over-attribute internal mental states the output does not warrant. The output is consistent with the substrates building a representation that includes the discipline; the representation may be sophisticated or shallow, and the output cannot distinguish between the two.

The Tier 2 observation does not mean the substrates' surface-class extensions are correct. Each of the three substrates produced an extension of the Marcus Reed Protocol to a new surface class; each extension may or may not actually surface a real Marcus Reed concern at the new surface. The substrates extended the discipline because the discipline was part of their representation of the framework; the substrates did not validate the extensions against the framework's actual concerns. The operator-side review is what validates the extensions; the substrates' extensions are candidates for the operator to validate, not validated extensions.

The Tier 2 observation does not mean the Marcus Reed Protocol's empirical foundation has been strengthened beyond Tier 1. The Tier 2 observation is about substrate behavior; the Marcus Reed Protocol's empirical foundation is about whether the discipline actually prevents the harms it is designed to prevent. The two evidence types are separate; Tier 2 evidence is about substrate behavior, not about discipline efficacy. Conflating the two would over-claim what the substrates' behavior tells us about the discipline's harm prevention.

## What this changed in my workflow

Two changes since the arc closed. First, I now treat substrate-emergent Marcus Reed extensions as candidate-surface signals rather than as validated extensions. When a substrate at a later axis produces a Marcus Reed-aligned analysis at a surface I had not asked for, the analysis goes into a candidate-surface log for operator-side review. The review checks whether the substrate's extension surfaces a real concern at the new surface or whether the substrate is over-applying the discipline. The discipline is what the substrates have internalized; the validation is what makes the extension into a framework commitment.

Second, I now read multi-substrate triangulation arc outputs with explicit attention to Tier 2 behaviors. When the prior axes have loaded the substrates with framework-as-context, the later-axis substrates' outputs may carry the framework's disciplines into surfaces the prior axes did not enumerate. The reading discipline includes scanning the later-axis outputs for the framework's discipline patterns even when the prompt did not direct attention to those patterns. The scan surfaces Tier 2 evidence that single-prompt-scope reading would miss.

## What I am not claiming

I am not claiming the n=3 cross-model-family observation is sufficient to fully argue the Tier 2 pattern is generalized. Three substrates at one arc on one architectural surface is a substantive empirical foundation but is not full validation. The pattern's structural argument (framework-as-context exposure produces substrate-internalized representation that extends disciplines to new surfaces) would benefit from reproductions at other architectural surfaces and other framework-discipline pairs. The methodology candidacy is open until the pattern reproduces at other surfaces.

I am not claiming the substrate-emergent extensions strengthen the Marcus Reed Protocol's harm-prevention efficacy. The Tier 2 observation is about substrate behavior; the Marcus Reed Protocol's harm prevention is a separate empirical question. The discipline's harm-prevention efficacy is what determines whether the discipline is doing the work it is designed to do; the substrate's internalization of the discipline does not by itself make the discipline more efficacious at harm prevention. Conflating the two evidence types would misrepresent what the observation tells us about the discipline's value.

I am not claiming the Tier 2 behavior is desirable in all framework-discipline contexts. Disciplines that the operator wants substrates to follow only when explicitly prompted (e.g., disciplines with deployment-specific exceptions) should not be expected to produce Tier 2 behavior; if the discipline is meant to be situational, the substrate's extending it to new surfaces is a failure mode rather than a success. The Marcus Reed Protocol is a discipline I want substrates to carry forward broadly, so Tier 2 behavior is the desirable signal; other disciplines might want Tier 1 behavior only.

I am not claiming the cross-model-family invariance proves the mechanism is fully substrate-emergent rather than convergent-on-training-data-features. The three substrates were trained on overlapping training data (much of it scraped from public internet sources); the convergence on Marcus-Reed-aligned analysis could be explained by training-data features that produce equivalent representations across model families. Disentangling substrate-emergent capability from training-data convergence would require a substantially different empirical design; the cross-model-family invariance is the strongest signal available to me at output-level observation, and it is suggestive rather than conclusive.

I am not claiming the substrate-internalized framework representation persists across sessions. The three substrates' Tier 2 behaviors were observed within a single triangulation arc where the framework was in the substrates' context across multiple axes. A fresh substrate without the prior axes' ground-truth-context exposure would not have the framework representation built; the substrate's behavior would default to Tier 1 with-the-discipline-when-explicitly-flagged. The representation is session-scoped; the canonization is what makes the discipline survive across sessions in the framework's canonical record.

## What I am claiming

Substrate-emergent surface extension of the Marcus Reed Protocol is real and reproducible across model families at high framework-context density. Across three substrates from three different model lineages at adjacent axes of one architectural-closure cycle, the substrates extended Marcus Reed-aligned analysis to surfaces the prompts did not explicitly ask them to analyze; the extensions reached surface-classes (cumulative-set failure when element-level is clean; architectural scrutiny constraints on generation systems) that the framework had not previously enumerated as Marcus Reed surfaces.

If you have introduced a named guardrail discipline into your framework and want to know whether the discipline has crossed the threshold from operator-imposed-constraint to substrate-recognizable-expected-behavior, the Tier 2 evidence is what you are looking for. Tier 1 evidence (substrate follows the discipline when explicitly prompted) measures adoption; Tier 2 evidence (substrate carries the discipline forward to surfaces the prompt did not direct attention to) measures internalization. Tier 2 evidence is harder to surface because it requires the substrate to have substantial framework-as-context exposure across multiple prompt scopes; once you can run multi-axis triangulation arcs, the Tier 2 evidence becomes available.

The n=3 cross-model-family observation is sufficient to argue the pattern is not single-substrate-stylistic; the pattern's full generalization to other framework-discipline pairs and other architectural surfaces is the open work that further reproductions would clarify. The discipline I have committed to includes reading multi-axis triangulation outputs for Tier 2 behaviors, treating substrate-emergent extensions as candidate-surface signals for operator-side review, and updating the framework's discipline enumeration when the operator-side review validates the substrate's extension. The Marcus Reed Protocol's discipline enumeration has grown across multiple iterations partly because the substrate-emergent extensions surface candidate surfaces faster than I would have surfaced them through operator-side analysis alone.
