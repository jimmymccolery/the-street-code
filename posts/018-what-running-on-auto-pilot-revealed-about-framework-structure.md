Today I deliberately ran my AI infrastructure session at reduced verification-reviewer intensity. Closer to auto-pilot than typical-state. The goal was to observe what the framework's other mechanisms would do when I wasn't paying full attention.

What showed up wasn't framework collapse. It was framework operating differently. Substrate-architectural mechanisms continued working independently of my attention; my own catch operated as backstop even at reduced intensity, sometimes later in cascade rather than at point of detection but still within session scope.

The session also produced something I didn't anticipate going in: empirical evidence that operator-side role architecture is structurally embedded in framework discipline rather than contingent on full-attention operator state. Six functional roles fired across the session even with verification-reviewer intensity reduced. Roles aren't dependent on me running at peak attention; they are part of the framework's fabric.

This post extends [Post 17's recovery-from-drift framing](017-recovery-from-drift-as-framework-discipline.md) with empirical evidence from today's experimental session. It covers what the experimental design was, what the framework's substrate-architectural mechanisms did, what the operator-side role architecture did, what the recursive operational evidence revealed, what cross-session experimental design might look like, and what I'm not claiming and what I am claiming.

## What the experimental design was

Today's session ran at deliberately reduced verification-reviewer intensity. I confirmed this post-session; the AI substrate didn't have experimental-condition awareness during execution, and its default assumption was typical operator-state. The reframe came when I surfaced the experimental design after the session's substantive canonical-record output had already produced two findings about operator-side roles and recovery-from-drift mechanisms. The reframe substantively strengthened both findings, because they aren't confounded by typical-state operator attention.

This is one experimental condition (reduced intensity) at one operator-state level on one session. It's not cross-session validation. But it isolates a variable that typical-state observation conflates: substrate-architectural-robustness vs operator-attention-load-bearing-roles.

## What the framework's substrate-architectural mechanisms did

[Post 17](017-recovery-from-drift-as-framework-discipline.md) named four recovery-from-drift mechanisms. Today's session produced concrete evidence for each, with one novel sub-finding about their internal structure.

Mechanism #1 (verify-canonical-first applied to mutation operations) operated across roughly sixteen Phase B mutations today. It caught drift between substrate's stated intent and canonical-state reality, before any mutation deployed. Mechanism #2 (Type 5 verify-then-deploy workflow Phase B baseline checks) ran roughly twelve times with clean execution, catching spec drift at multiple sub-steps. Mechanism #3 (reflection-prompt-discipline) produced canonical-record output (research-queue topic filings; [Post 13](013-reflection-as-methodology-research-discipline.md); Post 17) that operational-mode session work would have missed.

Mechanism #4 (operator-side catch as backstop) caught remaining drift even at reduced intensity, with roughly four of five substrate-side false-flag fires operator-surfaced. The catches sometimes landed later in cascade than at point of detection, but stayed within session scope.

The novel sub-finding: mechanisms #1 through #3 are substrate-architectural; they operate independently of operator attention. Mechanism #4 is operator-side; it operates even at reduced intensity but with graceful degradation rather than full-attention catch. The 4-mechanism composition has internal structure where three mechanisms are operator-attention-independent and one is operator-attention-dependent-but-graceful-degradation. That isn't visible at typical-state intensity, where all four mechanisms appear undifferentiated.

The methodologically substantive observation: roles fired even at reduced verification-reviewer intensity. They aren't contingent on the operator running at peak attention. They are structurally embedded in operator-side discipline.

## What cross-session experimental design might look like

Today's deliberate reduced-intensity experiment is one session at one condition. Cross-session experimental design could vary operator-state systematically: a typical-state baseline session; a reduced-intensity session (today's condition); a session with verification-reviewer deliberately late in cascade to observe mechanism #4 graceful-degradation envelope explicitly; an amplified-intensity session to observe upper-bound calibration.

Each experimental condition produces canonically distinct empirical evidence. Cross-session evidence accumulation across experimental conditions is methodologically richer than cross-session accumulation across typical-state observations only. That four-condition framework is concrete forward-research methodology today's observation surfaces.

## What I'm not claiming

I'm not claiming the framework eliminates drift. Drift fires happen. The framework catches them, sometimes later in cascade than at point of detection, especially under reduced operator-side intensity.

I'm not claiming reduced-intensity operation is sustainable indefinitely. Today was one session. Attention costs accumulate over time, and graceful-degradation envelopes have edges that single-session observation doesn't reveal.

I'm not claiming this generalizes beyond solo-operator AI-assisted methodology research at this empirical stage. Cross-session evidence is required for canonical promotion. Single-session experimental observation surfaces hypotheses; cross-session validation tests them.

I'm not claiming experimental-condition framing alone validates the findings. The reframe makes the empirical evidence methodologically stronger than typical-state observation, but doesn't substitute for cross-session validation across the four-condition framework above.

I'm not claiming the operator-side role architecture is canonized. It remains at research-queue scope; the canonization-scope asymmetry is the foundational gap, not the resolution.

## What I am claiming

I'm claiming today's empirical evidence is methodologically stronger than typical-state observation because the experimental design isolated operator-attention as a variable. Substrate-architectural mechanisms operating independently of operator attention is the novel sub-finding worth surfacing.

I'm claiming operator-side role architecture is structurally embedded in framework discipline. Today's six-role multi-fire-within-session evidence is empirical evidence stronger than typical-state observation; the roles are part of the framework's fabric.

I'm claiming the 4-mechanism composition has internal structure. Mechanisms #1 through #3 are substrate-architectural; mechanism #4 is operator-side with graceful degradation. That refines [Post 17's](017-recovery-from-drift-as-framework-discipline.md) recovery-from-drift threshold framing with empirical evidence about the mechanisms' internal differentiation.

I'm claiming recursive operational evidence (framework catching its own drift while documenting drift-catching) fired twice within today's session arc. That is structural pattern, not coincidence.

I'm claiming the cross-session experimental design pattern (four-condition session-state framework: typical, reduced, late, amplified) is concrete forward-research methodology today's observation produces.

I'm claiming the canonization-scope asymmetry (AI-side role architecture canonical; operator-side at research-queue scope only) is the foundational gap the experiment exposed. Resolving it would close the framework's canonization-scope asymmetry between AI-side and operator-side discipline.

Today's experiment was one session at one condition. The methodological observation is that running closer to auto-pilot revealed the framework's structural fabric rather than its dependencies. Cross-session evidence accumulation across the four-condition framework would test whether that observation generalizes.
