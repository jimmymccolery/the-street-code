I am a solo indie developer documenting the methodology of a procedural-character architecture for a multi-decade tycoon simulation. The Council methodology (documented in post 0129 in this arc for its first empirical fire at Council #1) is the framework's operational discipline for architectural decisions with non-trivial downside if wrong. Council #3 was the framework's second Rule 14 Madisonian Gate deployment. The question: is the framework's audit-first sequencing for sophisticated active inference (post 0119 in this arc) operationally tractable at the framework's target scale of 80 to 100 NPCs given realistic branch factors, or does the policy-evaluation complexity force demotion of sophisticated active inference from V1-critical machinery to a non-runtime audit metaphor?

This post documents how Council #3 closed. Three substrates verdict-token converged with one nominal exception, the framework's orchestrator layer independently re-derived Rule 14 Pattern B ADOPT-FOLD from the substrate primary texts via the same discriminator Council #1 canonized, and the record's partial adjudication of the per-frame regime plus the surfacing of a branch-factor discipline gap produced three riders that convert the composite verdict into a scheduled decision procedure. Post 0132 in this arc specs the actual architectural output: the Swift on-device benchmark harness at the meta-architecture level plus the Post 0119 operational-contract amendment.

Council #3 reserved distinct roles for three substrates so their contributions would not collapse into overlapping evidence. Substrate A ran on Perplexity Deep Research and covered the active-inference theory plus complexity literature question. Substrate B ran on Claude Web Opus extended thinking and covered the benchmark harness specification question. Substrate B was instructed to specify a benchmark harness plus expected runtime envelope. Substrate C was instructed to evaluate the operator's Path A budget envelope (270-410 hours iOS V1 per post 0119 in this arc) against the operator's actual published component-cost estimates.

## The substrate-C resolves-into rather than diverges-from finding

The orchestrator's sharpest reading was on Substrate C's V1-CRITICAL verdict. Substrate C's Dependency 1 clause states outright: "Substrate A's verdict is the load-bearing verdict; Substrate C's fit-within-budget verdict is downstream of Substrate A's fit-within-tractability verdict." Substrate C's DEMOTE trigger keyed on Substrate A finding intractability at Tier 2 consolidation-pass cadence and on Substrate B benchmark surfacing pass-cost beyond player-facing tolerance.

Substrate A's actual verdict was RECOMMEND-FURTHER-BENCHMARK, not V1-CRITICAL. Substrate A did not issue the tractability finding Substrate C's V1-CRITICAL is contingent on. Therefore Substrate C's V1-CRITICAL is currently unsatisfied on Substrate C's own terms. The verdict-token surface reads 2-vs-1 divergence; the modal-status underneath reads composite RECOMMEND-FURTHER-BENCHMARK because Substrate C's conditional cannot fire until Substrate A's antecedent resolves.

This is a distinct case from Council #1's Pattern A-versus-Pattern-B discriminator (documented in post 0129 in this arc). Council #1's discriminator was that 2 of 3 substrates preserved live reversal branches gated on future evidence, arguing Pattern B over Pattern A. Council #3's Pattern B holds via a related but different route: all three substrates preserve live DEMOTE reversal branches gated on the same benchmark measurement, which makes the benchmark the unique convergence point of the record. Both routes land at ADOPT-FOLD per Rule 10 Conservative-Bias default lean.

## Which reasoning chain adjudicated load-bearing

The orchestrator's Section 2.3 adjudication identified Substrate B's chain as load-bearing for the tractability question, with Substrate A as its theoretical warrant. Three grounded reasons: Substrate B operates at the exact evidence layer the Council question interrogates (wall-clock milliseconds against a 33 millisecond frame on Apple silicon); Substrate B's chain already adjudicates part of the question (the per-frame regime R1 infeasibility is settled by arithmetic that Substrate A's literature grounding independently licenses); Substrate C's chain, by its own declaration, is not load-bearing for tractability but is load-bearing for affordability.

The primary-text-grounding-over-cross-substrate-convergence discipline the framework adopts (Rule 14) argues Substrate B's chain is the load-bearing chain even though it is a 2-substrate token-convergence chain. Substrate A's chain supplies the theoretical warrant that makes Substrate B's arithmetic trustworthy. Substrate C's chain governs affordability, not tractability, by Substrate C's own scoping.

The subtle Substrate B caveat the orchestrator surfaced: Substrate B could not read posts 0119, 0120, 0123 from the repository (Substrate B honestly disclosed the ROBOTS_DISALLOWED plus PERMISSIONS_ERROR access failures and explicitly declined to fabricate post content). The cross-substrate patch holds but the operator should confirm it during ratification.

## Rider 1: the per-frame regime is adjudicated INFEASIBLE now

The orchestrator's Rider 1 is the sharpest partial adjudication banked from the Council record. Substrate B's arithmetic at 80 to 100 NPCs with candidate-action count A=3, horizon τ=3, and per-decision cost median 1.6 milliseconds produces per-frame total of 160 milliseconds. That is 5 times a whole 33 millisecond frame and 16 times a 10 millisecond AI slice at the cheapest cell in the sweep; at A=5 or A=8 the per-frame total is hundreds of milliseconds to seconds. Substrate A's literature grounding independently licenses the same conclusion: "a per-frame sophisticated evaluator for 80 to 100 NPCs would have to make hundreds of thousands of action-path plus belief-path operations disappear into a few milliseconds after rendering, input, physics, audio, and game logic are paid, which is not supported by the cited literature."

The per-frame regime is not merely improbable; it is arithmetically ruled out. No future benchmark outcome revives it. If any part of the framework's "audit-first sequencing" language in post 0119 in this arc was conceived as a per-frame call, that part is demoted today. This is a partial adjudication that no benchmark is needed to lock. The Council question's two disjuncts (V1-critical runtime machinery versus non-runtime audit metaphor) are not both open; the runtime-per-frame disjunct is closed.

## Rider 2: the benchmark scope is narrowed to the consolidation-pass regime

The open question after Rider 1 is exclusively whether the batched time-sliced consolidation-pass regime (Substrate B's R3, equivalent to the operator's published Tier 2 architecture per post 0123 in this arc) fits budget at NPC count 100, horizon τ=3, across candidate-action counts A in the set 3-5-8, observation branching b_o in the set 2-3-4, with Occam-window pruning as a toggle. Substrate B's pre-run envelope arithmetic says the deployable-regime answer is plausibly yes for A=3, marginal for A=5, and doubtful for A=8.

The benchmark's job is to collapse this uncertainty via empirical measurement. Substrate B specifies the harness at 3.5 to 4.5 focused solo dev-days (approximately 28 to 36 hours) with Swift on-device implementation, XCTest measure plus OSSignposter instrumentation, optional pymdp correctness oracle, and a fractional sweep of approximately 27 microbenchmark cells plus 9 headline integration cells. Post 0132 in this arc specs the harness at meta-architecture level.

## Rider 3: the branch-factor discipline is a spec gap independent of benchmark outcome

Substrate A's finding on post 0119 in this arc stands on primary text Substrate A actually read: "Post 0119 emphasizes horizon depth τ≤3 as the named computational defense and does not explicitly impose a maximum candidate-action branch factor per decision. That is a gap relative to Champion et al.'s formula, because the exponential term is |U|^T, making |U|, not just T, part of the operational contract."

The spec gap holds regardless of what the benchmark measures. Whatever number the harness produces for per-consolidation-pass wall-clock cost at specific (A, b_o, pruning) settings, the framework's operational contract must name the branching contract the measurement is of. Substrate C's audit inventory corroborates the gap structurally: the framework's four-audit mandatory test surface (per post 0119 in this arc) lists a horizon-bound test at τ≤3 but no B_max audit. The four-audit surface has state-space content audit, amnesia re-initialization test, Markov-blanket isolation test, and horizon-bound test. No test enforces branch-factor discipline.

Post 0132 in this arc specs the operational-contract amendment. The amendment adds a B_max stated per audit-time decision context, a probability-threshold pruning budget per Friston 2021 style (the paper's example threshold is 1/16), and optionally a BTAI/MCTS-style expansion budget K per Champion et al. 2022 with sparse factor-graph plus interruptible reactive message passing per de Vries 2023. This amendment is benchmark-independent adoption; it does not wait for the harness to run.

## The terminology refinement: consolidation-pass cadence, not audit-first sequencing

The Council question's phrase "audit-first sequencing" conflates two claims the substrates cleanly separated. Sequencing (build the audit layer before runtime promotion) is unchallenged; all three substrates endorse it. Cadence (when the sophisticated evaluator actually executes) is the live question, answered "consolidation-pass, never per-frame" across all three substrates.

The operator's Rule 10 Conservative-Bias default lean would adopt the precise term "consolidation-pass cadence" in any Post 0119 operational-contract amendment. Post 0132 in this arc adopts the terminology explicitly. The reframing does not require re-deploying Council #3; the reframed question is already answered by the record. The audit-first sequencing language remains valid for the buildup discipline (audit layer first, runtime promotion earned empirically); the cadence language pins down the specific implementation shape at V1.
